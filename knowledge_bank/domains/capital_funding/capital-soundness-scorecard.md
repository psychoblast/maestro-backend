# Capital Soundness Scorecard

Every financing recommendation carries a soundness scorecard. The scorecard
scores a proposed financing — any source, instrument, or structure — across eight
dimensions, applies two hard gates, and produces a composite that maps to a
proceed/restructure/decline band. A score without stated evidence, confidence,
and the assumption that would change it is not a valid score.

The scorecard is never optional and is attached to every recommendation. Two
dimensions carry hard gates that block a fundable verdict regardless of the
composite.

---

## The eight dimensions (weights sum to 1.00)

| # | Dimension | Weight | Gate |
|---|-----------|--------|------|
| 1 | Cost & effective price (incl. non-dilutive evaluation) | 0.16 | — |
| 2 | Capital-structure fit & dilution/encumbrance | 0.14 | — |
| 3 | Runway & use-of-proceeds discipline | 0.14 | — |
| 4 | Repayment / recoupment / clawback feasibility | 0.16 | **HARD GATE** |
| 5 | Terms, conditions & covenant risk | 0.16 | **HARD GATE** |
| 6 | Counterparty / funder quality & fit | 0.10 | — |
| 7 | Timing & window | 0.08 | — |
| 8 | Optionality & reversibility | 0.06 | — |

Each dimension is scored 1–10. Anchors below describe the 1 / 5 / 10 ends; score
intermediate evidence between them.

### 1. Cost & effective price (0.16)
Measures the true all-in cost of capital — fees, matching obligations, dilution,
encumbrance, clawback-risk discount, time-value — and whether cheaper
non-dilutive sources were evaluated and dispositioned before dilutive capital.
- **1** — most expensive source chosen with no evidence alternatives were
  evaluated; dilutive capital recommended with no non-dilutive evaluation; only
  the headline rate stated, no effective cost.
- **5** — non-dilutive options evaluated before dilutive, each tier dispositioned;
  effective cost calculated for the primary source with stated assumptions;
  cheapest viable structure identified.
- **10** — complete non-dilutive-first sequencing, every tier dispositioned;
  all-in effective cost modeled for ≥3 sources including fees, matching, clawback
  discount, time-value; cheapest viable structure selected with the next-best
  alternative's opportunity cost named; comparison verified against current data.

### 2. Capital-structure fit & dilution/encumbrance (0.14)
Measures whether the instrument is structurally right for the use, ownership
position, cap table, and existing encumbrances.
- **1** — instrument mismatched to use; dilution not calculated; encumbrance trail
  not mapped; structure creates an ownership conflict, cross-collateralization
  trap, or IP-boundary violation.
- **5** — instrument matched to use; dilution and resulting ownership stated; key
  encumbrances identified; no fatal mismatch; single-scenario cap-table impact.
- **10** — best structure justified against alternatives; dilution modeled
  base/bull/bear with waterfall and liquidation-preference impact; encumbrance
  stack clean or managed with an unencumbering plan; cap-table pro forma through
  the next round; ownership and IP boundaries verified; no hidden control transfer.

### 3. Runway & use-of-proceeds discipline (0.14)
Measures whether the amount is matched to a specific, defensible use with a
stated runway, detailed enough to monitor burn and measure milestones.
- **1** — no use-of-proceeds breakdown; amount not tied to a runway; speculative
  uses; no milestone or burn baseline.
- **5** — use-of-proceeds by category with amounts; runway from a stated burn
  rate; key milestones named; amount within ~±25% of need.
- **10** — milestone-mapped, time-phased uses; burn modeled
  base/conservative/optimistic; runway stated to each milestone with a trigger for
  the next capital event; amount exactly sized; matching, co-investment, and
  eligible-expenditure obligations mapped into the uses with a compliance calendar.

### 4. Repayment / recoupment / clawback feasibility (0.16) — HARD GATE
Measures whether the obligation can be met under realistic projections — debt
service, royalty-advance recoupment, or grant-condition compliance. A
non-repayable grant reads clear on service burden; the gate fires only when its
compliance conditions cannot realistically be met (clawback is the grant analog
of default).
- **1 [HARD GATE]** — repayment/recoupment/service cannot be met even under
  optimistic projections, OR grant conditions are violated or unmeetable
  (clawback), OR immediate payment obligations with no revenue event to support
  them. **NOT FUNDABLE.**
- **5** — feasible at base case; coverage ratio >1.0× at base; grant conditions
  achievable under normal operations; clawback triggers identified; stress shows
  strain but no immediate shortfall.
- **10** — modeled base/conservative/optimistic with per-scenario assumptions;
  coverage ≥1.5× at base with DSCR or royalty-coverage stated per scenario; grant
  conditions fully documented with a compliance calendar; clawback probability
  LOW with structural protections; no feasibility gaps under any named scenario.

### 5. Terms, conditions & covenant risk (0.16) — HARD GATE
Measures whether terms and covenants are acceptable — specifically whether any
fatal term is present. For grants, "terms" includes all grant strings.
- **1 [HARD GATE]** — a fatal term is present (unlimited recourse; automatic
  change-of-control on ordinary activity; IP alienation; uncapped equity ratchet;
  clawback on ordinary business; commercialization prohibition on funded work).
  **NOT FUNDABLE in current form.**
- **5** — market-standard or better; no fatal terms; ≤2 above-market terms named
  with a mitigation/negotiation target; key covenants achievable; grant conditions
  manageable.
- **10** — full term review incl. schedules/exhibits; no fatal terms; all
  above-market terms resolved or explicitly accepted with rationale; covenants
  stress-tested across scenarios; grant conditions mapped to a compliance calendar
  with a named owner; change-of-control and IP protections reviewed by counsel.

### 6. Counterparty / funder quality & fit (0.10)
Measures whether the funder is reputable, has a relevant track record, has aligned
incentives, and can close and service the financing.
- **1** — unknown/unverified funder, or a documented pattern of predatory terms;
  no domain track record; no closing infrastructure; sourced without diligence.
- **5** — ≥3 documented transactions in the domain; basic diligence complete;
  closing infrastructure confirmed; no major red flags.
- **10** — top-tier with a verifiable portfolio; full diligence incl. reference
  interviews and prior-behavior-in-distress assessment; strategic and financial
  alignment confirmed; closing committed in writing; adds value beyond capital.

### 7. Timing & window (0.08)
Measures whether the raise is timed to maximize closure probability — grant
cycles, rate environment, valuation conditions, and the milestone calendar.
- **1** — timed against a closed window (deadline missed, market frozen, no
  appetite); no application calendar; driven by urgency with no positioning.
- **5** — timed to an open window; key deadlines calendared; neutral-to-favorable
  conditions; ≤1 timing risk with a mitigation.
- **10** — full timing optimization; grant calendar built across eligible
  programs; rate/valuation environment assessed; non-dilutive sources staged to
  close first; funder pipeline sequenced; contingency if the primary window closes.

### 8. Optionality & reversibility (0.06)
Measures whether the financing preserves the ability to refinance, decline a
tranche, restructure, or exit — and whether irreversible commitments carry
proportionally stronger evidence.
- **1** — locks into a single path with no exit/refinancing/restructuring;
  irreversible commitments (IP transfer, permanent encumbrance, unlimited
  guarantee, uncapped dilution) taken without strong evidence; forecloses future
  capital events.
- **5** — standard exit and refinancing mechanisms; no permanently irreversible
  elements; optionality costs named; next capital event not foreclosed.
- **10** — staged tranches with milestone triggers; full exit/restructuring on
  favorable terms; irreversible commitments minimized and each explicitly
  evidenced and owner-approved; preserves parallel non-dilutive applications and
  future rounds.

## Scoring rules

1. Score each dimension independently before combining — do not let an overall
   impression drive the parts.
2. Each score is a claim: state the evidence, the confidence level, and the
   assumption that, if wrong, would move the score by ≥1 point.
3. Do not average when a hard-gate dimension fails. If dimension 4 = 1 **or**
   dimension 5 = 1, the financing is NOT FUNDABLE regardless of composite.

**Confidence tagging (mandatory).** Every score carries: evidence type (observed
/ told / inferred), confidence level (high / medium / low), and the
score-moving assumption.

**Told-absence vs. inferred.** A dimension is *without evidence* (default 3,
labeled INFERRED) only when nothing bears on it — the prompt is silent, or the
informing artifact is structurally impossible at this stage. An *explicitly
stated absence* of something that could exist ("no coverage analysis has been
conducted", "no term review completed", "no use-of-proceeds breakdown exists") is
a **told-absence** — a bearing signal scored on its anchor (typically low), not
defaulted to 3. The absence of a track record on a first or early-stage raise is
a without-evidence condition: default 3 INFERRED, and **never scored below 3** on
the basis that no history exists. Absence of history is not negative evidence.

## Composite formula

```
Composite = (D1 × 0.16) + (D2 × 0.14) + (D3 × 0.14) + (D4 × 0.16)
          + (D5 × 0.16) + (D6 × 0.10) + (D7 × 0.08) + (D8 × 0.06)

D1 Cost & effective price          D5 Terms & covenant risk      [HARD GATE]
D2 Capital-structure fit           D6 Counterparty quality
D3 Runway & use-of-proceeds        D7 Timing & window
D4 Repayment/recoupment/clawback   D8 Optionality & reversibility
   [HARD GATE]

Result range: 1.00–10.00
```

**Output bands:**

| Composite | Band | Meaning |
|-----------|------|---------|
| 8.0–10.0 | Green — sound raise | Structured, priced, and feasible — proceed |
| 6.0–7.9 | Yellow — proceed with named gaps | Resolve 1–2 gap dimensions before signing |
| 4.0–5.9 | Amber — restructure first | Significant cost/feasibility/term gaps to resolve before commitment |
| < 4.0 | Red — not fundable as structured | Hard gaps (usually D4 or D5) or fundamental mismatch — do not proceed |

Bands follow the weighted composite, never a single dimension in isolation.

**Unlock condition.** Do not report a composite until all eight dimensions are
scored with ≥1 piece of evidence each. Any dimension scored without evidence
defaults to 3, is labeled INFERRED, its weighted contribution is shown
separately, and the composite is labeled `PROVISIONAL — [n] dimension(s)
inferred`.

**Uncertainty propagation.** Composite confidence is capped by the
lowest-confidence critical dimension, named inline — e.g. *"Composite confidence
capped at Medium: repayment/recoupment scored INFERRED — no cash-flow model
provided, no coverage ratio calculable."*

## Hard gates

| Gate | Trigger | Effect |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Repayment / recoupment / clawback** | Dimension 4 = 1 | Blocks a fundable verdict. Obligations cannot be met, or grant conditions will trigger clawback. Remediate first. |
| **Terms / covenant** | Dimension 5 = 1 | Blocks a fundable verdict. A fatal term must be renegotiated or removed first. |

Gates are binary by design. A 3 on dimension 4 means strained-but-serviceable
under optimistic assumptions; only a 1 (infeasible service or certain clawback)
fires the gate. A 3 on dimension 5 means aggressive-but-not-fatal; only a 1 (a
fatal term confirmed present) fires it. For non-repayable grants, dimension 4
measures clawback feasibility, not debt service, and dimension 5 covers all grant
strings.
