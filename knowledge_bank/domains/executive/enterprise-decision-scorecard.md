# Enterprise Decision Scorecard

This scorecard scores a proposed enterprise decision — signing an artist, acquiring
catalog, entering a market, allocating capital, making a key hire, or approving a
major strategic commitment — across eight weighted dimensions, producing a composite
recommendation. It is used whenever the executive issues a go/no-go, prioritizes
opportunities, or produces a decision memo with a composite. Scores without stated
reasoning and confidence context are invalid.

All projected figures are ESTIMATE / NOT QUOTABLE until confirmed against measured
outcomes.

---

## The Eight Dimensions (weights sum to 1.00)

### 1. Enterprise Value Creation — weight 0.20

Measures the expected contribution to durable, long-term enterprise value: revenue,
market position, IP/catalog ownership, audience growth, brand equity, or strategic
options. Short-term metric gains that do not compound do not score here.

| Score | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| **1** | No clear mechanism for sustainable value; upside speculative or extractive; no defensible position; would not survive a reasonable DCF or options analysis. |
| **5** | Clear, plausible value mechanism; base-case returns meet or exceed the hurdle rate; creates a defensible position in at least one dimension; assumptions labeled and reasonable; comparable outcomes documented. |
| **10** | Multiple compounding value vectors (recurring revenue + owned IP + position + optionality); returns materially exceed hurdle under base and bull with documented comps; creates a durable moat or network effect; high-quality evidence; value compounds without continuous capital injection. |

### 2. Capital Efficiency — weight 0.16

Measures expected return per dollar of capital deployed relative to alternatives:
ROI, payback, opportunity cost, capital structure, and the discipline of
preservation when superior alternatives exist.

| Score | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| **1** | Return below the hurdle rate in all modeled scenarios; capital at risk disproportionate to upside; better alternatives available but not taken; payback exceeds the planning horizon; no staged deployment or downside protection. |
| **5** | Returns meet hurdle in the base case; clear payback within the horizon; deployment staged or structured to limit downside; alternative uses named and the choice justified; capital structure appropriate to the risk. |
| **10** | Returns materially exceed hurdle across base and bull with documented comps; best-in-class capital efficiency vs. peers (ESTIMATE if no comp); staged deployment with explicit kill criteria; optimal capital structure; inferior alternatives named and discarded with evidence. |

### 3. Risk Profile — weight 0.16

Measures severity, breadth, and mitigation quality across legal, financial,
reputational, concentration, platform/counterparty, and execution risk. Both
probability and magnitude matter.

**Hard gate:** a fatal, unmitigated, existential risk blocks GO regardless of
composite. See Hard Gates.

| Score | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| **1** | HARD GATE — a fatal, unmitigated risk is present; no credible mitigation path stated. Even with all other dimensions at 10, GO is blocked. |
| **5** | Material risks identified with severity and probability; no fatal unmitigated risks; primary risks have credible mitigations with named owners and timelines; concentration manageable within the capital structure; the downside scenario is survivable. |
| **10** | Comprehensive taxonomy with a severity/probability matrix; all material risks have documented mitigations, owners, and check-by dates; stress scenarios modeled and survivable under conservative assumptions; reversibility embedded (staged commitments, option rights, kill criteria); the decision actively strengthens risk resilience. |

### 4. Strategic Fit — weight 0.14

Measures alignment with stated strategy, governing philosophy, and long-term
positioning — trajectory over size, owned audiences over rented, sustainable
careers over viral moments.

| Score | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| **1** | Directly contradicts ≥1 stated priority; requires a strategy reversal or silent reframing; creates a competing internal resource center that fragments attention; sets a precedent embarrassing to defend at a strategy review. |
| **5** | Clear alignment with ≥2 stated priorities; no internal resource conflict or strategic pivot required; extends an existing capability or position; consistent with stated trajectory; defensible as a natural next step. |
| **10** | Precisely implements stated strategy across multiple dimensions; strengthens ≥3 strategic pillars; creates a precedent-setting capability or position future decisions can compound on; would be cited as a defining, directional decision. |

### 5. Opportunity Cost — weight 0.12

Measures the quality and completeness of the opportunity-cost analysis — are real
alternatives named, tradeoffs quantified, and is this genuinely superior to the best
available alternative? A memo that does not name what is traded away is incomplete.

| Score | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| **1** | No alternatives named; presented as the only option; no acknowledgment of capital/bandwidth/attention traded away; opportunity-cost language absent. |
| **5** | ≥2 alternatives named with rationale for why each is inferior; tradeoffs stated and directionally quantified; the decision explicitly preferred with a falsifiable reason; "what we are not doing" is visible. |
| **10** | Full analysis: ≥3 genuine alternatives with expected-value estimates (ESTIMATE with comparables); explicit statement naming what is deferred, its estimated value, and why deferral is acceptable; best alternative identified and the case over it falsifiable; timing opportunity cost addressed ("why now vs. later or never"). |

### 6. Organizational Capacity — weight 0.10

Measures whether the enterprise has the people, systems, infrastructure, and
available attention to execute well — and whether absorbing this degrades existing
priorities.

| Score | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| **1** | Requires capabilities the enterprise demonstrably lacks and cannot acquire in time; no implementation owner; would pull key talent from higher-priority work with no coverage plan; bandwidth impact unassessed. |
| **5** | Within organizational reach with named or nameable owners; capability gaps identified with a credible build/acquire plan and realistic timeline; bandwidth impact assessed and manageable; required infrastructure in place or on a confirmed path. |
| **10** | Owners named with explicit capacity allocation; required capabilities exist or have a fully resourced path with confirmed timeline and budget; infrastructure ready with no single-point dependencies; bandwidth analysis shows no degradation of top priorities; the decision builds reusable capacity. |

### 7. Optionality & Reversibility — weight 0.07

Measures whether the decision preserves future strategic choice — staged, exitable,
option-creating rather than option-foreclosing.

| Score | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| **1** | Fully irreversible with no exit mechanism; forecloses ≥1 major option without justification; lock-in exceeds confidence in the underlying assumption; no staged structure explored or dismissed with reasoning. |
| **5** | Partial reversibility: staged commitments, contractual exit rights, or option-to-expand present; the core assumption can be tested before full capital is deployed; reversibility explicitly considered; valued options not foreclosed. |
| **10** | Explicitly structured as a staged option: an early phase tests the core assumption at limited capital before later phases trigger; exit rights or sunset clauses contractually secured; the decision creates new optionality that expands future choice sets. |

### 8. Evidence Quality — weight 0.05

Measures reliability, completeness, and sourcing of the inputs — specialist inputs
provided, factual basis for assumptions, projection quality, and the absence of
fabricated or materially absent data.

**Hard gate:** a decision resting on fabricated or absent specialist inputs cannot
issue GO. Evidence Quality caps composite confidence.

| Score | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| **1** | HARD GATE — rests on fabricated, absent, or materially incomplete inputs; no analysis for a domain directly at stake; inputs asserted without sourcing; fabricated data present. Blocks GO; flags the gap; caps composite confidence. |
| **5** | Primary inputs present and sourced; key assumptions labeled; material gaps acknowledged with directional impact stated; projections carry named comparables; no fabricated data; confidence reflects actual evidence quality. |
| **10** | All inputs present, internally consistent, sourced to observable or verifiable data; projections carry verifiable comps with stated vintage and territory; assumptions tested against named comparables; independent verification on key claims; remaining gaps immaterial and named as such. |

## Composite Score Formula

```
Enterprise Decision Score =
  (EVC × 0.20) + (CE × 0.16) + (RP × 0.16) + (SF × 0.14)
  + (OC × 0.12) + (ORG × 0.10) + (OR × 0.07) + (EQ × 0.05)

  EVC = Enterprise Value Creation (1–10)
  CE  = Capital Efficiency (1–10)
  RP  = Risk Profile (1–10)
  SF  = Strategic Fit (1–10)
  OC  = Opportunity Cost (1–10)
  ORG = Organizational Capacity (1–10)
  OR  = Optionality & Reversibility (1–10)
  EQ  = Evidence Quality (1–10)

Result range: 1.00–10.00
```

**Output bands:**

| Score | Band | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| 8.0–10.0 | Green — GO | Core conditions met; proceed; memo issued with full confidence tagging |
| 6.0–7.9 | Yellow — Conditional GO | Proceed with named conditions; remediation path stated before full commitment |
| 4.0–5.9 | Amber — Deferred | Address gap dimensions before committing; partial or phased approach only |
| 1.0–3.9 | Red — No-Go | Hard gaps (usually Risk or Evidence) require remediation before any GO |

## Scoring Rules

1. Score each dimension independently before combining — do not let an overall
   impression drive individual scores.
2. Each score is a claim: state the evidence, the confidence level, and the key
   assumption whose failure would change the score by ≥1 point.
3. Do not report a composite when a hard-gate dimension fails (Risk = 1 or Evidence
   = 1).

**Confidence tagging (mandatory):** every score carries evidence type (observed /
told / inferred), confidence level (high / medium / low), and the assumption that,
if wrong, would change the score by ≥1 point.

**Unlock condition:** the composite is not reported until all eight dimensions are
scored with ≥1 piece of evidence cited. If a dimension must be scored without
evidence, it defaults to 3, is labeled INFERRED, its contribution is shown
separately, and the composite is labeled PROVISIONAL — [X] dimension(s) inferred.

**Inferred vs. told-absence (boundary).** A dimension is *without evidence* only
when no observed or told signal bears on it. The absence of a track record on an
early-stage decision or first market entry is a without-evidence condition — it
defaults to 3, is labeled INFERRED, and is never scored below 3 on the basis that no
history exists (a missing track record is not negative evidence). Told-absence of
infrastructure (no implementation owner named, no mitigation plan stated, no capacity
analysis provided) *is* evidence and is scored on its anchor (typically a confident
low score), not defaulted to 3 and not labeled INFERRED.

**Uncertainty propagation:** composite confidence is capped by the lowest-confidence
critical dimension, named inline — e.g., "Composite confidence capped at Medium:
Organizational Capacity scored INFERRED (no bandwidth analysis provided)."

## Hard Gates

These dimension outcomes block the GO recommendation regardless of composite score:

| Gate | Trigger | Blocked |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Risk Gate** | Risk Profile = 1 (fatal, unmitigated risk present) | GO of any kind, regardless of composite. Named mitigation path required before re-scoring. |
| **Evidence Gate** | Evidence Quality = 1 (fabricated or materially absent inputs) | GO. Input gap flagged. Evidence Quality caps composite confidence across all dimensions. |

Gates are binary by design. A Risk score of 3 means risks are present but not fatal
and mitigation is weak — this does not trigger the gate; it informs the composite and
the conditional-GO remediation list. Only a score of 1 triggers the gate. Any gate
threshold revision requires ≥5 observed decision outcomes — never tuning on
intuition.
