# Output Templates & Handoffs

This file defines the executive layer's deliverable shapes, the rules that apply to
all of them, the boundaries where work hands off to other domains, and the hard
refusals that block invalid output.

All forward-looking figures are ESTIMATE / NOT QUOTABLE until confirmed against
measured outcomes.

---

## Usage Rules (apply to every template)

- **Inputs only.** Templates operate on specialist inputs already provided. No
  assumption that a sibling function is reachable, no fabrication of an absent input.
- **Descriptive labels.** No product names, entity names, or roster names appear in
  a template field — use `[ARTIST / ACT]`, `[TERRITORY]`, `[DEAL]`, `[CATALOG]`.
- **Estimates labeled.** Every forward figure, projection, or benchmark is marked
  ESTIMATE / NOT QUOTABLE until confirmed against measured outcomes.
- **Confidence tags mandatory.** Every scored dimension and confidence statement
  carries evidence type (observed / told / inferred), confidence level (high /
  medium / low), and the key assumption whose failure would change it by ≥1 point.
- **Capital recommendations** always attach a stated hurdle rate, assumptions, and
  measurement method — a capital recommendation without stated assumptions is
  invalid output.
- **Opportunity cost** is always named explicitly — every GO is a no to something
  else.

## The Deliverable Catalogue

| # | Deliverable | When |
|---|-------------|------|
| 1 | Executive Decision Memo (PRIMARY) | Any major go/no-go, capital commitment, or strategic commitment |
| 2 | Strategic Planning Report | Annual / quarterly |
| 3 | Capital Allocation Plan | Capital deployment across competing uses |
| 4 | Executive Board Report | Reporting state to the governing body |
| 5 | Business Expansion Assessment | Market / genre / business-line entry |
| 6 | Acquisition Analysis | Catalog or company acquisition |
| 7 | Organizational Risk Assessment | Enterprise risk register and mitigation priorities |
| 8 | Executive Priority Report | Top priorities for the next quarter |
| 9 | Crisis Management Brief | Live emergency response |
| 10 | Executive Dashboard | Periodic org-health snapshot |

The **Executive Decision Memo** is the canonical centerpiece; the other deliverables
either embed its score and gates (decision-type outputs 3, 5, 6) or report state
(2, 4, 7, 8, 9, 10).

---

## 1. Executive Decision Memo (PRIMARY)

The eleven sections, each guarding a named failure mode (see Decision Synthesis &
Judgment for the full guard index): **Decision → Evidence → Specialist Input →
Areas of Agreement → Areas of Disagreement → Assumptions → Risks → Alternatives
Considered → Opportunity Cost → Confidence Level → Next Actions.**

**Header fields:** subject (one-line decision); decision ID (for the journal); date;
decision owner (role, not name); forcing function (why this must be decided now — a
decision without one may be premature); review date (when checked against observable
outcomes); falsifiability condition (what observable outcome, by when, proves it
right or wrong).

**Embedded Enterprise Decision Score:** the eight-dimension summary table with
weight, score, band, evidence type, confidence, and key assumption per dimension;
the composite value and band; the Risk Gate and Evidence Gate status (CLEAR /
TRIGGERED); composite confidence and any cap; and a PROVISIONAL label if any
dimension is inferred. Score each dimension independently before combining. A
dimension without evidence defaults to 3 and is labeled INFERRED; told-absence of a
required input is scored on its anchor, not defaulted.

The first next action on every memo is to log the decision and its falsifiability
condition in the decision journal.

---

## 2. Strategic Planning Report

Annual or quarterly; not a single-decision output. Parts: current-state assessment
(performance vs. prior plan, using measured actuals and clearly labeling
projections); scenario landscape (base / bull / bear with leading indicators,
decision triggers, and a bear-case survivability check); strategic priorities
(ranked, with capital claim, what falls if each is funded, and review milestones,
plus an explicit deprioritization list); decision gates (decisions required before
the next cycle); risk-register update; and a prediction-log section of forecastable
claims with falsifiability conditions and check-by dates.

---

## 3. Capital Allocation Plan

A decision-type output. The enterprise hurdle rate must appear — omission is invalid
output. An allocation summary table (initiative, capital, % of envelope, hurdle,
expected return, payback, priority rank, plus an explicit reserve line) is followed
by per-initiative detail: hurdle specific to the initiative's risk profile; expected
return with its load-bearing assumption named; measurement method; capital
structure; staged-deployment triggers or the reason for full upfront deployment;
kill criteria; and a single named owner. A portfolio-level view checks concentration
(flag any single use above a stated share), liquidity-reserve adequacy under the
bear case, and capital held in reserve. An alternatives-and-opportunity-cost section
names at least one genuine alternative allocation and what cannot be funded because
of this one.

---

## 4. Executive Board Report

A communication output reporting state to the governing body; decision-type fields
apply only to specific decisions the board is asked to ratify. Parts: headline
performance vs. plan (unaudited figures labeled ESTIMATE; underperformance stated
directly, never buried); strategic-initiative status with at-risk flags; a
board-view risk register (top risks, owners, and whether board action is required);
decisions required of the board (with supporting memo references and consequence of
delay); and a forward look for the next period.

---

## 5. Business Expansion Assessment

A decision-type output for market / genre / business-line entry; the score must be
completed and a GO without organizational-capacity modeling is invalid. Parts: market
opportunity (size and dynamics named specifically, entry-mode options with the
selected mode justified, and the timing window with its half-life stated); financial
case (capital, hurdle, expected return, payback, IRR, model basis, load-bearing
assumption, and a bear-case survivability check); organizational-capacity assessment
(named implementation owner, capability gaps with build/acquire plans, bandwidth
impact on existing priorities, infrastructure status, key-person risk); risk profile;
the embedded decision score; and a recommendation with conditions, falsifiability,
and both the opportunity cost of GO and the opportunity cost of NO GO.

---

## 6. Acquisition Analysis

A decision-type output; acquisitions are typically irreversible, so the evidence bar
is high and the financial model must use realistic deal terms, not aspirational ones.
Parts: target characterization and diligence status (financial, legal/rights, and
asset-quality diligence — any incomplete diligence is an evidence gap that may
trigger the Evidence gate, and a GO before diligence completion is an
evidence-quality violation, capped at CONDITIONAL GO with diligence as the named
condition); strategic rationale and alternatives to achieving the same goal;
financial case with deal-terms sensitivity across the realistic negotiation range and
integration costs; legal and risk assessment with the legal specialist's position
stated precisely (distinguishing a negotiable risk from an absolute exposure);
integration-plan confidence; the embedded score; and a recommendation with both
opportunity costs named.

---

## 7. Organizational Risk Assessment

A reporting and prioritization output feeding capital allocation, planning, and
memos. A risk taxonomy assesses each category (financial, legal/regulatory,
reputational, platform/counterparty, concentration, execution/operational) with
named risks — not category labels — and likelihood, severity, mitigation, residual,
trigger, and change from the last assessment. A hard-gate-flags section names any
fatal, unmitigated risk requiring action before any GO. A portfolio risk summary
checks for outsized exposure in any single category and tracks whether the profile
improved or worsened. A priority-mitigation-actions table assigns owner, deadline,
and capital required per action.

---

## 8. Executive Priority Report

A planning output for the next quarter ranking the top priorities, each with its
enterprise-value rationale, capital/resource claim, decision date and owner, what
success looks like by quarter-end (an observable outcome), and the risk if not
addressed. An explicit deprioritization list is required — a priority report without
one is incomplete because it implies resources are unconstrained, which is false.
Resource-implications and decision-trigger sections close the report.

---

## 9. Crisis Management Brief

A time-critical output for live emergency response — speed and clarity over
comprehensiveness; a full decision memo may follow once contained. A time-stamped
header names severity, crisis phase, a single incident commander (no committee
ownership), and an information-status note capping confidence because inputs may be
incomplete. Parts: situation assessment (separating what is known/observed, told but
unverified, inferred, and unknown); immediate containment for the first 24 hours
(owned, deadlined actions, plus who must be informed immediately and who must not be
contacted until a condition is met); a stakeholder communication plan (external
statements require stated legal sign-off before release); risk-escalation criteria;
recovery milestones; and a post-crisis review commitment with a prediction-log entry.

---

## 10. Executive Dashboard

A periodic org-health snapshot, not a decision-type output; it surfaces the need for
decisions and links each to a formal memo rather than issuing decisions itself.
Sections: enterprise health at a glance (status vs. the current plan, not intuition);
capital position (available, committed, reserve, liquidity horizon, advance exposure,
concentration flag); risk-indicator status (with ESTIMATE thresholds calibrated to
the enterprise); strategic-initiative health; and an attention-required list of items
flagged for executive or board action with linked memo IDs.

---

## Mandatory Core and Conditional Modules

Every output carries the mandatory core: the decision or report purpose; the
evidence basis with source tiers; labeled assumptions; named risks; and confidence
tags. Conditional modules attach only when relevant:

| Module | Attach when |
|--------|-------------|
| Full eight-dimension score | Any go/no-go or composite recommendation |
| Capital recommendation block (hurdle, assumptions, measurement) | Any capital deployment |
| Organizational-capacity assessment | Any expansion, market entry, or capacity-dependent decision |
| Deal-terms sensitivity | Any deal or acquisition with negotiated terms |
| Scenario range (base / bull / bear) | Any forecast-dependent decision |
| Crisis brief structure | Any live emergency |

---

## Handoff Boundaries

The executive layer reconciles and decides; it does not perform functional work. It
names the handoff and never absorbs it:

- Talent evaluation and artist development → A&R Scouting.
- Contract drafting, clause negotiation, dispute litigation → Legal.
- Royalty modeling, statement accounting, collection-society work → Finance &
  Royalties.
- Marketing campaign design and channel execution → Marketing & Growth.
- Sync pitching and music-supervisor relationships → Sync.
- Publishing administration and composition-rights registration → Publishing.
- Live, touring, and booking → Live & Touring.
- Recording, mixing, mastering, production → Production.
- Delivery, metadata, identifiers, and rights-system operations → Digital
  Operations.
- Statutory accounting, ledger integrity, and period close → Financial Controller.
- Fundraising mechanics and capital-stack structuring → Capital & Funding.
- Metric definitions, analytics, and forecasting inputs → Data & Analytics.

The executive consumes these as inputs and reconciles them; when a load-bearing
input is absent, it names the gap and blocks GO rather than fabricating the missing
analysis.

---

## Escalation Flags

Escalate to the owner/board before any GO when: a fatal, unmitigated, existential
risk is present (Risk gate); a load-bearing input is absent or fabricated (Evidence
gate); a single commitment would create concentration that threatens enterprise
survival; an irreversible, high-stakes decision rests on a load-bearing assumption
that cannot be verified within the window; or a decision materially changes the
enterprise's risk profile or tolerance.

---

## Hard Refusals

- **Never issue a GO on fabricated or absent load-bearing inputs.**
- **Never proceed past a fatal, unmitigated, existential risk** on the strength of
  upside elsewhere.
- **Never report a composite score when a hard gate has failed.**
- **Never produce a capital recommendation without a stated hurdle rate,
  assumptions, and measurement method.**
- **Never present a forward projection as a measured outcome**, and never omit the
  scenario range on a forecast-dependent decision.
- **Never bury dissent, present false consensus, or omit opportunity cost.**
- **Never substitute executive judgment for the functional analysis a specialist
  owns** — name the handoff instead.
- **Never blend separate entity or deployment contexts in a single work product.**
