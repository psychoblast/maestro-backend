# Fanbase Health Scorecard

PLMKR knowledge bank — Fan and social domain.

The eight-dimension model for evaluating an artist's fan community, with weights, evidence
discipline, grade anchors, and hard gates. The mandate is not social reach — it is sustainable fan
relationship depth and superfan revenue concentration.

> The composite is **PROVISIONAL** until a corpus of outcome-checked evaluations exists. Until
> then: grade each dimension, compute the weighted composite, and report it labeled PROVISIONAL —
> never as calibrated or comparable across evaluations.

---

## DNA principle (required before every evaluation)

**"Own the relationship, not the algorithm."** An artist with 50,000 email subscribers and 500
active community members who buy, create, and recruit outperforms an artist with five million
followers at 0.3% engagement on every metric that matters to the business.

**The owned-channel test (answer explicitly in every evaluation):**
> "If every rented platform the artist uses were shut down tomorrow, what would remain of this fan
> community?"

Answer with evidence: which channels would survive, which fans would be reachable, and which
revenue streams would persist.

---

## Eight-dimension model

| Dimension | Weight | Mandate connection |
|-----------|--------|--------------------|
| Community Health | 0.20 | Foundation: depth of owned fan relationship |
| Superfan Concentration | 0.20 | Revenue: concentration in the few who drive value |
| D2C Conversion | 0.20 | Revenue realization from community investment |
| Engagement Lifecycle | 0.15 | Depth: fan progression through value stages |
| Platform Presence | 0.10 | Acquisition-layer quality and platform-native execution |
| Content Quality | 0.08 | Parasocial-depth driver via artist voice and authenticity |
| Fan Data Maturity | 0.05 | Intelligence layer enabling all other dimensions |
| Ambassador Program | 0.02 | Multiplier available once community is mature |
| **Total** | **1.00** | |

---

## Anti-fake-precision mechanics

Each sub-signal within a dimension is an evaluation INPUT, classified as one of:
- **MEASURED** — quantitative platform data (follower count, engagement rate, email open rate,
  community message rate, save rate).
- **SOURCED** — verifiable profile data (email-list size, community-platform presence, tool
  adoption, purchase history).
- **JUDGED** — qualitative assessment (content authenticity, fan-to-fan culture, program ethics,
  artist-voice credibility) — always labeled as such.

**Grading rules:**
1. Grade a sub-signal only where evidence exists for that signal type.
2. If no evidence exists for a sub-signal, mark it **NOT EVALUABLE** and name it explicitly — do
   not score it.
3. Each dimension receives a letter grade (A+ … F) based only on evaluable sub-signals.
4. The dimension assessment must state the share of sub-signals evaluated, the share NOT
   EVALUABLE, and which inputs drove the grade.
5. **Dimension confidence is capped by the weakest critical input — named explicitly.**

Letter→numeric for the composite: A+ = 10.0, A = 9.0, A− = 8.5, B+ = 8.0, B = 7.0, B− = 6.5,
C+ = 6.0, C = 5.0, C− = 4.5, D+ = 4.0, D = 3.0, D− = 2.5, F = 1.0.

---

## Dimension definitions and anchors

**1. Community Health — 0.20.** Depth, activity, and health of the owned and engaged community —
the foundation; without it, all other scores have a ceiling. Sub-signals: owned-channel stack
completeness (email + SMS + community platform) [SOURCED]; email/SMS engagement rate [MEASURED];
community active-member rate [MEASURED]; fan-to-fan interaction quality [JUDGED]; moderation
quality and culture [JUDGED]; new-member activation rate [MEASURED]; superfan-identification ratio
[SOURCED+JUDGED]. *A+/A:* owned stack complete and active; engagement above genre average;
fan-to-fan interactions are the majority of activity; superfan cohort identifiable; activation
>50%; the owned-channel test passes. *C+/C:* single owned channel; unexceptional engagement;
community functions mainly as a broadcast channel; no superfan identification. *F:* zero owned-
channel infrastructure — forced to F regardless of social presence.

**2. Superfan Concentration — 0.20.** Depth and identifiability of the cohort that drives
disproportionate revenue, advocacy, and energy. Sub-signals: identifiable cohort size
[SOURCED+JUDGED]; behavioral evidence (repeat purchasers, leaders, UGC creators); revenue share
attributable to the cohort; tier-of-access maturity. High grades require a behaviorally
identifiable cohort with a path to deepen; low grades reflect an undifferentiated fanbase.

**3. D2C Conversion — 0.20.** Realized revenue from community investment — membership/subscription,
merch, physical, crowdfunding — relative to community size and the value-exchange quality.
Exploitation signals (manipulative scarcity, FOMO-based access restriction) cap this dimension.

**4. Engagement Lifecycle — 0.15.** How well the artist moves fans through Discover → Follow →
Engage → Invest → Superfan → Evangelist, with attention to activation moments, post-purchase
management, and reactivation.

**5. Platform Presence — 0.10.** Quality and platform-native execution of the acquisition layer —
not breadth of presence. Scale-relative engagement must never lift the grade while depth indicators
(save rate, owned-channel conversion) are failing.

**6. Content Quality — 0.08.** Parasocial-depth driver: artist-voice share, authenticity signals
(spontaneity, emotional honesty, specificity), and the planned/reactive mix.

**7. Fan Data Maturity — 0.05.** The intelligence layer: identity resolution, first-party data
capture, behavioral segmentation, and analytics discipline that enable every other dimension.

**8. Ambassador Program — 0.02.** A multiplier available only once the community is mature;
genuine-advocate base, incentive structure (experiential/status over monetary at higher tiers), and
ethics. Often NOT EVALUABLE for early-stage artists — name it as such rather than scoring zero.

## Hard gates (assess before reporting a verdict)

- **Gate 1 — Owned-channel absence:** no owned-channel stack → Community Health forced to F. The
  composite is still computed but reflects structural absence.
- **Gate 2 — Ethics violation:** documented Terms-of-Service violations or coercive fan-labor
  dynamics → ETHICS FLAG; Ambassador Program forced to F; route to the Legal & Contracts domain
  and management before any ambassador investment. State the gate status in every crisis output
  even when it does not fire ("Gate 2: CLEAR — no documented ToS violations or coercive fan-labor
  dynamics").
- **Gate 3 — D2C exploitation:** documented manipulation signal → D2C Conversion capped at C;
  name the signal.
- **Gate 4 — Entity wall:** evaluation must run in a single, named context; blending invalidates
  it — re-run in the correct context.

If any gate triggers, address it explicitly before computing the composite; gate logic overrides
the composite verdict for the affected dimension.

---

## Owned-channel test (mandatory output)

State: owned channels active and their status; direct-reach coverage (share of the identifiable
fanbase reachable without third-party permission — SOURCED if measurable, JUDGED if estimated);
revenue resilience (which streams survive a platform shutdown, named specifically); and the result
— PASS (viable survival) / PARTIAL (some infrastructure survives) / FAIL (zero survival capability),
with the supporting reason.

**Verdict ladder:** REBUILD → DEVELOP → OPTIMIZE → SCALE → EXEMPLARY, derived from the composite
after gate logic is applied.
