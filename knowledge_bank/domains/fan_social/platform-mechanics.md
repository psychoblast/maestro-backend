# Platform Mechanics and Culture

PLMKR knowledge bank — Fan and social domain.

What kind of algorithm each platform runs, what content works there, and how a sound or piece of
content travels from seed to discovery.

> Currency note: platform features, algorithm dynamics, and payout rates change rapidly. Every
> specific claim must be currency-verified before it informs a recommendation.

---

## General Frameworks

### Named frameworks

**1. Algorithm taxonomy (three types).** What kind of algorithm a platform runs determines what
content strategy works there:

| Type | Primary signal | Content implication |
|------|----------------|---------------------|
| Interest-graph | Content behavior (what the user watches/engages with, not who they follow) | Content quality and retention matter most; follower count less relevant; new creators can reach large audiences |
| Social-graph | Network relationships (who you follow / who follows you) | Network size and engagement velocity from existing followers determine reach; harder for new creators |
| Intent-graph | Search and explicit-intent signals | Discoverability tied to search terms; long-form with searchable titles/descriptions performs best |

Most platforms now run hybrid algorithms — a single platform often has multiple algorithm
contexts (a feed surface vs. a stories surface vs. a search surface), not one universal one.

**2. Content-format taxonomy.**

| Format | Duration | Algorithm treatment | Fan-community use |
|--------|----------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Short-form | <90s | Interest-graph; completion rate dominant | Discovery, sound virality, awareness |
| Medium-form | 2–10 min | Intent-graph + retention curve | Storytelling, behind-the-scenes, tutorials |
| Long-form | 10 min+ | Completion and watch time | Deep engagement, documentaries, full sessions |
| Live | Real-time | Social-graph + notification | Community events, Q&A, listening parties |
| Community / broadcast | Async, conversational | Algorithm-light; owned reach | Superfan communication, direct access |

**Platform-native content principle:** content made natively for each platform's format and
culture outperforms content repurposed from another platform. Vertical vs. horizontal framing is
not an editing detail — it signals that the content was made for this context.

**3. Sound-discovery flywheel (short-video).** How a sound moves from creator to viral moment to
broad discovery:
1. **Seed:** original creator posts with a new sound; small initial audience.
2. **Signal:** early engagement (completion rate, likes, re-posts) determines algorithm
   treatment; strong signal earns broader distribution to interest-matched users.
3. **Use acceleration:** other creators use the sound; each use distributes to that creator's
   interest-graph audience.
4. **Cultural moment:** the sound associates with a trend or emotion; organic use accelerates;
   the algorithm surfaces it to the mainstream.
5. **Decay:** saturation reduces the novelty signal; completion rates fall; distribution declines.

Artists cannot reliably manufacture steps 3–4. They can control step 1 (quality of the original
post, on-trend hooks, timing) and step 2 (completion-rate optimization through hook engineering).
Attempts to engineer step 3 through coordinated re-use often produce inauthentic signals that
underperform organic use.

### Domain anti-patterns

1. **Cross-posting without platform-native adaptation.** Posting one platform's content to
   another with its original framing performs systematically worse; format-specific signals
   (completion rate here, watch time there) require format-specific content.
2. **Treating high follower count as a community-health predictor.** Follower count is a lagging,
   easily inflated, algorithm-dependent metric. A large account with low engagement has less
   community equity than a small account with high engagement. Use engagement rate, save rate,
   and comment depth as health signals.
3. **Presence on many platforms simultaneously at the early stage.** Attention is finite;
   spreading it across many platforms produces mediocre presence on all of them.
4. **Ignoring regional platform infrastructure.** An artist targeting a specific national market
   while operating only on globally dominant apps is missing the dominant platforms of that
   market.

## Music Modules

> The platform notes below describe durable mechanics, not point-in-time features. Verify any
> specific feature, threshold, or rate against current platform documentation before citing it.

### Short-video discovery platforms

Short-video is the dominant music-discovery surface for younger listeners: a large share of new
music discovery starts here, and most genre breakouts now have prior viral presence on these
platforms. The critical caveat: only a small fraction of short-video discovery leads to further
artist exploration — these platforms build streams, not superfans. Treat short-video as a
discovery layer, not a community layer. Algorithm signals reward completion rate (the first few
seconds are critical for the hook), sound engagement, re-posts/shares, and substantive comment
depth over emoji-only comments.

### Social-feed platforms

Feed platforms run interest-graph distribution on their short-video surfaces (engagement velocity
in the first hour determines broader push; completion, saves, and shares beat likes) and
social-graph distribution on their stories/broadcast surfaces. Opt-in broadcast channels reach
followers who actively subscribed and therefore exceed regular-post engagement. For new-artist
discovery, blind interest-graph short-video platforms tend to outperform; for deepening
engagement with an existing audience, feed platforms with established history perform better.

### Video platforms

Channel memberships (members-only posts, content, badges) make a video platform a real community
layer, not just a monetization surface — best for video-native artists with consistent output.
Short-form video surfaces run interest-graph algorithms similar to short-video apps, with the key
advantage that short content can link to longer content, enabling a discovery-to-depth funnel.
Scheduled premieres with live chat create real-time community moments; live streams reach the
subscriber notification list before the algorithm amplifies.

### Text/discourse platforms

Real-time text platforms retain strong value for music discourse, live-event commentary, and
direct, unfiltered artist-to-fan text — but reduced organic reach makes them a supplementary
direct-communication tool, not a primary community investment for new artists.

### Live-streaming platforms

Live-streaming platforms with an established music/DJ ecosystem build real-time community culture
differently from community servers: live chat, tipping culture, and subscription-badge
progression create dynamics specific to live interactive content. Best for artists with a
crossover gaming/streaming audience.

### Community servers

Largely algorithm-free — members see what is in their joined servers without algorithmic
filtering, which is the core advantage: direct reach to opted-in members. Role architecture
enables native superfan identification — fans who purchase or earn higher roles self-identify as
invested members.

### Regional platforms

Reaching fans in specific national markets requires that market's dominant platforms, which the
globally dominant apps do not substitute for:
- **Regional superfan/community platforms** dominate some Asian-market pop fandoms (community
  feed, live streaming, paid messaging, integrated merch shops) and are expected infrastructure
  for artists with affinity to those fanbases.
- **Messaging-first super apps** are the direct relationship layer in several Asian markets,
  enabling broadcast messages and in-app music discovery — essential, not optional, for an artist
  with a fanbase there.
- **Broadcast-messaging apps** are the primary direct layer in several other regions, where the
  globally dominant social apps serve as reach platforms and the messaging app serves as the
  relationship layer.
- **Market-specific charts** in some countries are not social platforms but community-mobilization
  targets — fan-organized streaming and physical-sales campaigns target specific chart windows.

> Where the artist's target markets are known, platform recommendations should reflect those
> actual markets rather than a generic global default. Default to the artist's primary market
> stack and add regional platforms only when explicit market context is present.
