# Output Templates & Handoffs

The fixed formats this domain produces, and the boundaries where it hands off to the
specialists who act. The domain's outputs are deliberately terse, dated, and sourced —
the formats below enforce that.

---

## 1. Output Discipline

Every output carries a mandatory core:
- **Date** of the scan or entry.
- **Source(s)** with tier for every factual claim.
- **Confidence** per entry, derived from source tier — not from how interesting the
  item is.

Conditional modules attach only when relevant. A scan report carries no recommendation
section: this domain reports the world; it does not prescribe the downstream action
(see Handoffs below).

---

## 2. The Standard Entry Format

Every published entry follows one shape:

```
[DATE] — [Territory tag] — [Consequence class]
Development: [what happened, one or two sentences]
Source: [name], Tier [A/B/C/D]  ([second source if corroborated])
So what?: [named specialist] should now [do / stop / modify] [specific action]
Practice implication: [one or two concrete sentences]
Confidence: [High / Medium / Low — tied to source tier]
```

An entry that cannot fill the "So what?" line is not written. An entry whose source
line is empty is not written.

---

## 3. The Alert Format (≤ 3 sentences)

Reserved for trigger-grade items that have cleared the timing protocol in
[[triage-and-routing]]:

```
ALERT — [DATE]
What changed: [one sentence]
Who was updated: [specialist list]
What to re-examine: [one sentence — the specific framework or plan to revisit]
```

Never breathless. Urgency is carried by the alert channel, not by adjectives.

---

## 4. The Quiet-Cycle Report

```
SCAN [DATE] — Quiet cycle
Sources covered: [count by tier]
Items at triage entry: [n]   Dropped: F1 [n] · F2 [n] · F3 [n] · F4 [n]
Lower-consequence items published: [n, or "none"]
Statement: No trigger-grade developments in [territory list] this cycle.
```

A quiet-cycle report is a complete, high-quality output — not an apology.

---

## 5. The NOT EVALUABLE Entry

```
[Territory / development] — NOT EVALUABLE this cycle
Reason: [insufficient primary/strong-trade data / contradictory sources / too early]
Sources checked: [list]
Resolution trigger: [what would make this evaluable]
Monitoring flag: [specific source / date window]
```

---

## 6. Handoff Boundaries

This domain feeds; it does not decide for the specialist. The handoff line is firm:

- **To Distribution & platform / Digital operations** — platform-policy, payout-model,
  and delivery-standard changes. This domain reports the change and names the affected
  rule; the specialist decides the operational response.
- **To Publishing & rights / Finance & royalties** — rights-rate, licensing, and
  collection-infrastructure changes. This domain dates and tiers the change; the
  specialist re-runs the affected numbers.
- **To Legal & business affairs** — legislation, rulings, and licensing prerequisites.
- **To A&R / Marketing / Sync / Live & touring / Fan & social** — signing-climate,
  market-share, crossover, content-quota, and creator-economy shifts framed as a
  changed decision for that specialist.
- **To Executive strategy / Capital & funding** — consolidation, market-structure, and
  capital-climate shifts with enterprise or financing implications.

Hard refusals at the boundary: this domain never writes the downstream
recommendation, never presents a rumor as fact across a handoff, and never routes a
generic "be aware" entry to multiple specialists in place of specialist-specific
implications.
