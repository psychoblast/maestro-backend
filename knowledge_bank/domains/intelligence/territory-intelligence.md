# Territory Intelligence

Music is regional again. A single-territory view is a blind view: market structures,
chart systems, rights bodies, content rules, and live economics diverge enough that
ignoring them produces confidently wrong advice. This file is the lens set — what to
scan for in each major region, framed structurally so it stays valid as specific
figures and named bodies change over time.

The discipline below is generic by design: name the *structural feature* to watch, not
a dated statistic. Specific market-share or rate figures belong in entries, each
carrying a source tier and date per [[source-trust-and-assessment]].

---

## 1. The Territory-Lens Method

For each territory, scan five structural axes:

1. **Streaming landscape** — which platforms hold share, and is share shifting?
2. **Chart system** — what counts a stream, what is the measurement methodology, and
   has it changed?
3. **Rights bodies** — which performing-rights and mechanical bodies operate, and what
   is their current rate cycle?
4. **Content rules** — domestic-content quotas, language requirements, broadcast
   regulation, licensing prerequisites for foreign catalog.
5. **Live economics** — touring access, ticketing structure, venue and promoter
   landscape, cross-border touring friction.

A change on any axis that survives the Four-Filter Method is an entry; a change to the
*rules* of an axis (a new quota, a chart-methodology change, a rights-rate reset) is a
candidate rule-changer.

## 2. North America

- **Streaming & charts:** mature, multi-platform market; chart methodology is
  documented and periodically revised — methodology changes are rule-changers for the
  data specialist.
- **Rights bodies:** multiple performing-rights organizations plus a dedicated
  mechanical-licensing body; rate schedules move on regulatory cycles.
- **Content rules:** domestic-content regulation exists in the northern market
  (broadcast content quotas, funding-body eligibility) — these shape catalog and
  promotion strategy and route to marketing and the distribution specialist.
- **Live economics:** large, consolidated touring and ticketing market; structural
  changes here route to live & touring with finance implications.
- **Sub-market to watch:** the regional-language and regional-genre segment can move
  independently of the national chart and is a frequent leading indicator.

## 3. Europe

- **Streaming & charts:** several large national markets, each with its own chart
  authority and methodology; national broadcasters carry outsized discovery weight in
  some markets.
- **Rights bodies:** strong national performing-rights and mechanical societies; rate
  changes persist for a regulatory cycle and are rule-changers for publishing and
  finance.
- **Content rules:** language quotas and domestic-content regulation exist in some
  markets and directly affect playlisting access and promotion — route to marketing
  and distribution.
- **Live economics:** cross-border touring friction (visa, carnet, and customs
  regimes after regulatory shifts) is a recurring structural cost — route to live &
  touring.

## 4. Asia-Pacific

- **Streaming & charts:** highly distinct per market — some are physical-sales-heavy,
  some platform-dominated by a small number of domestic services; chart bodies and
  sales-tracking methodologies differ sharply and are not interchangeable.
- **Production & fandom models:** some markets run a structured idol/production model
  where superfan physical-purchase behavior is the dominant health signal; treat
  participating-retailer sales data as partial, not total.
- **Rights bodies:** strong national societies; some markets impose licensing
  prerequisites on foreign catalog that are operational gating items — route to legal
  and distribution.
- **Platform distinctions:** short-video and streaming platforms that look similar
  across borders are often legally and operationally distinct entities per market;
  never collapse them.

## 5. Emerging & High-Growth Markets

- **Streaming landscape:** fast-growing, often mobile-first, frequently led by a
  regional platform rather than the global incumbents.
- **Crossover dynamics:** several regional genres have structural crossover pipelines
  into larger markets — a confirmed structural crossover shift is an opportunity-creator
  for A&R and marketing.
- **Rights bodies:** collection infrastructure is uneven; rights-coverage gaps are
  themselves intelligence and route to publishing and finance.
- **Coverage caution:** these markets are the most thinly covered in primary and
  strong-trade sources. Apply extra caution to useful-signal-tier claims; prefer
  official platform regional data where available; an explicit NOT EVALUABLE is often
  the honest output.

---

## 6. Cross-Territory Discipline

- **Never globalize a single-territory fact.** "Streaming is slowing" is meaningless
  without the territory and the window.
- **Watch the rules, not just the numbers.** A new quota or a chart-methodology change
  outranks a quarter of share movement, because it changes the game rather than the
  score.
- **Coverage gaps are reportable.** "No primary or strong-trade coverage of [region]
  this cycle" is a legitimate, useful entry — it tells downstream specialists not to
  treat silence as stability.
