# Playlist and DSP — Curator Relationships

Two-layer file: GENERAL FRAMEWORKS (domain-neutral) + MUSIC MODULES (music-specific).

---

## LAYER 1 — GENERAL FRAMEWORKS

### Relationship cadence logic (the trust account)

1. **Model every gatekeeper relationship as an account.** Deposits: delivered fit, useful
   intel, honest conduct, respecting a no. Withdrawals: every ask, every miss, every minute of
   wasted attention. Asks are budgeted against the balance — and the balance is tracked
   (evidence-tagged memory entries), not vibed.
2. **Cadence rule.** Never let consecutive withdrawals outnumber deposits. Between asks, the
   only correct contact is value — something useful to *them*, or silence. Manufactured
   check-ins are withdrawals wearing a costume.
3. **The no is handled better than the yes.** Graceful rejection handling is the cheapest
   deposit available and the most-remembered conduct signal. Arguing with a no closes the
   account.
4. **Relationship state is explicit:** cold / opened / warm / damaged / closed — each with the
   evidence for the current state. Strategy differs per state; treating cold as warm reads as
   presumptuous, treating warm as cold wastes the asset.
5. **People move; the account follows the person, not the seat.** Gatekeepers change lists,
   platforms, and roles — track the human, not the chair.

### Cadence numbers (working defaults)

- Follow-up timing by tier: **A = 3 days, B = 5 days, C = 7 days.**
- A product-wide daily send ceiling (~50 sends/day) caps total outreach volume.
- At most one follow-up per pitch unless the curator engages.
- A minimum of one release cycle between asks to the same curator after a no.
- After two consecutive non-responses, the next contact must be a **deposit** (useful intel, a
  thank-you with the outcome of their last add) — not an ask.
- Cadence violations are a checkable-gate failure: the pitch does not go out.

## LAYER 2 — MUSIC MODULES

### DSP editorial posture

- **There is no lobbying channel to platform editorial — the pitch tool is the relationship.**
  Posture: flawless tool usage, metadata hygiene, honest tagging (a mis-tagged genre to sneak
  onto a list is a withdrawal against the *platform* account), and patience across release
  cycles. Industry-event or trade contact with editorial staff follows strict professional
  conduct — never a pitch-ambush; log any stated preference as told-evidence.
- **Platform standing is the foundation all playlist work sits on.** Account health includes
  artificial-stream exposure, ToS compliance, and correctly-licensed content. Threats here
  destroy every other access surface. Route the legal questions to legal and business affairs —
  this discipline flags account risk, it does not advise on law.

### Independent curator vetting and outreach

- **Vet before pitching — the artist's account is on the line.** Playlist-health checks before
  any outreach: real-listener signals vs. follower count, engagement plausibility, add/removal
  churn pattern, and placement-for-pay red flags. A public "submission fee = guaranteed add"
  offer disqualifies the target entirely (that is the pay-for-play anti-pattern from the target
  side). A target failing the vet is blocked regardless of reach.
- **Outreach follows the trust-account rules** with the added reality that independents are
  unpaid humans: shorter pitches, exact list-fit naming, zero follow-up pressure.
- **Curator memory.** Every interaction lands in contact and playlist memory with evidence
  tags: observed (their adds/removals), told (their stated preferences), inferred (our
  hypotheses — never stated as fact). Placement history per list feeds fit profiles that
  compound over time.

### Relationship state → pitch decision

State gates the ask: damaged accounts get repair (or silence), not pitches; cold high-value
targets get fit-proof first contact, not favors. The decision table:

| State (evidence) | Ask allowed? |
|---|---|
| **cold** (no interactions) | yes — first contact, fit-proof-forward, lowest-pressure ask |
| **opened** (replied ≥1, no add) | yes — only with a *stronger-fit* track than the last; reference their feedback if any (told-evidence) |
| **warm** (≥1 add or positive reply) | yes — normal cadence; deposit after every delivered outcome |
| **damaged** (negative reply, cadence breach by us, or a missed commitment) | no — repair or silence for ≥1 release cycle |
| **closed** (explicit opt-out, or pay-for-play discovered) | never — opt-out is permanent; fee-for-add targets are disqualified entirely |

States are derived from observed primitives (last-pitched date, response rate, interaction
history); the gates above are the working policy applied on top of those facts.
