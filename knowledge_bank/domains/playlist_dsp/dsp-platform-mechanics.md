# Playlist and DSP — Per-Platform Mechanics

Two-layer file: GENERAL FRAMEWORKS (domain-neutral) + MUSIC MODULES (music-specific).
All benchmark figures (cost-per-listener, save-rate targets, threshold counts) are ESTIMATES —
not quotable — until confirmed against measured campaign data. Platform mechanics change
without notice; re-verify against the live platform surface each release cycle.

---

## LAYER 1 — GENERAL FRAMEWORKS

### Platform-as-gatekeeper model

Every streaming platform has two distinct access modes that require entirely different
strategies:

1. **Editorial access** — a human decision, by a named team, within a defined calendar window,
   through a specific submission channel. Editorial is programmable; relationships matter; the
   pitch is the lever.
2. **Algorithmic access** — a machine decision, on observed user-behavior signals, with no
   direct submission channel. Algorithms are not programmable; the lever is the *quality of
   early engagement signals* fed to the system, not any relationship or pitch.

Confusing these modes is the most common platform-strategy error. Emailing a platform hoping
for an algorithmic-playlist add wastes trust; assuming algorithmic lift will follow an
editorial add without understanding the signal chain misses the compounding opportunity.

**Relationship-posture hierarchy:**
1. **Platform standing** — account health, ToS compliance, metadata integrity, no artificial
   streams. Table stakes; violations here destroy all other access.
2. **Editorial relationship** — through the designated submission channel; conduct matters
   because editorial teams see cumulative pitch history, not just this pitch.
3. **Algorithmic positioning** — through the quality of early audience signals (saves,
   completes, user-playlist adds) after a release lands.

Paid promotional tools are **amplifiers that sit on top of this hierarchy** — they accelerate
what organic signals support; they cannot manufacture signals that do not exist.

### Paid promotion decision logic

Single principle: **amplify signals that already exist, never manufacture signals that do not.**
- New release + an active listener base → release-window paid tools are appropriate; ROI is
  measurable.
- Catalog release + active listeners → algorithmic paid tools (discovery-boost equivalents) are
  appropriate.
- New release + no established listener base → paid tools are ineffective; the signal base is
  not there to amplify. Spend on content and editorial pitch instead.
- Any release + the intent is cold-audience acquisition → most platform paid tools target
  existing/known listeners, not cold audiences. Social paid (off-platform) fits cold
  acquisition; platform paid tools serve re-engagement and conversion.

### Chart eligibility — general framework

1. **Unique-listener weighting** — most charts weight unique listeners alongside raw streams to
   blunt individual-user inflation; charts vary in how heavily.
2. **Authenticity rules** — abnormal patterns (repeat plays from one source, bot-pattern
   behavior) trigger suppression or removal.
3. **Territory eligibility** — most charts count only plays from the target territory.
4. **Time window** — rolling 24-hour, 7-day, or 4-week windows depending on chart type; fresh
   and catalog releases often have different decay curves.

Where a platform does not publish exact component weightings, any specific split is an
analyst estimate — label it as such and never quote it as confirmed.

## LAYER 2 — MUSIC MODULES

The four largest Western platforms each split into editorial and algorithmic mechanics. Treat
them separately; never aggregate.

### Largest streaming platform (editorial + algorithmic)

**Editorial:**
- **Access:** the artist-facing pre-release pitch tool is the *only* legitimate editorial
  channel — no email, no side door, no distributor shortcut. The artist or their management
  pitches directly.
- **Rules:** one track pitchable at a time; must be pre-release and not yet live; complete
  metadata required (ISRC, release date, distribution complete).
- **Lead time:** a documented hard floor of ~7 days before release; practical norm 2–4 weeks;
  pitching ≥2 weeks out materially improves consideration. Working default: target ~3 weeks,
  treat ~2 weeks as the comfortable floor, ~7 days as the hard floor — re-verify each cycle.
- **Fields:** genre/mood/instrumentation tags (mandatory — mis-tagging is an account
  withdrawal), audience description, and the written pitch (the narrative fit case). Fields
  gate; prose persuades.
- **Team structure:** a large global editorial team organized by genre, mood/occasion, and
  territory; each editor owns a playlist portfolio. There are no named external pitch contacts —
  the tool is the relationship. The highest-volume weekly add event is the new-music-Friday
  refresh.
- **The release-day ceiling:** if the editorial pitch is not submitted before release, the
  follower-reach algorithmic surface is also lost. Missing the window costs two surfaces.

**Algorithmic:**
- **Follower-reach new-release feed:** a personalized new-release playlist for an artist's
  followers; eligibility requires the pre-release pitch be submitted (even if rejected) plus a
  minimum gap since the prior release. Reach scales with follower count — the one major surface
  where follower count genuinely matters.
- **Personalized discovery playlist:** collaborative filtering surfaces unfamiliar tracks to
  users with similar listening patterns; save rate and completion of initial listeners are the
  primary seeding signals. A large editorial add can seed this surface — the
  editorial-to-algorithmic amplification chain.
- **Radio / autoplay:** driven by audio-feature analysis (tempo, key, energy, valence) plus
  behavioral co-occurrence. A discovery-boost program can trade a royalty reduction on the
  enrolled track for higher probability in radio/autoplay sessions — best for evergreen catalog
  and the small-to-mid listener range, never where full royalty rate matters, and never cold
  acquisition.
- **Key algorithmic signals (rough priority):** (1) save-to-stream ratio in the first ~72h
  (ESTIMATE target north of ~12% for strong seeding — validate against measured outcomes);
  (2) completion rate vs. genre baseline; (3) user-playlist adds; (4) early-skip rate in the
  first 30 seconds (high early-skip suppresses push — the opening seconds are a distinct
  optimization target); (5) follow conversion from discovery surfaces.

**Paid promotion:** a release-window full-screen tool aimed at recent active listeners
(re-engagement, cost-per-listener model, new releases only) and a home-feed banner tool that
works for both new and catalog releases. Both require a minimum recent-stream-or-follower base;
neither is cold acquisition. For new-audience acquisition, use off-platform social advertising.

### Selective-editorial platform (smaller team, more weight per add)

- **Access is distributor-gated** — artist-direct access is not generally available; independent
  artists submit through their distributor, whose editorial relationships vary. Choosing a
  distributor with active editorial relationships on this platform is a *strategic* decision,
  not an administrative one.
- **Lead time:** a ~10-day-before-release floor for standard consideration; below ~7 days is a
  low-odds "late add." Best practice 3–4 weeks. How earlier submission affects odds is opaque
  by design here.
- **Spatial / immersive audio is a positioning lever:** tracks delivered in immersive audio
  receive elevated visibility in this platform's discovery and editorial surfaces, which it
  treats as a differentiator. Not mandatory, but meaningful.
- **Editorial philosophy:** a smaller team carries proportionally more curation weight per
  placement and will back artistically ambitious work, not only streaming velocity. Adds carry
  high credibility *because* the team is selective.
- **No self-serve paid promotion** exists here — editorial is the only programmable surface, so
  editorial strategy is higher-leverage per unit of effort. Invest pitch bandwidth accordingly.
- **Cross-language lyric features** (translation/pronunciation surfaces) create concrete new
  editorial pitch angles for catalog with non-English lyric content into cross-language
  audience corridors.

### Self-serve post-release-window platform

- **Access is self-serve and direct** — no distributor intermediary needed for submission; the
  track must be live in the catalog with streaming rights.
- **Post-release pitch window:** the pitch window stays open for ~14 days *after* release — the
  notable Western platform with a meaningful post-release editorial window. Tracks that gain
  organic early momentum can be pitched mid-wave with data behind the case.
- **Smart-speaker reach is the unique value:** this platform's editorial playlists are the
  primary content surfaced by its voice ecosystem when users ask to "play [genre/mood]." An
  editorial add here carries voice-assistant reach the others cannot replicate. Voice-driven
  listeners tend to be more passive and skip less, which shifts completion-rate signals.
- **No self-serve paid promotional equivalent.**

### Video-first platform (editorial + algorithmic)

- **No self-serve editorial submission portal** — access runs through content-partner and
  distributor relationships. The verified artist-channel status is the prerequisite for serious
  consideration; a channel without it is not recognized as an artist presence.
- **The main platform recommendation engine is the dominant discovery surface** — a recommended
  music video reaches orders of magnitude more listeners than an in-app music-playlist placement
  alone. Primary ranking signals for video: watch time / completion, re-watch, shares, comments
  with reply-chain engagement, and click-through from impressions.
- **Short-form clips** are a separate algorithm that cross-promotes to full-length content; a
  viral short seeding a full video is a documented amplification path, with short completion as
  the primary ranking signal.
- **Leading-indicator behavior:** a song trending here often *precedes* lift on the audio
  platforms — practitioners watch the sequence of organic video trend → audio radio/autoplay
  lift → editorial consideration.
- **Video-first floor:** even in the music tab, a professional static-art-with-audio upload is
  the minimum; a music or lyric video is meaningfully better. Rights-fingerprinting registration
  (through the distributor or a rights partner) generates royalties on user-generated content
  using the sound, independent of channel monetization.

### Lossless-positioned regional-strength platform

- **Access is label/distributor-gated;** lead time ~7 days before release recommended, with a
  post-release window of roughly two weeks. Strongest editorial presence in French-language and
  selected regional markets — a placement there in those markets carries different value than
  the same placement on the largest platform. An invitation to record one of its in-house
  acoustic sessions is a meaningful editorial-relationship signal, not just a content format.
- **Algorithmic surfaces:** a primary personalized continuous-radio surface and a weekly
  personalized new-discovery playlist; collaborative filtering with save rate, completion, and
  user-playlist adds as the primary inputs. No self-serve paid promotion — editorial is the only
  programmable surface.
