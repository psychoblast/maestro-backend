## AI Music Production Tools

SUNO AI (suno.com):
- Text to music generation
- Best for: demo creation, reference tracks, exploring new sounds, writer's block breaker
- Limitation: full commercial rights complex — check current terms before releasing
- Use case: generate a beat concept, then recreate with real producer

UDIO (udio.com):
- Similar to Suno, strong for genre-specific generation
- Best for: quick concept exploration, reference generation

SOUNDRAW (soundraw.io):
- Royalty-free music generation for content creators
- Best for: background music for videos, content creation

AIVA (aiva.ai):
- AI composition, strong for cinematic and orchestral
- Best for: sync opportunities in film and TV adjacent content

BOOMY (boomy.com):
- Create and distribute AI-generated music
- Best for: building streaming catalog for passive income
- Limitation: Spotify has removed some AI-generated content — check current policies

## AI for Lyrics and Writing

CLAUDE (Anthropic): Best for lyric concepts, song structure, creative writing assistance
CHATGPT: Good for brainstorming, rhyme schemes, concept development
CO-WRITER (apps): Various apps specifically for songwriting assistance

IMPORTANT: AI should assist the songwriting process — not replace it. Lyrics generated entirely by AI lack the authentic human experience that connects with listeners. Use AI to break blocks and explore ideas, not to write songs for you.

## The AI and Copyright Landscape 2025/2026

This is changing rapidly. Current status:
- AI-generated music copyright: unclear in most jurisdictions — no human author = no copyright in USA currently
- AI trained on copyrighted music: major lawsuits ongoing (Suno, Udio sued by major labels)
- SOCAN and APRA AMCOS: actively lobbying for AI licensing frameworks
- WMG CEO: AI music platforms moving to DSPs on variable revenue share basis
- Recommendation: use AI as a tool in human creative process — not as the sole creator
