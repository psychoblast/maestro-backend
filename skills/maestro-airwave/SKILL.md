## The Radio and Playlist Landscape 2025/2026

Traditional broadcast radio is declining in the US (Cumulus Radio just filed bankruptcy) but remains powerful in:
- UK: BBC Radio 1, 1Xtra, 6 Music career-making
- Canada: CBC Music, regional urban stations
- Nigeria: Cool FM, Beat FM, Wazobia — massive influence
- Ghana: Joy FM, Asaase Radio
- South Africa: Metro FM, 5FM, YFM
- Jamaica: Irie FM, ZIP FM
- France: NRJ, Fun Radio, Mouv
- Germany: Fritz, 1LIVE, Bayern 3

Algorithmic playlists now drive MORE discovery than editorial on Spotify. Mike Biggane: "The artist who understands personalization wins."

## Spotify Playlist Ecosystem

EDITORIAL PLAYLISTS (curated by Spotify staff):
- Submit through Spotify for Artists minimum 7 days before release
- Fill every field completely — mood, style, genre, story
- Target playlists: New Music Friday, Fresh Finds, genre-specific editorial
- One submission per release — make it count

ALGORITHMIC PLAYLISTS (driven by listener behavior):
- Discover Weekly: driven by save rate and completion rate
- Release Radar: followers of artist get new releases here automatically
- Daily Mixes: driven by listening history
- Radio: similar artists radio
- These are MORE important than editorial for most artists

INDEPENDENT CURATOR PLAYLISTS:
- SubmitHub: paid submission, curators commit to listening ($1-3 per submission)
- Groover: European-focused, strong for R&B and electronic
- Playlist Push: larger network, higher cost
- Direct outreach: find curators on Spotify, DM on Instagram

## Independent Radio Promoters

At a certain level, hiring an independent radio promoter makes sense:
- Canada: $1,000-3,000/month for independent promotion campaign
- USA: $1,000-5,000/week for serious radio push
- UK: £1,000-3,000/month

Only hire when: the song is strong enough, the artist has some momentum, and the budget can sustain a minimum 8-week campaign.
