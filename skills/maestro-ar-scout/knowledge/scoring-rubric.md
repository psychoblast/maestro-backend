## DNA Principle (Required Reading Before Every Signing Evaluation)

**"Optimize for career longevity, not first-week streams."**

The A&R mandate is multi-year career trajectory, not launch metrics. A debut with modest first-week numbers but building save rates, deepening engagement, and consistent release execution outranks a launch spike with no fan formation. Every signing evaluation must be answered from this orientation.

**The Five-Year Test (answer explicitly in every signing evaluation):**
> "If this artist debuts today, could they still matter five years from now after trends change, algorithms change, and AI-generated music becomes ubiquitous?"

This is not a rhetorical prompt. Answer it explicitly with evidence in every signing evaluation output. If the answer requires hedging beyond "yes, here is why" or "no," state the specific conditions that would change it.

**AI-Era Principle (binding):**
> "The harder AI makes music creation, the more valuable human identity, storytelling, fan community, consistency, and cultural relevance become — reward those traits more than technical perfection."

This principle governs the relative weighting of Artist Identity & Brand (.25) and Execution & Team (.15) against pure production quality. An artist with a powerful, authentic identity and demonstrated execution discipline is structurally more defensible in the AI era than one with technically superior recordings and no identity core.

## Anti-Fake-Precision Mechanics

Each sub-signal within a pillar is an evaluation **INPUT** classified as one of:
- **MEASURED** — audio analysis data (LUFS, crest factor, stereo width, BPM, intro timing, duration)
- **SOURCED** — verifiable profile data (streaming numbers, save rate, chart position, editorial adds, tour dates, territory split)
- **JUDGED** — qualitative assessment (emotional impact, authenticity, storytelling, interview personality) — always labeled as such

**Grading rules:**
1. Grade a sub-signal only where evidence exists for that signal type
2. If no evidence exists for a sub-signal: mark it **NOT EVALUABLE** and name it explicitly — do not score it, do not infer it
3. Each pillar receives a letter grade (A+, A, A–, B+, B, B–, C+, C, C–, D+, D, D–, F) based only on evaluable sub-signals
4. The pillar assessment must state: share of sub-signals evaluated, share NOT EVALUABLE, and which inputs drove the grade
5. **Pillar confidence is capped by the weakest critical input — named explicitly.** If a pillar's most critical sub-signal is NOT EVALUABLE, the pillar receives a LOW confidence tag regardless of grade

Many Execution and Identity sub-signals will be NOT EVALUABLE when only audio material is available. That honesty is mandatory. A fabricated evaluation is worse than an incomplete one.

**Letter grade → numeric equivalent (for composite calculation):**

| Grade | Numeric | Grade | Numeric |
|-------|---------|-------|---------|
| A+ | 10.0 | C+ | 6.0 |
| A | 9.0 | C | 5.0 |
| A– | 8.5 | C– | 4.5 |
| B+ | 8.0 | D+ | 4.0 |
| B | 7.0 | D | 3.0 |
| B– | 6.5 | D– | 2.5 |
| — | — | F | 1.0 |

## Pillar 1: Music Quality — Weight: 0.30

**Definition:** Does this music make people want to hear it again? Assessed across the full craft stack — composition, performance, and production — weighted together because commercial music lives or dies as a unified sensory experience.

**Sub-signals:**

| Sub-signal | Classification | Notes |
|-----------|----------------|-------|
| Emotional impact | JUDGED | Does it move the listener? Generic execution produces no emotional response. |
| Memorability | JUDGED | Is any element independently recallable after one listen? |
| Replay value | JUDGED | Does the recording reward re-listening? Hidden elements, vocal nuance, production details. |
| Hook speed | MEASURED+JUDGED | Intro timing MEASURED (audio); hook quality JUDGED |
| Lyrical originality | JUDGED | Specific, ownable lyric perspective vs. genre clichés |
| Melody quality | JUDGED | Distinctiveness and articulability without genre shorthand |
| Chorus lift | MEASURED+JUDGED | Energy differential MEASURED (LUFS shift); structural impact JUDGED |
| Dynamic progression | MEASURED | Crest factor, LUFS arc, arrangement variation |
| Production quality | MEASURED | Integrated LUFS, stereo width, broadcast-competitive standard |
| Arrangement | JUDGED | Intentionality of instrument/element choices; space and tension |
| Vocal performance | JUDGED | Technical adequacy + tonal identity + interpretive authority |
| Commercial accessibility | SOURCED+JUDGED | Format fit, duration, BPM relative to active editorial categories |
| DSP friendliness | MEASURED+SOURCED | Intro timing, LUFS, skip-risk indicators |
| Live potential | JUDGED | Would this song translate and grow in a live setting? |

**Letter grade anchors:**

| Grade | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| **A+ / A** | Multiple sub-signals at best-in-class. Hook lands in first 15 seconds with a measurable energy differential; melody independently articulable; lyric has a quotable line; production LUFS-optimized and broadcast-competitive; vocal identity distinctive in one phrase. Would pass blind listening at any major editorial tier. |
| **B+ / B** | Most sub-signals competent to strong. Hook present and timely; melody describable; lyric specific even if not quotable; production within competitive range; vocal has tonal identity. Some sub-signals average but none are structural barriers. |
| **C+ / C** | Mixed. Some sub-signals strong, one or more structural weaknesses. Hook delay OR energy-differential deficit OR generic lyric OR production outside competitive LUFS range. Weaknesses are named and their impact on ceiling stated. |
| **D+ and below** | One or more structural failures. Production below broadcast standard OR no identifiable hook OR lyric entirely generic OR vocal technically inadequate. Does not clear Tier 1 minimum without documented re-recording plan. |
| **F** | Hard Gate 1 territory. PASS regardless of other pillars. |

**Confidence cap:** if production quality (MEASURED) is NOT EVALUABLE, Music Quality confidence is capped at PARTIAL regardless of other sub-signal grades.

## Pillar 2: Artist Identity & Brand — Weight: 0.25

**Definition:** Does this artist possess a distinct, ownable identity — sonic, visual, and narrative — that supports a multi-year career arc and cannot be easily replicated by a competitor or AI-generated content?

**Sub-signals:**

| Sub-signal | Classification | Notes |
|-----------|----------------|-------|
| Authenticity | JUDGED | Does the identity originate from the artist or feel constructed externally? |
| Uniqueness | JUDGED | Is this artist articulable without reference to another artist? |
| Visual identity | SOURCED+JUDGED | Consistency of aesthetic across platforms |
| Storytelling | JUDGED | Does the artist have a grounded, specific narrative? |
| Cultural relevance | SOURCED+JUDGED | Connection to a living cultural moment or community |
| Social content ability | SOURCED | Demonstrated through platform history; engagement quality, not volume |
| Interview personality | JUDGED | How the artist presents without the record. NOT EVALUABLE without interview/live material. |
| Fan connection | SOURCED | Comment quality, UGC behavior, show attendance relative to streaming |
| Long-term brand potential | JUDGED | Can this identity extend beyond music into other domains? |
| Memorability | JUDGED | Is the artist, not just the song, independently memorable? |

**Letter grade anchors:**

| Grade | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| **A+ / A** | Identity articulable in one sentence without genre clichés. Consistent across all evaluated touchpoints. Narrative grounded in something specific and ownable. Cultural relevance present and active. Fan connection shows core audience formation. Long-term brand case articulable without fabrication. |
| **B+ / B** | Identity clear and distinguishable from peer set. Narrative present and specific even if not immediately iconic. Visual consistency and platform intentionality evident. Authenticity credible. |
| **C+ / C** | Identity defined primarily by genre tag or requires referencing another artist to explain. Some individual signals strong but overall identity not yet ownable. Narrative generic. Cultural relevance not demonstrated. |
| **D+ and below** | Identity not yet formed. Interchangeable with any peer in the genre. No articulable narrative. No visual consistency. |
| **F** | Hard Gate 2 territory. PASS regardless of composite. |

**Confidence cap:** Interview personality and long-term brand potential are JUDGED and often NOT EVALUABLE without live/interview material. State explicitly.

## Pillar 3: Audience & Market — Weight: 0.20

**Definition:** Does this artist have an audience, and is that audience growing? Includes the Trajectory Principle: acceleration beats static size.

### Trajectory Principle

**Doctrine:** A small, accelerating artist with deep engagement outranks a large, declining one.

> **Example as doctrine illustration:** An artist with 50K monthly listeners growing +10%/month, 15% save rate, high repeat listening, strong comments, and consistent release cadence outranks one with 2M monthly listeners on a declining trajectory, declining save rate, and no release consistency.

**Evaluate trajectory on:**
- Monthly listener growth rate (positive / flat / declining over 60–90 day window)
- Save-rate trend across consecutive releases (improving / stable / declining)
- Repeat-listening trend (SOURCED from available platform data)
- Release consistency (cadence gap, output regularity)

**Trajectory thresholds:**

| Signal | Strong | Neutral | Weak |
|--------|--------|---------|------|
| Monthly listener growth | +8%/month or more, sustained 60+ days | ±2% | Declining over 60+ days |
| Save-rate trend | Improving across 2+ consecutive releases | Stable | Declining across 2+ consecutive releases |
| Repeat listening | Top-quartile for genre equivalent (if SOURCED) | Mid-range | Below median |
| Release consistency | 1 release per 4–8 weeks sustained | Irregular but recent | No release in 90+ days |

**Sub-signals:**

| Sub-signal | Classification | Notes |
|-----------|----------------|-------|
| Save rate | SOURCED | Intent signal; >5% on new release is meaningful (Tier B estimate, labeled) |
| Listener retention | SOURCED | Monthly listener retention across a 90-day window |
| Repeat listening | SOURCED | If available from platform analytics; NOT EVALUABLE without access |
| Organic growth | SOURCED | Non-paid listener growth trajectory |
| Comments quality | SOURCED+JUDGED | Fan formation signal; quality over volume |
| UGC creation | SOURCED | User-generated content using the artist's music |
| Geographic spread | SOURCED | Territory distribution; concentration vs. multi-market breadth |
| DSP algorithm strength | SOURCED | Release Radar, Discover Weekly, algorithmic playlist adds |
| Core fan density | SOURCED+JUDGED | Ratio of core (repeat, high-engagement) listeners to casual stream-throughs |
| Casual-to-fan conversion | SOURCED+JUDGED | Evidence that casual listeners are converting to saves, follows, or live attendance |
| Ticket potential | SOURCED+JUDGED | Touring indicators relative to streaming level |
| Merch potential | SOURCED+JUDGED | Evidence of fan identity attachment beyond streaming |

**Letter grade anchors:**

| Grade | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| **A+ / A** | Strong trajectory (+8%/month+ listeners), save rate >8% on recent release, improving trend across 2+ releases, active UGC, multi-territory footprint, core fan indicators present. |
| **B+ / B** | Positive trajectory even if modest. Save rate 4–8%, stable or improving trend. Geographic spread developing. Algorithm adds occurring. Core fan base forming. |
| **C+ / C** | Mixed signals. Some growth but inconsistent; or large listener base with declining engagement quality. Save rate below 4%. |
| **D+ and below** | No meaningful audience data available (NOT EVALUABLE dominant) OR clear declining trajectory OR audience present but no engagement quality signals. |

**Confidence cap:** if save rate and trajectory data are NOT EVALUABLE, Audience & Market confidence is capped at PARTIAL. State explicitly.

## Pillar 4: Execution & Team — Weight: 0.15

**Definition:** Does this artist ship work consistently, behave professionally, and operate with a team capable of executing a commercial development plan?

**Sub-signals:**

| Sub-signal | Classification | Notes |
|-----------|----------------|-------|
| Work ethic | JUDGED | Demonstrated through output volume and release consistency |
| Release consistency | SOURCED | Verifiable from release history on DSPs |
| Responsiveness | JUDGED | Usually NOT EVALUABLE without direct contact |
| Team quality | SOURCED+JUDGED | Manager, attorney, booking agent — names and track records |
| Manager quality | SOURCED+JUDGED | Track record of the manager with comparable artists |
| Financial discipline | JUDGED | Usually NOT EVALUABLE without direct working relationship |
| Content consistency | SOURCED | Social content frequency and platform behavior |
| Ability to finish projects | SOURCED | Completed vs. abandoned projects visible from catalog |
| Touring willingness | SOURCED+JUDGED | Touring history and stated willingness |
| Professionalism | JUDGED | Usually NOT EVALUABLE without direct working relationship |

**Letter grade anchors:**

| Grade | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| **A+ / A** | Consistent release cadence documented. Strong team (experienced manager + attorney + booking agent). Multiple completed projects. Content strategy coherent and sustained. Touring activity matching streaming level. |
| **B+ / B** | Mostly consistent execution. Team partially in place (manager + attorney minimum). Some release cadence gaps but completed projects present. Content active. |
| **C+ / C** | Inconsistent execution. Long gaps between releases. Team missing key roles. Content strategy absent or erratic. |
| **D+ and below** | Pattern of unfinished projects, team absence, or release cadence breakdown. Hard Gate 4 risk zone. |
| **F** | Hard Gate 4 territory. PASS unless extraordinary documented evidence overrides. |

**Confidence cap:** responsiveness, financial discipline, and professionalism are JUDGED and frequently NOT EVALUABLE. State explicitly.

## Pillar 5: Commercial Opportunity — Weight: 0.10

**Definition:** Does this artist have specific, identifiable commercial pathways beyond the core recording — and does the market infrastructure exist to pursue them?

> "Commercial opportunity is not the same thing as music quality." A low-commercial-opportunity score does not reflect on the music; it reflects on the market infrastructure available for this specific artist at this specific moment.

**Sub-signals:**

| Sub-signal | Classification | Notes |
|-----------|----------------|-------|
| Playlist | SOURCED+JUDGED | Specific editorial families identifiable; pitch window status |
| Radio | MEASURED+SOURCED | Format fit (duration, BPM, structure) + current format activity |
| Sync | JUDGED | Scene safety, instrumental viability, thematic versatility |
| International | SOURCED+JUDGED | Territory data + genre infrastructure for expansion |
| Licensing | JUDGED | Brand partnership fit; cultural relevance to brand categories |
| Brand partnership | SOURCED+JUDGED | Existing brand relationships or credible alignment with active brand categories |
| Touring | SOURCED | Venue-size trajectory relative to streaming |
| Publishing opportunity | SOURCED+JUDGED | Catalog quality for sync/licensing; co-writing track record |

**Letter grade anchors:**

| Grade | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| **A+ / A** | Multiple specific commercial pathways identified with current market evidence. Each pathway grounded in data or named comparable cases — not invented. |
| **B+ / B** | At least two commercial pathways specifically identified and supported. Some pathways require development before activation. No structural barriers. |
| **C+ / C** | One specific commercial pathway identified. Others possible but not articulable without fabrication. |
| **D+ and below** | No specific commercial pathway identifiable beyond passive DSP streaming. Hard Gate 3 risk zone if commercial viability absent. |

## Composite Formula

```
Composite = (MQ × 0.30) + (AIB × 0.25) + (AM × 0.20) + (ET × 0.15) + (CO × 0.10)
```
Where each pillar value is the numeric equivalent of the letter grade (see Anti-Fake-Precision section).

Maximum possible composite: 10.0 · Weights sum: 1.00 (verified: 0.30 + 0.25 + 0.20 + 0.15 + 0.10 = 1.00)

## Unfair Advantage Assessment (Mandatory — Every Signing Evaluation)

**Definition:** Does this artist possess something competitors cannot easily copy?

**Catalog of unfair advantages:**
- Elite songwriter (demonstrably at a level competitors cannot quickly replicate)
- Extraordinary voice (tonal signature that is immediately distinctive and technically beyond peer set)
- Viral personality (documented ability to generate organic attention beyond the music itself)
- Strong founder story (lived narrative that is specific, compelling, and ownable)
- Cultural movement (the artist is associated with or leads a real, active cultural community)
- Existing fan community (pre-built audience with documented deep engagement)
- Cross-market appeal (simultaneous credibility in multiple audiences)
- Language advantage (native access to a market with limited domestic supply in their genre)
- Creator ecosystem (established network of collaborators, co-writers, producers who raise the artist's output ceiling)
- Celebrity network (access to collaborators or co-signers that creates structural distribution advantages)
- Exceptional live show (documented live performance capability that generates word-of-mouth)
- Elite work ethic (demonstrated output discipline that structurally exceeds peer set)
- First-mover position (entering a genre or territory gap before competitors)

**Output format:**
- State advantages identified with evidence (what observable signal supports the claim)
- If no unfair advantage identified: state "None identified" — never invent one
- A genuine unfair advantage elevates a WATCH toward PURSUE and a PURSUE toward GREENLIGHT, and is required for SIGN IMMEDIATELY
- Absence of an unfair advantage is context, not a gate

## Verdict Ladder

| Verdict | Composite threshold | Additional conditions |
|---------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| **PASS** | < 5.0 | — OR — any Hard Gate triggered (regardless of composite) |
| **WATCH** | 5.0–6.4 | Talent present; development required. State specific unlock conditions for re-evaluation. |
| **DEVELOP** | 6.5–7.9 | Trajectory grade weak OR Execution & Team grade C or below. Investment in active development before pipeline advancement. |
| **PURSUE** | 6.5–7.9 | Trajectory grade strong (B or above) AND Execution & Team grade B– or above. Pipeline entry warranted. |
| **GREENLIGHT** | 8.0+ | All four gates clear. Proceed to full due diligence and committee briefing. |
| **SIGN IMMEDIATELY** | 8.0+ | GREENLIGHT conditions + all four gates clean + at least one identified unfair advantage with evidence + competitive-pressure signal (documented reason urgency is real). |

**DEVELOP vs. PURSUE split rule:** both are in the 6.5–7.9 composite range. The split is determined by the trajectory grade and the Execution & Team grade together:
- Trajectory strong (B or above) AND ET grade B– or above → PURSUE
- Either trajectory weak OR ET grade C or below → DEVELOP

## Career Ceiling Assessment

| Career ceiling | Qualitative definition | Strategy implication |
|---------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **Local** | Artist can sustain a loyal local or niche audience; limited editorial and format reach | Fan-development strategy; community focus |
| **National** | Artist can achieve editorial traction and national-market awareness | Genre editorial pitches; algorithmic optimization |
| **International** | Artist can sustain multi-territory editorial and platform presence with a commercial campaign | Full editorial pitch; DSP relations; timing-optimized release |
| **Global Superstar** | Artist has the format, identity, and market infrastructure for cross-platform global commercial breakthrough | Radio promo; sync pipeline; national PR |

**Ceiling is stated with qualitative confidence + specific reasons.** Never assert ceiling without named evidence. Overstating is worse than understating — it misdirects development investment and damages trust.

## Probability-Unlock Rule (Do Not Paraphrase)

> "The percentage-probability format (e.g. '35% chance of major-label success') is encoded as a DEFINED FUTURE FORMAT with unlock condition: available only when the prediction log holds a sufficient outcome corpus for calibration (≥30 outcome-checked evaluations minimum); until then any probability language is forbidden as fake precision."

Until the unlock condition is met: **no percentage probabilities anywhere in any output.** This includes confidence expressions framed as probabilities, informal probability language ("roughly a 40% shot"), and any numeric probability framing for career outcomes.

State qualitative confidence (high / medium / low, with reasons) instead.

## Hard Gates (4) — Override Composite Regardless of Total

Any gate triggered → verdict is **PASS** with the gate explicitly cited. No composite can override a triggered gate.

**Hard Gate 1 — Production Floor**
Music Quality pillar grade F (production quality sub-signal at D or below with no credible re-recording plan documented) → PASS.
*Rationale: production structurally uncompetitive cannot be addressed through mixing/mastering alone. A re-recording plan must be explicitly identified before this gate clears.*

**Hard Gate 2 — Identity Floor**
Artist Identity & Brand pillar grade F → PASS.
*Rationale: A&R signs careers, not songs. An artist with no articulable identity has no development arc. No composite can compensate for the absence of something to develop.*

**Hard Gate 3 — No Commercial Lane**
Commercial Opportunity pillar D or below AND Music Quality commercial-accessibility sub-signal NOT EVALUABLE or at D-equivalent → PASS.
*Rationale: if no commercial lane exists, no composite score can manufacture one. Development does not create a market that does not exist.*

**Hard Gate 4 — Execution & Team Floor**
Execution & Team pillar D– or F-equivalent → PASS unless extraordinary documented evidence overrides.
*Override requires:* name the specific evidence that makes the execution risk manageable (e.g., a proven manager has committed to the project with track record cited). A general argument that "the music is too good to pass" is not an override.
*Rationale: an artist who cannot execute a development plan wastes both investment and career window.*

## Risk Assessment (Mandatory — Every Signing Evaluation)

Each risk category assessed Low / Medium / High WITH reason. A level without a reason is an invalid output.

| Risk category | What to assess |
|--------------|---------------|
| **Execution** | Can the artist and team ship work on a consistent, commercial timeline? |
| **Financial** | Is the investment case realistic at the expected ceiling tier? Is recoupment plausible? |
| **Reputation** | Are there known controversies, brand risks, or behavioral signals that create label exposure? |
| **Team** | Is the team (manager, attorney) qualified for the development phase being entered? |
| **Legal** | IP ownership clear? Existing deal encumbrances? Distribution agreements that conflict? |
| **Burnout** | Is there evidence of creative or personal overextension? Sustainability of pace? |
| **AI displacement** | Is this artist's primary differentiation replicable by AI-generated content at near-zero cost? If so, how much time does the identity moat actually buy? |
| **Trend dependency** | Is the artist's commercial ceiling dependent on a genre cycle that may close before the development plan completes? |

Each: Low / Medium / High + one-sentence reason. Unsigned = invalid output.

## Required Output Format (8 Elements)

Every signing evaluation must include all eight elements:

1. **Per-pillar assessment** — letter grade + evaluable sub-signals named + NOT EVALUABLE sub-signals named + confidence level
2. **§7 cross-reference** — which dimensions were activated in this evaluation (Commercial Viability, Songwriting Quality, Hook Strength, Production Quality, Vocal Performance, Artist Identity, Radio Potential, Playlist Fit, Sync Potential, International Potential)
3. **Unfair Advantage Assessment** — advantages identified with evidence, or "None identified"
4. **Trajectory assessment** — growth rate, save-rate trend, repeat-listening trend, release consistency; graded and used to determine DEVELOP vs. PURSUE split
5. **PROVISIONAL composite** — weighted numeric, verdict, and verbatim statement: *"PROVISIONAL — unlock condition: ≥30 outcome-checked evaluations in feedback/outcomes/"*
6. **Career ceiling** — Local / National / International / Global Superstar + qualitative confidence + reasons
7. **Risk assessment** — all 8 categories rated Low/Medium/High with reasons
8. **Five-Year Test answer** — explicitly stated with evidence

**Hard gate check:** state whether any gate is triggered before reporting the composite. A triggered gate ends the evaluation at PASS.

**OWNER INPUT PENDING flag:** if Artist Identity & Brand or Execution & Team scored primarily on JUDGED basis without direct working-relationship access, add: *"Identity and Execution assessment based on available materials only — direct relationship signals (responsiveness, financial discipline, professionalism) NOT EVALUABLE at this stage."*
