## What Are Neighbouring Rights

Neighbouring rights are separate from performance royalties (PRO/SOCAN/PRS). They pay the MASTER RECORDING OWNER and FEATURED PERFORMER when a recording is:
- Played on AM/FM radio
- Broadcast on TV
- Played in public spaces (bars, restaurants, hotels, shops)
- Streamed on non-interactive digital radio (Pandora, SiriusXM)

Two separate payments flow:
1. To the FEATURED PERFORMER (the artist who recorded the song)
2. To the MASTER OWNER (whoever paid for the recording)

Independent artists who own their masters collect BOTH.

## Global Neighbouring Rights Societies

### NORTH AMERICA
CANADA: Re:Sound (resound.ca)
- Collects for AM/FM radio, TV, background music, live venues
- Free to register
- Pays both featured performer and master owner separately
- Significant income for artists with Canadian radio play

USA: SoundExchange (soundexchange.com)
- Collects for NON-INTERACTIVE digital services only
- Pandora Radio, SiriusXM, iHeartRadio, webcasters
- Split: 45% featured artist, 50% master owner, 5% non-featured performers
- Register as BOTH featured artist AND sound recording copyright owner
- Free to register

### UNITED KINGDOM
PPL (Phonographic Performance Limited) (ppluk.com)
- Collects for UK radio, TV, and public performance
- PPL International: collects in 30+ countries on behalf of registered performers
- Free to register
- One of the most active neighbouring rights societies globally

## The Cross-Border Stack

A UK artist with music on digital radio globally should collect from:
1. PPL (UK radio and public performance)
2. PPL International (30+ countries)
3. SoundExchange (US digital radio — Pandora, SiriusXM)
4. Re:Sound (Canadian radio if played there)
5. GVL (German radio if played there)
6. SENA (Dutch radio if played there)

Most independent artists collect from zero of these. All are free to register.
