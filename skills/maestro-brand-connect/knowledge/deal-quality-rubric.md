# Brand Partnership Deal Quality Rubric
Version: v1.0 — 8-dimension Deal Quality Score (DQS) for PLMKR brand partnership evaluation

---

## Rubric Scope

This rubric produces a Deal Quality Score (DQS) for a proposed brand partnership, platform deal, or strategic alliance. It is used when evaluating whether a deal is worth doing, assessing proposed terms, or determining a go/no-go recommendation. Scores without stated reasoning are invalid. Output bands describe directional quality — they are never pinned to a specific score target and never used to reverse-engineer dimension scores.

---

## Eight Dimensions (weights sum to 1.00)

### 1. Strategic Value, Fit & Leverage — Weight: 0.18

What it measures: the genuine strategic value this deal adds — access, distribution, data, credibility, exclusivity, and network effects — INDEPENDENT of immediate deal economics. A deal with thin economics that opens a structurally valuable door can score high here; a lucrative deal that delivers no durable strategic position scores low.

Six leverage types to assess independently:
- **Access** — entry to a market, channel, audience segment, or relationship network otherwise unavailable
- **Distribution** — catalog or content reach meaningfully superior to current stack
- **Data** — proprietary, current, decision-differentiating audience or market intelligence (all three conditions must be met)
- **Credibility** — association that shifts how target buyers or partners perceive the enterprise (transfer must flow TO the enterprise)
- **Exclusivity / preferred position** — structural advantage competitors cannot replicate during the term
- **Network effects** — value that compounds as participation grows

Strategic fit test (all three required for genuine fit):
1. Partnership addresses a documented specific gap in the enterprise's reach, capability, or market position
2. Deal's primary output connects directly to that gap
3. Value of closing the gap is estimable, even directionally

Dim-1 / Dim-2 independence is structural: Dim-1 captures leverage only. It is NEVER adjusted upward because Dim-2 economics are strong, and NEVER adjusted downward because Dim-2 economics are thin.

**Anchors (1–10):**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1–2 | No identifiable strategic value beyond the transaction. No leverage type evidenced. |
| 3–4 | Minor adjacency — deal touches a relevant category but creates no durable leverage; replicable easily. |
| 5–6 | Moderate, real strategic value. One named leverage type clearly evidenced; replicable within 12 months by a determined alternative. |
| 7–8 | Strong, differentiated. Two or more named leverage types evidenced; not readily replicable within the relevant window. |
| 9–10 | Exceptional, compounding. Three or more leverage types; competitors structurally excluded during the term. |

### 2. Economic Value & Deal Economics — Weight: 0.16

What it measures: the deal's direct financial quality — whether economics (consideration, revenue share, cost commitments) are favorable relative to comparables, whether the financial model is coherent, and whether the enterprise captures meaningful value under base-case assumptions.

Key economic structures to assess:
- **Upfront capital transfers** — advance, signing bonus, non-recoupable payment
- **Revenue share / royalty** — rate and royalty base definition (effective rate matters more than stated rate)
- **Cost commitments** — bounded vs. unbounded obligations (unbounded commitments reduce Dim-2 quality)
- **Milestone / performance-contingent** — do NOT include at face value in base case; probability-weight them

Financial modeling discipline: assess three cases. A deal evaluated only under optimistic assumptions has not been economically assessed.
- **Base case** — most probable set of assumptions; return should be positive and achievable
- **Downside case** — plausible-adverse scenario; enterprise should survive without operational impairment
- **Upside case** — ceiling scenario; NOT the basis for the go/no-go; labeled separately

Market-rate characterizations are ESTIMATE / NOT QUOTABLE with stated comparable basis and vintage. Every figure in deal economics that is not directly confirmed carries this label inline.

**Anchors (1–10):**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1–2 | Economics are destructive or undefined; deal is net-negative under any plausible scenario. |
| 3–4 | Below-market economics; base-case return thin or speculative; cost commitments unclear. |
| 5–6 | Market-rate economics; base-case return positive and achievable; cost commitments defined and bounded. |
| 7–8 | Above-market economics; base and downside cases modeled; cost commitments capped; no hidden exposure. |
| 9–10 | Best-in-class economics; all three scenarios fully modeled; downside value-preserving; no undisclosed exposure. |

### 3. Partner Quality & Credibility — Weight: 0.14
**HARD GATE — score 1 blocks RECOMMEND regardless of composite.**

What it measures: whether the partner can and will deliver on their commitments. A strong deal with a partner who cannot perform is a liability, not an asset.

Hard gate: partner is disqualifying — cannot or will not deliver, carries unresolvable reputational or financial risk, or has a documented history of non-performance relevant to this deal type → RECOMMEND BLOCKED regardless of composite.

Five due diligence domains:
1. **Track record** — documented comparable deals with verifiable outcomes
2. **Financial health** — ability to sustain obligations through the deal term
3. **Delivery capacity** — operational infrastructure specific to this deal type
4. **Reputation and integrity** — regulatory history, commercial reputation, public-facing integrity
5. **Organizational alignment** — senior-level deal owner with authority, committed operational resources

Critical boundary: "no history" defaults to score 3 (INFERRED — uncertain, not disqualifying). "Bad history" is scored on evidence and may be disqualifying. These are categorically different conditions.

**Anchors (1–10):**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | [HARD GATE] Partner is disqualifying — confirmed inability to deliver, dissolved/shell entity, documented non-performance on comparable deals, or active disqualifying legal/regulatory/reputational issue. RECOMMEND BLOCKED. |
| 3 | Partner credibility substantially uncertain. Limited or no verifiable track record. Financial health unclear. Due diligence not conducted. |
| 5 | Baseline credibility threshold met. 1–2 comparable deals documented. No adverse findings; some due diligence gaps remain. |
| 7 | Well-assessed and credible. ≥3 documented comparable deals with known outcomes. Financial health confirmed. No unresolved issues. |
| 10 | Best-in-class. Extensive, documented track record at comparable scale with measurable positive outcomes. Comprehensive diligence; no adverse findings. |

---

### 4. Deal Structure & Terms — Weight: 0.14

What it measures: whether the deal is structured favorably — term length, exclusivity scope, exit mechanisms, IP ownership, and whether foreclosing terms are absent or mitigated.

Three structural axes (assess independently):
- **Time axis** — term length and review points; over-long terms create structural risk when underlying assumptions may shift
- **Scope axis** — exclusivity (must be precisely scoped to direct deal category), IP ownership (enterprise default: IP created under deal vests in enterprise), licensing scope
- **Optionality axis** — enterprise-exercisable exit rights, renegotiation triggers, MFN provisions

Foreclosing term identification: a provision that permanently restricts core operations, creates unlimited liability, or forecloses a strategically critical option. Test: is it both FATAL (material to core operations) and UNMITIGATED (no contractual protection limits the harm)?

If a foreclosing term is both fatal and unmitigated → Dim-5 gate assessment is triggered.

**Anchors (1–10):**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Foreclosing term present and unmitigated. Perpetual exclusivity, unlimited liability, or no-exit structure without mitigation. |
| 3 | Material structural gaps or imbalances. Exclusivity broader than needed; exit rights narrow or costly; below market standard. |
| 5 | Broadly market-standard structure. Term, exclusivity, and exit rights in line with comparable deal types (ESTIMATE). Minor gaps, none material. |
| 7 | Favorable structure. Term calibrated to value; exclusivity precisely scoped; enterprise-exercisable exit right on reasonable notice; IP ownership favorable. |
| 10 | Best-in-class. All structural elements optimal. Exclusivity minimum viable scope with explicit carve-outs. Multiple enterprise-exercisable exit pathways, low cost. |

---

### 5. Risk & Downside Exposure — Weight: 0.14
**HARD GATE — score 1 blocks RECOMMEND regardless of composite.**

What it measures: whether the enterprise's downside exposure is known, bounded, and mitigated — or whether a fatal, unmitigated risk is present.

Hard gate: fatal, unmitigated exposure present — unlimited liability, active litigation risk existential to the deal, regulatory prohibition, or structural risk to core operations that cannot be mitigated → RECOMMEND BLOCKED regardless of composite.

Risk categories to assess for brand deals:
- **Reputational exposure** — partner association risk; termination right if brand reputation deteriorates
- **Creative control risk** — approval rights that constrain the enterprise's creative integrity
- **Exclusivity concentration** — single-category exclusivity creating dependency
- **Performance penalty risk** — organic engagement is algorithmic and outside the artist's control; penalties on benchmarks the enterprise cannot guarantee are structural risks
- **Payment risk** — front-loading vs. back-loading of cash consideration

Naming a risk as a caveat without a mitigation plan is NOT mitigation. An unmitigated fatal risk triggers Dim-5 = 1 regardless of how thoroughly it is described.

**Anchors (1–10):**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | [HARD GATE] Fatal, unmitigated exposure confirmed. RECOMMEND BLOCKED. |
| 3 | Significant unmitigated risks. Downside exposure not bounded; maximum liability unclear or large. |
| 5 | Risk profile manageable but not fully mitigated. Primary risks identified; one or two lack full mitigation; no fatal exposure. |
| 7 | Well-assessed and substantially mitigated. All material risks identified with mitigation plans. Exposure capped by contract. |
| 10 | Comprehensively assessed and fully mitigated. All risks documented; liability capped; enterprise can absorb full downside without operational impairment. |

---

### 6. Execution Feasibility & Capacity — Weight: 0.10

What it measures: whether the enterprise has the operational capacity and organizational readiness to execute this deal — internal bandwidth, delivery infrastructure, implementation owner, and integration requirements.

Key elements:
- Named implementation owner with confirmed capacity
- Bandwidth impact assessed against current commitments
- Delivery infrastructure operational for this deal type
- Key dependencies resolved

Told-absence of an implementation owner (no one named to execute) is evidence, not an inferred gap — it scores on the rubric anchor, not defaulted to 3.

**Anchors (1–10):**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | No implementation owner. No bandwidth assessment. Infrastructure absent. |
| 5 | Feasible with known gaps. Owner identified; bandwidth assessed; one or two dependencies unresolved. |
| 10 | Fully resourced and ready. Owner confirmed with dedicated capacity. All dependencies resolved. |

---

### 7. Opportunity Cost & Alternatives — Weight: 0.08

What it measures: whether the enterprise has assessed what it is giving up by doing this deal — bandwidth, capital, exclusivity slots, or relationships.

Three opportunity cost components (assess independently):
- **Bandwidth cost** — management attention and operational capacity consumed
- **Exclusivity cost** — expected value of best forgone alternative in any foreclosed category, probability-weighted
- **Capital cost** — return forgone on alternative uses of committed capital

BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement) standard: the enterprise's realistic best alternative if this deal does not close. "Pursue other opportunities" is not a BATNA. A specific named deal structure at ESTIMATE economics with ESTIMATE probability is the operational standard.

**Anchors (1–10):**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | No opportunity cost analysis. No alternatives named. No BATNA. |
| 5 | Basic assessment. 1–2 named alternatives broadly compared. BATNA directionally valued. |
| 7 | Well-assessed. 2–3 alternatives compared on primary criteria. BATNA specific, current, and credible. Exclusivity cost named and accepted or rejected with stated reasoning. |
| 10 | Comprehensive. All material alternatives formally evaluated. Every exclusivity provision assessed against foreclosed alternatives. Decision explicitly premised on this deal being superior. |

---

### 8. Reversibility & Optionality — Weight: 0.06

What it measures: whether the enterprise retains meaningful ability to exit, renegotiate, or pivot — and whether the deal preserves future strategic options or prematurely forecloses them.

Key features of a strong exit right:
- Enterprise-exercisable (not only partner-triggered)
- Reasonable notice period
- Low or no penalty
- Performance-failure triggers included

**Anchors (1–10):**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Deal is effectively irreversible. No enterprise-exercisable exit. Perpetual or indefinite term. Future categories foreclosed. |
| 5 | Moderate reversibility. Enterprise exit right present with reasonable notice. Review point defined. |
| 10 | Maximum optionality. Multiple enterprise-exercisable exit pathways; minimum viable exclusivity scope with carve-outs; renegotiation triggers bilateral and defined. |

## Scoring Rules

1. Score each dimension independently before combining. Do not let overall deal impression drive individual scores.
2. Every score is a claim: state the evidence, confidence level (high / medium / low), and key assumption.
3. **Confidence tagging (mandatory):** each dimension carries evidence type (observed / told / inferred), confidence level, and the assumption that, if wrong, would change the score by ≥1 point.

### Composite Score Formula

```
DQS = (SV×0.18) + (EV×0.16) + (PQ×0.14) + (DS×0.14) + (RE×0.14) + (EF×0.10) + (OC×0.08) + (RO×0.06)

SV = Strategic Value, Fit & Leverage
EV = Economic Value & Deal Economics
PQ = Partner Quality & Credibility
DS = Deal Structure & Terms
RE = Risk & Downside Exposure
EF = Execution Feasibility & Capacity
OC = Opportunity Cost & Alternatives
RO = Reversibility & Optionality

Weights sum: 0.18 + 0.16 + 0.14 + 0.14 + 0.14 + 0.10 + 0.08 + 0.06 = 1.00
```

### Output Bands

| Score | Band | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| 8.0–10.0 | Green — RECOMMEND | Deal is sound across dimensions; proceed to documentation and close |
| 6.0–7.9 | Yellow — CONDITIONAL | Move forward while resolving 1–2 gap dimensions before commitment |
| 4.0–5.9 | Amber — NOT READY | Resolve material gaps before executing; conditional interest only |
| 1.0–3.9 | Red — NOT VIABLE | Hard gaps (typically Partner Quality or Risk Exposure) require remediation before re-evaluation |

### Hard Gates

| Gate | Trigger | Effect |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Partner Quality Gate** | Partner Quality & Credibility (Dim-3) = 1 | RECOMMEND blocked; verdict is NOT VIABLE regardless of composite |
| **Risk & Exposure Gate** | Risk & Downside Exposure (Dim-5) = 1 | RECOMMEND blocked; verdict is NOT VIABLE regardless of composite |

Gates are binary (blocked / clear). Score 3 on either dimension means uncertain or unmitigated but not disqualifying — only confirmed score 1 triggers a block.

### Provisional Label

Composite is labeled PROVISIONAL when any dimension must be scored INFERRED (no evidence → defaults to 3). The INFERRED dimension's contribution to the composite is shown separately.

Unlock condition for removing PROVISIONAL: all 8 dimensions scored with ≥1 piece of direct evidence cited. Dimensions without evidence remain INFERRED at 3; only told-absence of a required element (e.g., no implementation owner named) is evidence and is scored on its anchor — it is NOT labeled INFERRED and NOT defaulted to 3.
