# Partner Due Diligence
Version: v1.0 — PLMKR Brand Connect

Note: Partner financial health characterizations and credibility ratings are ESTIMATE / NOT QUOTABLE unless sourced from a specific verified assessment. Due diligence conclusions must carry evidence type (OBSERVED / TOLD / INFERRED), confidence level, and the assumption whose failure would change the Dim-3 score by ≥1 point.

---

## The Purpose of Partner Due Diligence

Partner due diligence determines whether the brand can and will do what the deal requires. The question "is this deal worth doing?" is only answerable after "can this partner deliver on their side of the deal?" is settled. A structurally attractive deal with a brand that cannot perform is not an asset — it is a commitment to a counterparty who will consume the artist's time, reputation, and creative energy before the deal is complete.

Due diligence produces three practical outputs: a partner credibility score for Dim-3; identification of specific diligence gaps and whether they are tolerable given the deal's scope; and a recommendation on whether additional diligence is needed before the artist commits.

---

## The Five Due Diligence Domains

### Domain 1: Track Record

Has the brand successfully delivered on comparable partnerships, with comparable artists, at comparable scale? Track record is the most predictive forward indicator of delivery performance.

A relevant track record is: **comparable** (the partnerships in the brand's history are similar in type, scope, and complexity to the deal under evaluation); **recent** (the track record is current — a historical track record from five or more years ago may not reflect the brand's current organizational state); and **verifiable** (the track record can be confirmed through direct evidence — reference artists, public campaign announcements, documented outcomes — not solely the brand's self-report).

Track record gaps are treated differently depending on their source. A new brand entering artist partnerships has no track record by definition — this scores at Dim-3 baseline 3 (INFERRED, not evidence of non-performance). A brand with a documented history of non-payment, creative disputes, or morality clause invocations on prior artist deals is told-absence: the track record is assessable, and it scores below baseline.

### Domain 2: Financial Health

Can the brand sustain their contractual payment obligations through the deal term? Financial health assessment covers: whether the brand can meet payment commitments on schedule; whether the brand is financially stable enough to complete a multi-year deal without cash flow disruption; and whether the brand is controlled by a parent entity whose financial decisions could unilaterally affect the deal.

Financial health is assessed relative to the deal term, not abstractly. A brand that is financially adequate for a 90-day campaign may be uncertain for a 3-year ambassador program.

### Domain 3: Delivery Capacity

Does the brand have the organizational infrastructure, approvals process, and internal coordination to deliver on this specific deal? Delivery capacity is deal-type specific — a brand with a strong track record in product placement may lack the internal creative coordination infrastructure for a co-branded content series.

Specifically assess: what internal sign-off chain does creative content pass through before approval? What is the typical timeline from creative submission to approval, based on reference artist experiences? Who is the named deal owner on the brand's side and do they have the authority to approve deliverables without additional escalation?

### Domain 4: Brand Reputation and Values Alignment

Does the artist's association with this brand create reputational risk, or does the brand's reputation transfer positively? Reputation assessment covers: whether the brand has any active or recent public controversies; whether the brand's consumer positioning is compatible with the artist's values and public image; and whether the brand's category carries inherent reputational risk (regulatory exposure, consumer activism targeting, contested marketing practices).

A brand in an active regulatory investigation, under consumer boycott, or whose marketing practices have been publicly criticized is a higher-risk association than a brand with a clean and established reputation. These risks are not disqualifying on their own — they require assessment against the specific artist's positioning and audience values.

### Domain 5: Organizational Alignment

Is the brand's team genuinely committed to this deal, or is the partnership peripheral to their priorities? Organizational alignment failure is the most underdiagnosed cause of partnership underperformance. A deal championed by an enthusiastic brand marketing team can fail when the legal or approvals team treats it as a low-priority item.

Assess alignment at both the deal-sponsorship level (is there a named senior decision-maker on the brand's side who has authority and apparent intent to execute?) and the operational-delivery level (does the brand have creative, legal, and production resources allocated to this specific partnership?).

## Credibility Scoring: The Disqualifying vs. Uncertain Boundary

The most consequential due diligence decision is the distinction between a **disqualifying partner** (Dim-3 = 1, hard gate fires) and an **uncertain partner** (Dim-3 = 3, INFERRED baseline).

A **disqualifying condition** is one where the evidence positively establishes that the brand cannot or will not perform, or that engaging with this brand creates a material reputation risk to the artist. Disqualifying conditions include: documented history of non-payment to prior artist partners; active brand values crisis that directly conflicts with the artist's public positioning; regulatory action that makes the partnership legally problematic; and a shell or pass-through entity with no operational infrastructure.

An **uncertain condition** is one where the artist lacks sufficient evidence to assess the brand's credibility with confidence — not where the evidence is negative, but where evidence is absent. A brand new to artist partnerships with limited public track record is uncertain, not disqualifying. Proceeding with an uncertain partner is a risk management decision (acceptable or not depending on the deal's structure and stakes), not a categorical prohibition.

The practical test: can the artist's team state a specific, evidence-based reason why the brand cannot or will not deliver? If yes, and if that reason is directly relevant to this deal type, the brand is potentially disqualifying and the hard gate must be explicitly evaluated. If the team can only state that they do not have enough information, the brand is uncertain and Dim-3 scores at 3 baseline.

## Due Diligence Gaps: Tolerable vs. Requiring Resolution

**Tolerable gaps** are those where: the missing information is unlikely to change the overall credibility assessment if obtained; the deal structure provides adequate protection against the risk the gap represents; or the cost of obtaining the information exceeds the expected value of the risk reduction.

**Gaps requiring resolution** are those where: the missing information is directly load-bearing — obtaining it could either confirm a disqualifying condition or meaningfully raise confidence; the deal's risk exposure is high enough that proceeding without the information is not defensible; or the brand's unwillingness to provide the information is itself a signal (a brand that refuses to provide references, payment history confirmation, or documentation of their approval process is providing evidence of something the artist should weight).

## Music Artist Brand Partnership Due Diligence Patterns

### Major Consumer Brands (Established Global or National)

Established brands operating at scale provide the highest baseline of institutional credibility: financial health is typically observable through parent company public reporting; track records in commercial partnerships are extensive; and organizational infrastructure is demonstrated at scale. The credibility risks at this level are not financial health or delivery capacity but **creative approval complexity** and **organizational alignment**. A major brand with six internal approval layers and a 15-business-day turnaround standard has a delivery infrastructure problem even if their financial standing is impeccable. Diligence at this level focuses on creative approval process and organizational alignment, not financial verification.

### Emerging and Challenger Brands

Emerging brand partners range from well-capitalized brands with documented music partnership history to newer entrants with thin capitalization and limited track record. The credibility assessment must not treat "emerging brand" as a homogeneous class. Specific diligence covers: funding runway relative to the deal term (particularly for multi-year commitments); payment track record from any prior creative partnerships; and internal creative coordination capability. A brand offering an ambassador deal that requires multi-year payment commitments whose funding depends on a revenue projection rather than current liquidity is a financial health concern regardless of their creative vision.

### Regional and Local Brands

Regional brands operating in the artist's primary market offer high relevance and potential audience alignment but often have limited organizational infrastructure for formal brand partnership execution. Diligence priorities: payment reliability (cash flow from smaller brands can be lumpy); approval process reality (small brands may have fewer formal approval layers but also fewer resources to manage complex creative deliverables); and deal documentation sophistication (smaller brands may offer informal term sheets that require LEX-CIPHER review before execution to ensure adequate protections are in place).

## Dim-3 Anchors for Artist Brand Partnerships

**Score 1 (HARD GATE — disqualifying):** Documented pattern of non-payment to prior artist partners; active brand controversy that directly conflicts with artist's core public values or key audience's values; regulatory action directly implicating the deal structure; shell entity status with no operational infrastructure and no parent guarantee. RECOMMEND BLOCKED.

**Score 3 (uncertain; significant gaps):** New-to-market brand with no prior music artist partnership track record; no reference artists or campaigns available for assessment; financial health not assessed; organizational alignment not confirmed. Artist is proceeding without adequate credibility confidence.

**Score 5 (baseline credibility; some gaps):** 1–2 confirmed prior music artist partnerships with acceptable outcomes; payment track record appears adequate but not independently verified; no known reputation issues; approval process complexity not fully assessed.

**Score 7 (well-assessed; solid track record):** 3+ prior artist partnerships with documented positive outcomes; payment track record confirmed through references; brand reputation compatible with artist values; approval process timeline assessed and workable; no adverse findings.

**Score 10 (best-in-class):** Extensive, documented track record with music artists at comparable scale; payment reliability is category-defining; brand reputation actively enhances artist credibility; approval infrastructure is purpose-built for artist partnerships and confirmed through reference assessment; organizational alignment confirmed at both deal-sponsorship and operational-delivery levels; no adverse findings of any materiality.
