# Brand Connect — Deal Quality Score (DQS) Rubric
Version: v1.0 — PLMKR Brand Connect scoring standard

---

## Rubric Scope

The Deal Quality Score (DQS) evaluates a proposed brand partnership, sponsorship deal, or commercial association for a PLMKR artist. It produces a composite score and a band verdict. Scores without stated reasoning and evidence context are invalid — every dimension requires the evidence type, confidence level, and the key assumption whose failure would change the score by ≥1 point.

---

## Dimensions (8 — weights sum to 1.00)

### 1. Strategic Value, Fit & Leverage
**Weight: 0.18**

What it measures: the genuine strategic value this deal adds to the artist's career — audience access, credibility transfer, distribution reach, data about fans, exclusivity positioning, and network effects — INDEPENDENT of immediate deal economics. A thin-margin deal that meaningfully repositions the artist's brand can score high here; a lucrative deal with a partner whose audience is irrelevant scores low.

Key factors: Does this brand give the artist access to an audience they cannot reach efficiently through their own channels? Does the brand's credibility and cultural positioning transfer meaningfully to the artist's image? Is there a preferred or exclusive commercial position the deal creates? Does the deal open distribution infrastructure — retail presence, marketing spend, global reach — the artist cannot self-fund?

Anchors:
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **1** | No identifiable strategic value beyond the immediate cash. Brand's audience is demographically unrelated to the artist's target listener. No credibility transfer, no distribution advantage, no access leverage. Artist position is unchanged if deal lapses. |
| **3** | Minor adjacency — brand is in a relevant category but creates no durable leverage. Audience overlap is general rather than specific. Credibility transfer is marginal or neutral. No exclusivity or preferred position created. |
| **5** | Moderate, real strategic value. Brand provides a meaningful audience reach or credibility advantage the artist does not already hold. One named leverage type (access, credibility, distribution) is clearly evidenced. Advantage is real but replicable through an alternative brand partner within 12 months. |
| **7** | Strong strategic value. Brand provides access or credibility that is differentiated and not readily replicable. Two or more named leverage types are evidenced. Brand aligns directly with the artist's genre positioning and growth target audience. Co-marketing infrastructure is confirmed and committed. |
| **10** | Exceptional, durable strategic leverage. Brand gives the artist a structurally privileged market position — exclusive category association, co-marketing infrastructure at scale, and data about consumer behavior the artist cannot access independently. Three or more named leverage types evidenced. Deal materially shifts the artist's commercial positioning in a measurable direction. |

### 2. Economic Value & Deal Economics
**Weight: 0.16**

What it measures: the deal's direct financial quality — whether cash consideration, in-kind value, co-marketing investment, and revenue participation are favorable relative to comparables, and whether the net economics make sense after production costs and obligations.

Key factors: Is the total consideration (cash + in-kind + co-marketing) competitive for this deal type and artist profile? Are cost commitments bounded and known? Is the payment schedule artist-protective (front-loaded vs. back-loaded)? Is there a kill fee if the brand cancels?

Anchors:
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **1** | Economics are clearly unfavorable or undefined. Cash consideration is absent or below production cost. Cost commitments are unbounded. No kill fee or payment guarantee. Deal is economically destructive under any scenario. |
| **3** | Below-market economics. Consideration is directionally unfavorable vs. comparable deals at this artist's reach. Financial model exists in outline only. One or more cost commitments are unclear or unbounded. Payment is disproportionately backend-weighted. |
| **5** | Market-rate economics. Total consideration is broadly in line with comparable deals for this deal type and artist profile (ESTIMATE, stated basis). Base-case return is positive after stated production costs. Kill fee or payment protection is present or standard for this deal class. |
| **7** | Above-market economics. Consideration is favorable vs. comparables (ESTIMATE). At least 50% upfront. Kill fee confirmed. No unbounded cost commitments. Upside scenario (revenue participation or renewal bonus) is credible and modeled separately from base case. |
| **10** | Strongly favorable economics, fully modeled. Consideration is best-in-class for this deal type and artist profile. Payment structure is front-loaded with a defined kill fee. All cost commitments are capped by contract. Upside scenario confirmed and negotiated. No undisclosed financial exposure. |

---

### 3. Partner Quality & Credibility
**Weight: 0.14**
**HARD GATE — score 1 blocks RECOMMEND regardless of composite.**

What it measures: whether the brand partner is genuinely credible, financially stable, and capable of delivering on their commercial commitments. A structurally attractive deal with a partner who cannot or will not deliver is not a deal — it is a reputational liability.

Hard gate: Partner is disqualifying — documented history of deal non-performance, active reputational crisis that conflicts with artist values, regulatory action, or financial distress that makes advance/payment delivery uncertain → RECOMMEND BLOCKED regardless of composite.

Key factors: Does the brand have a documented track record of artist partnerships? Is the brand's payment history reliable? Is the brand's public standing compatible with the artist's values? Does the brand have the organizational infrastructure to execute the creative deliverables?

Anchors:
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **1** | [HARD GATE] Partner is disqualifying. Active brand reputational crisis directly conflicting with artist values. Documented history of non-payment to prior artist partners. Financial distress making payment delivery implausible. OR brand entity is a shell with no operational infrastructure. RECOMMEND BLOCKED. |
| **3** | Partner credibility substantially uncertain. No verifiable prior music artist partnership track record. Payment reliability not assessed. Reputation carries unresolved questions. Delivery infrastructure unconfirmed. |
| **5** | Baseline credibility. 1–2 confirmed prior music artist partnerships with acceptable outcomes. Payment reliability appears adequate. No known material reputational issues. Approval process complexity not fully assessed. |
| **7** | Credible and well-assessed. 3+ documented prior artist partnerships with positive outcomes. Payment track record confirmed clean through references. Brand reputation is solid and compatible with artist values. Approval process complexity assessed against realistic delivery timelines. |
| **10** | Best-in-class. Extensive, documented track record with music artists. Payment reliability is category-defining. Brand reputation is strong and actively enhances artist credibility. Approval infrastructure is purpose-built and demonstrated. No adverse findings. |

---

### 4. Deal Structure & Terms
**Weight: 0.14**

What it measures: whether the deal is structured artist-protectively — term length, exclusivity scope, exit mechanisms, IP ownership, approval rights, and whether foreclosing terms are absent or mitigated.

Key factors: Is the exclusivity category scoped correctly? Are creative approval timelines defined and reasonable? Is there an artist-exercisable exit right? Is category exclusivity too broad? Are IP rights on content created for the deal clearly defined?

Anchors:
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **1** | Deal structure contains a foreclosing term. Category exclusivity is over-broad, covering multiple revenue streams or genres beyond the deal's direct subject. Unlimited approval rights or unlimited liability provision is present and unmitigated. No artist-exercisable exit right. |
| **3** | Structure has material gaps. Exclusivity broader than needed. Exit rights narrow or partner-only. Creative approval timeline is undefined or unreasonably long. IP ownership of created content is ambiguous. One non-standard term disadvantages the artist. |
| **5** | Market-standard structure. Term, exclusivity, and exit rights are broadly in line with comparable deals. Approval timeline is defined. IP ownership of created content is acceptable. No foreclosing terms present. Minor structural gaps exist but none are material. |
| **7** | Favorable structure. Category exclusivity precisely scoped to the deal's subject. Artist-exercisable exit right on reasonable notice. Creative approval has defined rounds and turnaround SLAs. IP ownership clearly artist-protective. Kill fee confirmed. |
| **10** | Best-in-class structure. All structural elements optimally configured. Exclusivity is minimum viable scope with explicit carve-outs for non-competing categories. Exit provisions are flexible, low-cost, and artist-triggered. All IP created under the deal vests clearly in the artist. Reviewed and confirmed by LEX-CIPHER. |

---

### 5. Risk & Downside Exposure
**Weight: 0.14**
**HARD GATE — score 1 blocks RECOMMEND regardless of composite.**

What it measures: whether the artist's downside exposure from this deal is known, bounded, and mitigated — or whether a fatal, unmitigated risk is present that makes the deal inadvisable regardless of upside.

Hard gate: Fatal, unmitigated exposure — morality clause with undefined termination triggers, unlimited liability provision, active regulatory prohibition, or structural reputational risk to the artist's core market that cannot be mitigated → RECOMMEND BLOCKED regardless of composite.

Key factors: Are reputational risks from brand association assessed? Are morality clauses with defined (not vague) termination triggers? Is liability capped? Are there category exclusivity provisions that block other revenue streams in ways that exceed the deal's value?

Anchors:
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **1** | [HARD GATE] Fatal, unmitigated exposure. Morality clause with vague, unlimited termination triggers and no cure period. Unlimited liability with no cap. Brand is in an active values crisis that directly conflicts with artist's public positioning. Deal forecloses core revenue streams without adequate compensation. RECOMMEND BLOCKED. |
| **3** | Significant unmitigated risks. Morality clause undefined. Multiple material risks without mitigation plans. Downside exposure not bounded. Reputational risk from brand association is elevated and unassessed. Category exclusivity closes other revenue streams at a cost exceeding deal value. |
| **5** | Risk profile manageable but not fully mitigated. Primary risks identified and named. Morality clause is present but scoped to specific defined triggers. Liability is directionally bounded. One or two material risks lack a full mitigation plan. No fatal exposure. |
| **7** | Risk profile well-assessed and substantially mitigated. All material risks identified with mitigation plans. Morality clause is specifically scoped with a cure period. Liability is contractually capped. Category exclusivity is proportionate to deal value. No active brand controversy. |
| **10** | Risk profile comprehensively assessed and fully mitigated. All material and foreseeable risks addressed with documented mitigation. Morality clause has specific, defined triggers and a cure period. Liability capped at a level the artist can absorb. No reputational risk. Artist can absorb the full downside scenario without operational impairment. |

---

### 6. Execution Feasibility & Capacity
**Weight: 0.10**

What it measures: whether the artist has the operational capacity and organizational readiness to execute the deal's creative deliverables — content production bandwidth, team capacity, and platform infrastructure.

Key factors: Has a production plan been identified? Does the artist have the content creation bandwidth to deliver on the timeline? Are dependencies (photography, video production, social scheduling) resolved?

Anchors:
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **1** | No production plan. Delivery infrastructure for this deal type does not exist. Key dependencies (photographer, video team, content calendar) are unresolved with no timeline. |
| **3** | Execution uncertain. Production capacity tentatively assessed. Bandwidth impact not evaluated against existing commitments. One or more dependencies unresolved. |
| **5** | Execution feasible with known gaps. Content production capacity is sufficient under base-case timeline assumptions. One or two dependencies need resolution but plans are in place. |
| **7** | Execution feasible and well-planned. Content production plan confirmed with dedicated capacity. Bandwidth impact assessed and absorbed without degrading other artist commitments. All dependencies identified and plans in place. |
| **10** | Execution fully resourced. Production plan confirmed and detailed. Bandwidth impact is zero incremental strain. All dependencies resolved and documented. Full execution plan exists with owner sign-off. |

---

### 7. Opportunity Cost & Alternatives
**Weight: 0.08**

What it measures: whether the artist has assessed what this deal forecloses — category exclusivity slots, bandwidth, and alternative brand relationships — and whether alternatives have been evaluated.

Key factors: Are alternative brand partnerships in the same category identified and assessed? Is the opportunity cost of category exclusivity stated explicitly? Is there a best alternative to this negotiated agreement (BATNA) identified?

Anchors:
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **1** | No opportunity cost analysis conducted. Alternative brands in the same category not identified. Artist is proceeding without understanding what is foreclosed. No BATNA identified. |
| **3** | Opportunity cost noted but not analyzed. "Other brands exist" acknowledged without identifying or evaluating them. BATNA vague or unstated. Category exclusivity consequence acknowledged without naming the alternatives it closes. |
| **5** | Opportunity cost assessed at a basic level. 1–2 named alternatives identified and broadly compared. BATNA identified. The most significant foreclosed opportunity is named and directionally valued. |
| **7** | Opportunity cost well-assessed. 2–3 named alternatives identified and compared. BATNA is specific and credible. Category exclusivity cost quantified directionally. The most significant foreclosed opportunity is explicitly named, valued, and accepted or rejected with stated reasoning. |
| **10** | Opportunity cost comprehensively assessed. All material alternatives in the same category formally evaluated on the same DQS criteria. BATNA is specific, current, and credible. Every exclusivity provision assessed against its foreclosed alternatives. Decision to proceed explicitly premised on this deal being superior to identified alternatives. |

---

### 8. Reversibility & Optionality
**Weight: 0.06**

What it measures: whether the artist retains meaningful ability to exit, renegotiate, or pivot — and whether the deal preserves future commercial options or prematurely forecloses them.

Key factors: Are exit rights artist-exercisable (not just brand-exercisable)? Is there a kill fee if the brand cancels? Are renewal rights mutual? Does the deal preserve the artist's ability to work with other brands in adjacent categories?

Anchors:
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **1** | Deal is effectively irreversible. No artist-exercisable exit right. No kill fee. Perpetual or indefinite term with no review point. Category exclusivity crowds out the artist's ability to pursue future brand opportunities in adjacent categories for the foreseeable future. |
| **3** | Limited reversibility. Exit right exists but is costly, time-constrained, or brand-triggered only. Term is long with no interim review. Category exclusivity is broad and reduces future flexibility materially. |
| **5** | Moderate reversibility. Artist-exercisable exit right present with reasonable notice. Term includes a review point. Category scope is bounded enough to preserve some future brand categories. |
| **7** | Good reversibility and optionality. Exit right is low-cost and artist-exercisable on reasonable notice. Kill fee confirmed. Term includes review points. Future brand categories explicitly preserved. |
| **10** | Maximum optionality. Multiple artist-exercisable exit pathways, each with low cost and short notice. Kill fee confirmed. Category exclusivity is minimum viable scope — every non-competing category is carved out. Term is modest with early review rights. Deal structure is designed to preserve rather than foreclose future commercial options. |

## Scoring Rules

1. Score each dimension independently before combining — do not let an overall impression drive individual scores.
2. Each score is a claim: state the evidence, the confidence level (high / medium / low), evidence type (observed / told / inferred), and the key assumption that, if wrong, would change the score by ≥1 point.
3. Do not average when hard-gate dimensions fail. If Partner Quality & Credibility (Dim-3) = 1 or Risk & Downside Exposure (Dim-5) = 1, the deal is not recommendable regardless of composite.
4. Inferred vs. told-absence: a dimension without evidence defaults to 3, labeled INFERRED. Told-absence of a required element (e.g., no kill fee confirmed, no exit right included) is evidence scored on its anchor — NOT defaulted to 3, NOT labeled INFERRED.

## Composite Score Formula

```
DQS = (SV × 0.18) + (EV × 0.16) + (PQ × 0.14) + (DS × 0.14) + (RE × 0.14) + (EF × 0.10) + (OC × 0.08) + (RO × 0.06)

Where:
  SV = Strategic Value, Fit & Leverage score (1–10)
  EV = Economic Value & Deal Economics score (1–10)
  PQ = Partner Quality & Credibility score (1–10)
  DS = Deal Structure & Terms score (1–10)
  RE = Risk & Downside Exposure score (1–10)
  EF = Execution Feasibility & Capacity score (1–10)
  OC = Opportunity Cost & Alternatives score (1–10)
  RO = Reversibility & Optionality score (1–10)

Weights sum: 0.18 + 0.16 + 0.14 + 0.14 + 0.14 + 0.10 + 0.08 + 0.06 = 1.00
```

**Output bands:**
| Score | Band | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| 8.0–10.0 | Green — RECOMMEND | Deal is sound across dimensions; proceed to documentation and close |
| 6.0–7.9 | Yellow — CONDITIONAL | Move forward while resolving 1–2 gap dimensions before commitment |
| 4.0–5.9 | Amber — NOT READY | Resolve material gaps before executing; conditional interest only |
| 1.0–3.9 | Red — NOT VIABLE | Hard gaps require remediation before re-evaluation |

**Composite is PROVISIONAL until 30+ outcome-checked deal evaluations have been logged in the feedback outcomes file. Label all composites PROVISIONAL until that threshold is met.**

## Hard Gates

| Gate | Trigger | Blocked verdict |
|------|---------|----------------|
| **Partner Quality Gate** | Partner Quality & Credibility (Dim-3) = 1 | RECOMMEND verdict. Deal is NOT VIABLE with this partner. |
| **Risk & Exposure Gate** | Risk & Downside Exposure (Dim-5) = 1 | RECOMMEND verdict. Deal carries a FATAL, UNMITIGATED EXPOSURE. |

Gates are binary. A score of 3 on Partner Quality means the partner is uncertain but not disqualifying — only a score of 1 triggers the block. When a hard gate fires: (1) state the gate trigger explicitly, (2) identify the specific condition that triggered it, (3) confirm that no composite score overrides the gate, (4) state the required remediation path.
