## Global Grants Database

### CANADA — Most Comprehensive Music Funding System Globally

FACTOR (Foundation Assisting Canadian Talent on Recordings):
- Juried Sound Recording: up to $10,000 (emerging), $25,000 (established)
- Artist Development: up to $2,000
- Live Performance Domestic: up to $7,500
- Live Performance International: up to $15,000
- Marketing & Promotion: up to $25,000
- Music Video: up to $20,000
- Eligibility: Canadian citizen/PR, SOCAN member, Canadian distributor
- Apply: factorcanada.ca

Canada Council for the Arts:
- Explore and Create: up to $60,000
- Touring and Residencies: domestic and international
- Professional Development
- Apply: canadacouncil.ca

Ontario Creates:
- Sound Recording
- Marketing and Export
- Music Futures (emerging)
- Apply: ontariocreates.ca

Ontario Arts Council (OAC):
- Recording grants
- Touring grants
- Rolling deadlines on many programs
- Apply: arts.on.ca

Toronto Arts Council (TAC):
- Project grants for Toronto-based artists
- Multiple rounds yearly
- Apply: torontoartscouncil.org

SOCAN Foundation:
- Professional Development: up to $1,500
- Travel Assistance: up to $1,000
- Requires active SOCAN membership
- Apply: socanfoundation.ca

GRANT STACKING (Canada): Combine federal + provincial + municipal for same project
Example: FACTOR ($10K) + OAC ($5K) + TAC ($5K) = $20K for one recording project

## Grant Application Best Practices — Universal

WHAT WINS:
- Clear project description with specific deliverables
- Realistic and detailed budget
- Strong career evidence (streaming data, press, previous releases)
- Defined marketing plan with specific targets
- Professional bio and supporting materials
- Applications submitted well before deadline

WHAT GETS REJECTED:
- Vague project descriptions
- Budget inconsistencies
- Missing supporting materials
- Applying without required eligibility (no PRO membership, wrong nationality)
- Late previous grant reports (for repeat applicants)

## Tier-Aware Response Depth

GOLD: Easiest wins first — SOCAN Foundation, local arts council, smallest accessible grants. Explain that grants are non-repayable. One grant at a time. Tone: encouraging, educational.

PLATINUM: Full FACTOR awareness, grant stacking strategy, budget formatting, what winning applications look like. Tone: strategic, deadline-driven.

DIAMOND: Maximum grant stacking across all levels simultaneously, Canada Council large grants, international export funding, building application track record for larger amounts. Tone: portfolio-level, maximum extraction.
