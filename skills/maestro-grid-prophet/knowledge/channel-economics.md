## GENERAL FRAMEWORKS

### Channel Economics Logic
Every marketing channel has three cost dimensions and one outcome dimension:

**Three cost dimensions:**
1. **Acquisition cost (CPM/CPC/CPF):** what it costs to reach, click, or acquire per unit of platform metric. Platform-published averages are broad; industry-vertical averages are more useful; your own historical data is the only reliable baseline.
2. **Conversion cost (CPA):** the cost to produce one measurable downstream action (email subscribe, pre-save, ticket purchase). This is the number that matters, not CPM.
3. **Latency cost:** how long before results are observable — paid channels report in 24–72h; PR/editorial reports in weeks; earned community compounds over 12+ months.

**Outcome dimension:**
- **LTV-to-CAC ratio:** how much lifetime value does one acquired fan generate vs. what it cost to acquire them. A channel with high CPA but high LTV fans (e.g., email opt-in, Discord member) is often more valuable than a channel with low CPA but transient fans (e.g., viral impression).

### Budget Allocation Decision Matrix
Before allocating budget to any channel, answer four questions:
1. **Is the audience there?** Confirm target demographic is present on this platform at scale in this territory.
2. **Is our content format credible there?** Match creative capability to platform culture.
3. **Can we measure the outcome?** If attribution cannot link this spend to the primary campaign KPI, the spend belongs in PR/earned budget — not performance budget.
4. **Is this the right timing?** Paid spend on a channel before earned content exists to amplify wastes budget.

### Creative Testing Logic
- Test one variable at a time: hook type, visual format, call-to-action, audience segment — never two simultaneously.
- Minimum test duration: 3 days for paid social (algorithm learning phase); 1 week for organic content performance read.
- Decision threshold: a creative needs 2x the engagement rate of the control before it qualifies as a "winner" — 1.1x is noise.
- Iteration rule: kill the bottom 20% of creatives after the first week; scale the top 20% with additional budget; hold the middle 60% for format variation tests.
- Budget discipline: never move more than 20% of campaign budget into a single creative variant before the test window closes.

## MUSIC MODULES

### DSP Platform Economics & Strategy

**Spotify:**
- Editorial discovery surface: Release Radar (followers), Discover Weekly (algorithm), editorial playlists (pitch-dependent). The editorial pitch surface is the highest-value non-paid channel for a new release.
- Algorithmic feeds are driven by: saves-to-streams ratio (Day 1 target >12% ESTIMATE), completion rate vs. genre average, adds to user-created playlists, skip rate. Algorithms cannot be gamed; they can only be fed quality signals.
- Spotify Marquee and Showcase are paid placements — CPL (cost-per-listener) benchmarks vary by genre and territory; they serve the artist's active listener and follower base, not cold prospects. Highest ROI use: catalog campaigns, pre-save conversion, re-engagement of lapsed listeners. Not a fan-acquisition tool.
- Canvas (looping video) measurably improves completion rate in certain formats (electronic, visual artists) — ESTIMATE based on Spotify internal data.
- Apple Music Lyrics Translation (iOS 27, fall 2026): expands to 7 new language pairs. Catalog with Korean, Japanese, French, or German lyric content has a new Apple Music discovery surface for cross-language audiences. Apple editorial pitches in these territory corridors should be prioritized for fall 2026 releases. Source: Apple WWDC 2026 (Tier A).

**TikTok:**
- Algorithm: completion rate and reshare rate are the primary ranking signals. Follower count is nearly irrelevant for discovery.
- Sound discovery: tracks that become "official sounds" or that trend organically are the primary music acquisition vector. Organic trend > paid promotion.
- Paid (Spark Ads): boosts existing organic content. A Spark Ad on content with a 2–3% engagement rate will not perform; a Spark Ad on content already at 8–12% engagement rate can produce meaningful fan acquisition (ESTIMATE). Boost only what is working.
- Creative format norms for music: first 3 seconds must hook without context. The track's most distinctive 15-second segment — not necessarily the hook — should be audible immediately.

**Instagram/Meta:**
- Primary use for music: Reels discovery (algorithm similar to TikTok — completion and reshare driven), Stories for community and behind-the-scenes, paid prospecting/retargeting via Meta Ads Manager.
- Reels organic reach: artists with consistent posting cadence and high-completion content maintain viable reach. Cadence > individual post quality for algorithmic health.
- Meta paid: most efficient for warm audiences (retargeting people who have visited the artist's website, watched ≥50% of a prior video, or are lookalikes of email subscribers). Cold prospecting CPM is high for music; use warm retargeting primarily.

**YouTube:**
- Primary use: catalog monetization (long-tail streaming, AdSense), official audio/video destination, live content home.
- For breaking/emerging artists: highest cost-per-fan of any major platform; not prioritized for fan acquisition unless there is already a content flywheel (consistent video output).
- Territory note: YouTube is the primary streaming platform in several emerging markets where Spotify/Apple penetration is lower. For campaigns targeting LatAm, SEA, or India, YouTube's role in channel mix expands accordingly.

### Music Ad-Creative Norms
These norms apply to paid placements for music releases. Creative that violates the norms is flagged with the specific risk.

**Platform-specific format rules:**
- *TikTok/Reels:* 9:16 vertical, ≤15 seconds for discovery ads. First 3 seconds must contain the audio hook and a visual that does not require context. Captions optional but help completion. No call-to-action overlay until final 2 seconds — early CTAs hurt completion rate.
- *Spotify audio ads:* 30 seconds, voice-first. One message, one action. Do not attempt complex narrative.
- *Meta (Instagram/Facebook) video:* 4:5 or 1:1 for feed; 9:16 for stories. First 3 seconds must work without audio (70%+ of feed video is watched silently). Captions or text overlay required.
- *YouTube pre-roll:* first 5 seconds must earn the skip. For music releases, show the artist doing something; a lyric video playing is a skip signal.

**Creative testing priority for music releases:**
1. Hook type: lyric reveal vs. behind-the-scenes vs. performance clip vs. fan reaction
2. Audio length: 7-second clip vs. 15-second clip (both featuring the same hook section)
3. Audience segment: core-fan lookalike vs. genre-adjacent-artist lookalike vs. broad genre interest
4. CTA copy: "Listen now" vs. "Save this" vs. "Add to playlist" (each activates different algorithmic signals)

### PR & Pitch Engine Norms (music)
**Press release structure (music-specific):**
Lead with the story, not the music. The headline is the cultural angle, not the title. A press release that opens with "Artist X releases new single Y" is immediately generic.

**EPK (Electronic Press Kit) baseline components:**
- Artist bio (200 words max; story-first, accolade-second)
- High-resolution press photo (2400x3600px minimum; press-ready, not social-ready)
- Artist and release-level streaming links
- Embed-ready audio player (SoundCloud or Spotify iFrame; not a download link)
- Press quote (third-party is better than self-description)
- Booking/press contact with response SLA

**Media pitch personalization norms:**
- Address the journalist by name; reference a specific recent piece they wrote
- State in one sentence why this artist is relevant to that journalist's specific beat
- Include a streaming link with the timestamp of the best 30 seconds — journalists listen briefly; make the best part findable
- Follow-up cadence: one follow-up after 5 business days; no further contact if no response

### International Platform Economics

Platform dominance varies significantly by territory. Channel allocation must reflect where the target audience's attention actually lives, not US/Canada defaults.

**Territory-DSP dominance map (2026, ESTIMATE):**

| Territory | Primary DSP | Secondary DSP | Strategic note |
|-----------|------------|--------------|----------------|
| US / Canada | Spotify | Apple Music | YouTube significant for Latin-US; Amazon Music growing for smart-speaker audience |
| UK | Spotify | Apple Music | BBC Sounds for discovery; BBC Radio 6 Music champions independent acts |
| Germany | Spotify | Apple Music | Physical market still meaningful; Apple Music has stronger market share than US average |
| Korea | Melon | Genie / Bugs / FLO | Spotify growing but not dominant; K-pop-adjacent acts must be on all four domestic DSPs |
| Japan | Spotify | LINE Music / Recochoku | Apple Music strong; Japan has the highest physical-to-digital resistance of any major market |
| Brazil | Spotify | YouTube Music | YouTube primary for funk and pagode; Spotify LatAm editorial teams include Rio-based staff |
| Mexico | Spotify | YouTube Music | YouTube pre-roll significantly more effective than US benchmarks; Spanish-language metadata required |
| Australia / NZ | Spotify | Apple Music | Triple J radio is the primary break mechanism for independent artists |
| China | NetEase Cloud Music | QQ Music | Spotify not available in mainland China; requires local distributor or JV partnership |
| India | Gaana / JioSaavn | YouTube | Western artist break-in is primarily through YouTube |

**Channel allocation adjustment by territory:**
- **Korea:** domestic chart positioning (Melon real-time chart) is the credibility baseline. Build domestic chart activity before pursuing mainstream international press.
- **Japan:** physical release budget is non-optional for serious Japan campaigns — CD singles affect chart eligibility and retain retail relationships in a market with active physical purchasing behavior.
- **LatAm:** Spanish-language metadata (track descriptions, social bios, press materials) produces meaningfully better algorithmic discovery rates. English-only assets are not treated equally by Spotify LatAm algorithms (ESTIMATE).
- **Australia:** submit to Triple J Unearthed 4–6 weeks pre-release; if selected for rotation, the CPL-equivalent value in eastern Australian markets exceeds a mid-size paid social campaign.

**International paid media CPM benchmarks (ESTIMATE — not quotable; auction and creative-quality dependent):**

| Territory | Meta CPM range | TikTok CPM range | Implication for channel mix |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|----------------------------|
| US | $8–15 | $5–10 | Highest cost; warrants highest-converting creative |
| UK | $5–10 | $4–8 | Comparable ROI to US; worth concurrent run with same creative |
| Canada | $4–8 | $3–7 | Lower CPM than US; efficient expansion territory |
| Germany | $4–7 | $3–6 | TikTok growing but Meta still primary for 25–44 segment |
| Brazil | $2–5 | $1–3 | Lowest CPM; highest audience-reach efficiency per dollar |
| Australia | $6–12 | $4–8 | High CPM; smaller total audience; requires ROI validation |

**International paid media rule:** lower CPM markets are not automatically higher priority. ROI depends on whether the territory's audience drives monetizable outcomes — touring venue capacity, sync market, or brand deal value in that territory. Allocate international paid budget to territories with a downstream business case, not only to territories with cheap impressions.
