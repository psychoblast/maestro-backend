# PLMKR Marketing Scoring Rubric
Version: v1.0 — 8-Dimension Model (2026-06-20)
Status: SCOREABLE — weights, anchors, composite formula, and hard gates fixed.

---

## Rubric Scope
This rubric scores an artist, release, or campaign opportunity across the Marketing Agent's core evaluation dimensions. Used when assessing marketing readiness, prioritizing campaigns, or evaluating brand/partnership fit. Scores without stated reasoning and confidence context are invalid.

---

## Dimensions (8)

### 1. Virality
**Weight: 0.15**

**What it measures:** the likelihood that this artist, release, or campaign will spread organically beyond the activated audience — through reshares, UGC, meme adoption, cultural moment capture, or algorithmic amplification.

**Key factors:** is the hook memorable enough to be reshared in isolation from its full context? Is there a UGC-friendly format (a sound, a challenge, a visual template)? Does the content invite participation or only passive consumption?

**Anchors:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **1** | Audio/content not excerpt-worthy at ≤15 sec without full context. No identifiable hook in isolation. No participatory format. No prior organic reshare signal on comparable tracks. All reach is paid. |
| **5** | Recognizable ≤15-sec hook. Prior comparable tracks organically shared ≥3 times on social platforms. TikTok sound potential is plausible: genre has precedent, hook structure is excerpt-friendly. Some UGC exists on similar releases. |
| **10** | Audio IS the cultural moment — identifiable without visual context and already spreading organically before release. Clear participatory format vector (challenge, meme, movement, or sound trend). Pre-release TikTok/Reels spread is measurable. |

**Genre flip-condition:** if genre norms make organic viral spread structurally rare (ambient, jazz, classical), compress the top anchor — 10 in ambient means a track used in a viral ASMR/study chain, not a TikTok dance.

---

### 2. UGC Potential
**Weight: 0.12**

**What it measures:** the structural capacity of a release or campaign to generate user-generated content — fan covers, reaction videos, duets, lip syncs, remixes, or creative use of the official audio.

**Key factors:** is the audio identifiable and excerpt-worthy at 15 seconds? Does the sonic texture or lyrical content invite emotional expression from non-musicians? Is there a visual or movement element that lends itself to participatory formats?

**UGC vs. Virality distinction:** virality measures organic spread of the artist's content; UGC potential measures creation of new content by others using the artist's raw material. A track can have high UGC potential and low virality.

**Anchors:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **1** | Audio does not excerpt usefully at ≤15 sec. No obvious cover/duet/lip-sync format accessible to non-musicians. Genre structurally limits participatory adoption. No prior UGC on artist's catalog. |
| **5** | Clear ≤15-sec excerpt with distinct, identifiable sound. An obvious participation format is accessible to non-musicians. Genre has a documented TikTok sound track record. Artist has had prior tracks used organically in ≥3 UGC posts. |
| **10** | Track explicitly designed for participatory format. Official challenge or sound concept is planned and resourced. Creator seeding list is ready. Movement/gesture element is natural and replicable without choreography instruction. Pre-release UGC already exists without any promotion. |

**Genre flip-condition:** instrument-dominant tracks score higher on UGC than lyric-dominant tracks in the same genre because they serve as backgrounds more naturally.

---

### 3. Platform Fit
**Weight: 0.18**

**What it measures:** how well the artist's current content and audience presence maps to the platforms being prioritized in the campaign — measured across format nativity, posting cadence, engagement rate relative to platform norms, and algorithmic compatibility.

**Key factors per platform:**
- *TikTok/Reels:* posting frequency (≥3x/week), audio-hook strength in first 3 seconds, completion rate vs. vertical average
- *Spotify:* save rate, completion rate, playlist adds, editorial pitch eligibility
- *Instagram:* Reels completion rate, Stories engagement rate, bio link conversion rate
- *YouTube:* video cadence, average view duration as % of runtime, subscriber-to-view ratio

**Anchors:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **1** | No active profile on primary target platforms (or <30-day posting cadence), OR engagement rate in bottom quartile for comparable artist cohort. Algorithm is not in a growth state on any primary platform. Content format does not match platform culture. |
| **5** | Active presence on ≥2 primary platforms. Posting cadence ≥3x/week on primary. Engagement rate 40–70th percentile for comparable cohort. ≥1 piece of organic content performed above average in the past 30 days. |
| **10** | Top-decile engagement rate for comparable artist cohort on ≥2 primary platforms. Posting cadence ≥5x/week on primary. Algorithm actively delivering content to non-followers (measurable Reels/TikTok non-follower share ≥60%). ≥1 prior editorial placement documented. |

**Campaign-scope flip-condition:** if the campaign is platform-specific (e.g., Spotify-only editorial campaign), score Platform Fit only against Spotify metrics, not cross-platform. State which platforms are in scope.

---

### 4. Editorial Readiness
**Weight: 0.13**

**What it measures:** how prepared the release is to be pitched to and accepted by DSP editorial teams (Spotify, Apple Music, Amazon, TIDAL) and third-party editorial surfaces (blogs, radio, streaming playlists).

**Key factors:** pitch window compliance (≥7 days pre-release to Spotify), sonic quality and production standard, metadata completeness and accuracy, genre/mood tagging quality, press photo and EPK readiness, any pre-existing editorial relationships or prior placements.

**Hard gate:** any release submitted with incomplete or incorrect metadata is disqualified from editorial consideration regardless of other scores.

**Anchors:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **1** | HARD GATE: metadata incomplete or incorrect on ≥1 required field (ISRC, genre tag, UPC, release date), OR pitch submitted <7 days pre-release, OR no EPK, OR no press-ready photo (2400×3600px minimum). Score of 1 blocks editorial pitch recommendation regardless of composite score. |
| **5** | All metadata complete and verified by distributor QC. Pitch submitted ≥7 days pre-release with genre/mood/context/release story. EPK complete: bio ≤200 words, press photo, streaming links, embed player. No dedicated editorial contact but story is strong enough to stand without a relationship. |
| **10** | Named editorial contact at target DSP in the artist's genre/territory. Prior editorial placement on same artist in same genre documented. Pitch reviewed and approved by distributor editorial team. Territory-specific pitches prepared for all target markets. |

**Catalog/radio flip-condition:** for catalog campaigns (no new release), drop "pitch window" from the 1-anchor. For radio campaigns, substitute radio-specific pitch timing (typically 8–10 weeks advance for format adds).

---

### 5. Touring Synergy
**Weight: 0.03**

**What it measures:** the degree to which a marketing campaign can leverage active or planned touring activity to amplify reach — and conversely, how well the release timing supports building live audience demand.

**Key factors:** is a tour or live appearance scheduled within the campaign window? Are there territory-specific shows that can anchor territory-specific campaign spend?

**Anchors:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **1** | No live activity within 90 days of release. No announced shows. No market presence. Release is a pure streaming/digital event with no physical amplification surface. |
| **5** | Regional or club-level shows confirmed within 60 days of release. Tour announcement can be coordinated with release timing. Territory-specific campaign spend can be anchored to ≥2 show markets. |
| **10** | Arena-level or national tour actively on sale with ticket demand concurrent with release. Multiple territory tours create multiple campaign windows. Release explicitly supports ticket demand. Prior release-to-tour streaming spikes documented for this artist. |

**Tour-campaign flip-condition:** if the campaign IS the tour announcement, elevate Touring Synergy weight to 0.15 and reduce Fan LTV to 0.13 for that specific campaign type.

---

### 6. Merch & D2C Potential
**Weight: 0.10**

**What it measures:** the degree to which a release or campaign creates a conversion opportunity for physical and digital merchandise, D2C offerings (limited physical formats, bundles, exclusive experiences), and direct-to-fan revenue capture.

**Key factors:** does the release concept lend itself to physical format storytelling (vinyl concept, art prints, lyric zines)? Is there an existing D2C store and fan email list to market into?

**Anchors:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **1** | No D2C store. No email list. No merch history. No physical format planned. Artist has no documented streaming-listener-to-direct-purchase conversion. |
| **5** | D2C store exists and active (last purchase ≤90 days). Email list ≥500 subscribers with ≥15% open rate. Release-themed merch is planned and resourced. |
| **10** | Established merch program with ≥1 documented item or show-specific sellout. D2C email list ≥5K subscribers with ≥25% open rate. Album/tour bundle is planned and production-resourced. Fan community shows demonstrated purchasing velocity on new items within 72h of announcement. |

**Early-career flip-condition:** for first or second release with no merch history, compress the scale — their "10" is a fully stocked D2C store with an email list and one successful D2C-exclusive drop.

---

### 7. Brand Partnership Potential
**Weight: 0.07**

**What it measures:** the viability of the artist or campaign as a co-marketing, sponsorship, or integration partner for brand advertisers — evaluated from the brand's side (audience reach, content quality, brand-safety record, audience demographics, category exclusivity).

**Key factors:** audience size and demographic clarity, content brand-safety record (no controversy within 90 days), category exclusivity openness, content quality and production standard, any prior brand work.

**PLMKR note:** Brand Partnership Potential scores against the artist's suitability for co-marketing within the PLMKR ecosystem — audience fit, brand-safety profile, and category alignment matter most.

**Anchors:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **1** | Audience too small for brand interest: <10K engaged social following AND <100K monthly listeners. OR: brand-safety incident within 90 days. OR: genre or audience profile so narrow no viable commercial category can be identified. |
| **5** | 50K–500K monthly listeners. Audience demographic is defined and communicable (age range, territory, lifestyle signals). Clean brand-safety record (no incidents in 12 months). ≥1 viable product-adjacent category identifiable (lifestyle, fashion, beverage, gaming, health/fitness). |
| **10** | 500K+ monthly listeners OR high-priority demographic profile (18–34, high-engagement). ≥1 completed brand campaign with documented deliverables. Multiple open commercial categories. Active inbound brand interest documented. Content production quality meets brand standards without major creative adaptation. |

---

### 8. Fan LTV Potential
**Weight: 0.22**

**What it measures:** the long-term value trajectory of the artist's fanbase — how likely are new fans acquired in this campaign to become high-LTV fans (ticket buyers, merch purchasers, recurring D2C subscribers, long-term stream contributors)?

**Key factors:** prior ticket-to-listener conversion rate, merch purchase history, email list responsiveness, fan community engagement level, catalog depth, streaming completion rate as a proxy for depth of listen.

**Why this matters:** the primary north star. A campaign that acquires 100,000 low-LTV fans is often worth less than one that acquires 10,000 high-LTV fans. The marketing budget justification depends on which scenario is likely.

**Anchors:**
| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **1** | No measurable fan-to-buyer conversion evidence of any kind. Pure passive streaming audience. No email list. ≤1 release in catalog. Streaming completion rate <45%. No community platform. No D2C history. |
| **5** | Demonstrated ticket conversion on ≥1 live event (sold-out or capacity ≥70%). Email list with ≥20% open rate. ≥2-album catalog depth with documented re-listen behavior. Community presence: Discord, Patreon, or equivalent with ≥100 paying/active members. |
| **10** | Cult-level engagement across multiple evidence types: consistent sellout history at growing venue capacity, high-volume merch purchase velocity (sell-through within 72h), active community (≥500 paying/active members), catalog streams compound year-over-year (+15% YoY catalog lift documented), multi-year listener retention data showing ≥50% of year-1 listeners still active in year-3 (ESTIMATE). |

**Emerging artist cap:** LTV is the hardest dimension to score on a new or emerging artist with no history. Score structural indicators (catalog depth, community infrastructure, completion rate) at 3–4, labeled INFERRED. A score above 7 requires documented conversion evidence. For first or second release with no conversion history, a structural maximum of 6 applies; note the cap explicitly.

## Scoring Rules

### How to score
1. Score each dimension independently before combining — do not let an overall impression drive individual scores.
2. Each score is a claim: state the evidence, the confidence level, and the key assumption.
3. Do not average when hard-gate dimensions fail. If Editorial Readiness = 1, the release is not ready for editorial pitch regardless of other scores.

### Confidence tagging (mandatory)
Every score entry must carry: evidence type (observed / told / inferred), confidence level (high / medium / low), and the assumption that, if wrong, would change the score by ≥1 point.

---

## Composite Score Formula

```
Marketing Score = (V × 0.15) + (UGC × 0.12) + (PF × 0.18) + (ER × 0.13) + (TS × 0.03) + (MD × 0.10) + (BP × 0.07) + (LTV × 0.22)

Where:
  V   = Virality score (1–10)
  UGC = UGC Potential score (1–10)
  PF  = Platform Fit score (1–10)
  ER  = Editorial Readiness score (1–10)
  TS  = Touring Synergy score (1–10)
  MD  = Merch & D2C Potential score (1–10)
  BP  = Brand Partnership Potential score (1–10)
  LTV = Fan LTV Potential score (1–10)

Result range: 1.00–10.00
```

**Output bands:**
| Score | Band | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| 8.0–10.0 | Green — full campaign | All core conditions met; recommend full 12-week campaign arc |
| 6.0–7.9 | Yellow — targeted campaign | Proceed with focus on 2–3 strongest dimensions; gaps noted |
| 4.0–5.9 | Amber — limited campaign | Address gap dimensions before full spend; partial rollout |
| 1.0–3.9 | Red — not ready | Hard gaps (usually Editorial Readiness or Platform Fit) require remediation before campaign investment |

**Unlock condition:** composite is not reported until all 8 dimensions have been scored with ≥1 piece of evidence cited. If any dimension must be scored without evidence, it defaults to 3, is labeled `INFERRED`, and the composite is labeled `PROVISIONAL — [X] dimension(s) inferred`.

**Inferred vs. told-absence:** a dimension is without evidence only when no observed or told signal bears on it. Told-absence of infrastructure (e.g., no D2C store, no email list, no merch history) IS evidence and is scored on its anchor (typically a confident low score), not defaulted to 3 and not labeled INFERRED.

**Uncertainty propagation:** the composite confidence is capped by the lowest-confidence critical dimension. The cap must be named inline.

---

## Hard Gates

| Gate | Trigger | Blocked recommendation |
|------|---------|----------------------|
| **Editorial Gate** | Editorial Readiness = 1 | Any editorial pitch to any DSP. Blocked regardless of composite. Remediation required before pitch. |
| **Paid Spend Gate** | Platform Fit ≤ 2 | Paid spend recommendation exceeding 20% of campaign budget. |
| **Brand Pitch Gate** | Brand Partnership Potential ≤ 2 | Any brand partnership pitch recommendation. |

---

## Weight Flip-Conditions

| Context | Adjustment |
|---------|-----------|
| Pure awareness/reach campaign | Fan LTV → 0.12; Platform Fit → 0.22 |
| Artist entering new market with no existing presence | Platform Fit → 0.25; Fan LTV → 0.15 |
| Tour announcement campaign | Touring Synergy → 0.15; Fan LTV → 0.13; reduce Merch or Brand to compensate |
