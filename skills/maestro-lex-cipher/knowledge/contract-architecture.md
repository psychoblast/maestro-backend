# PLMKR Contract Architecture — Deal-Type Legal Knowledge
Version: v1.0 — Lex-Cipher contract knowledge (2026-06-20)
Status: ACTIVE — clause-level legal mechanics by agreement type.

This file holds the deal-type-specific legal mechanics the agent applies during
red-flag review. It identifies clause types and explains what they do. It does
not advise on what position to take — that routes to the management function and
to qualified counsel. Every risk-touching output carries the counsel footer.

---

## Recording Contracts

**Deal-type matrix:**
- **Exclusive recording agreement (major / indie):** label owns masters, artist
  is exclusive for a term plus option periods. Highest rights transfer.
- **License deal:** artist (or artist's entity) owns masters and licenses them to
  the label for a term, after which rights revert. Lower rights transfer.
- **Distribution deal:** artist retains masters and ownership; distributor
  handles supply chain for a fee. Lowest rights transfer.
- **Joint venture / P&D:** shared ownership or profit split; specific governance
  and accounting provisions control.

**Clause mechanics to identify:**
- **Royalty base** — SRLP (suggested retail list price), PPD (published price to
  dealer / wholesale), or net receipts. The base changes the effective rate
  dramatically; a "higher" percentage on a lower base can pay less. Identify the
  base before discussing any rate.
- **Recoupment** — advances and defined costs are recovered from the artist's
  royalty account before the artist sees royalties. Identify which costs are
  recoupable and whether the definition is enumerated or open-ended.
- **Cross-collateralization** — netting recoupable balances across projects or
  rights so a profitable project pays down another's deficit. Identify presence
  and scope.
- **Option periods** — label's unilateral right to extend for additional album
  cycles. Many option periods can bind a long stretch of an artist's career.
- **Key-man / key-person clause** — ties the deal to a specific executive; if they
  leave, defined rights may follow. Identify presence.
- **Controlled-composition clause** — reduces mechanical royalties payable on the
  artist-writer's own compositions, often to a fraction of statutory. Flag as a
  red-flag clause (income reduction for writers).
- **Reversion** — whether and when master rights return to the artist. Absence is
  a high-severity gap.
- **Audit rights** — the only mechanism to verify royalty accounting. Identify the
  window from statement date, scope, notice, and cost allocation.

## Publishing & Songwriter Agreements

The economic administration analysis routes to the publishing function. This
agent handles legal mechanics, red-flag clause review, and work-for-hire
analysis.

**Structures:**
- **Full publishing deal** — publisher acquires copyright in compositions;
  highest transfer.
- **Co-publishing deal** — writer retains a share of the publisher's side;
  ownership is split.
- **Administration deal** — publisher administers but does not own; lowest
  transfer; admin fee defined.

**Clause mechanics:**
- **Term and reversion** — how long the publisher controls compositions and
  whether/when they revert. Identify the trigger.
- **Scope of grant** — existing catalog vs. future compositions only. Inclusion
  of existing catalog is a material expansion of the grant.
- **Work-for-hire language** — frequently misapplied to compositions. WFH in the
  US is effective only for enumerated work categories with a qualifying written
  agreement; applied to a composition it is often legally ineffective and creates
  dispute risk. Flag when present without a qualifying basis.
- **Split sheets** — the documentary basis for ownership shares among
  co-writers. Their absence is a chain-of-title gap.
- **Black-box / unclaimed-royalty provisions** — how unmatched income is handled.
  Identify presence.
- **Administration fee** — the percentage the publisher/administrator retains.
  Identify whether it is defined; do not state a "standard" figure without a
  Tier A/B source.

## Management Agreements

**Commission legal structure:**
- Commission is the manager's compensation, typically a percentage of defined
  income. Identify the **commission base** — gross vs. net — because the base
  changes the effective economics as much as the percentage. Do not state a
  "standard" rate without a documented source.
- **Income carve-outs** — categories excluded from commission (e.g., recording
  costs, tour support pass-throughs, co-writer shares). Identify which are defined.

**Fiduciary-duty framework:** a manager generally owes fiduciary duties to the
artist — loyalty, disclosure, and avoidance of undisclosed conflicts. Identify
whether the agreement addresses conflicts of interest (e.g., manager also acting
as promoter, publisher, or label).

**Sunset clause:** governs post-term commission — whether and for how long the
manager continues to commission income from deals originated during the term, and
on a declining schedule or flat. Absence is a material gap (potential indefinite
post-term commission).

**Termination:** identify termination for cause, cure periods, and what survives
termination. "Termination of the management agreement" does not by itself unwind
deals the manager originated — those are governed by their own provisions.

## Licensing Agreements

- **Sync license** — grants use of a master and/or composition synchronized to
  visual media (film/TV/ads/games). Identify whether BOTH master and composition
  are cleared (a "one-stop" controls both); scope of use (media, territory,
  term); exclusivity; and option/renewal terms. A composition-only or
  master-only grant leaves the other side uncleared.
- **Sample clearance** — using a portion of an existing recording/composition
  requires clearance from BOTH the master owner and the publishing owner. The
  chain is recursive: a sample within the sampled work must also be cleared.
- **Brand / endorsement license** — grants use of name, image, and likeness.
  Identify category exclusivity scope, NIL (name/image/likeness) territory and
  term, morality-clause trigger (subjective vs. defined), kill-fee presence, and
  payment timing.
- **Cover-song mechanical license** — the statutory mechanical license permits
  recording a previously released non-dramatic composition; identify whether the
  license is compulsory/statutory or negotiated.

## Live Performance & Touring Contracts

- **Performance agreement anatomy** — parties, date, fee, payment timing,
  deposit, cancellation/force-majeure, technical rider, and hospitality rider.
- **Liability and indemnity** — identify whether liability is capped. No liability
  cap in a performance agreement is a high-severity red flag (unlimited exposure
  for venue incidents).
- **Force majeure** — what excuses performance and how the deposit/fee is treated
  on cancellation. Post-COVID doctrine on force majeure is currency-sensitive;
  flag for currency verification.
- **Cancellation and pay-or-play** — whether the artist is paid if the engagement
  is cancelled by the buyer.

## Business Entities & Structuring

This agent identifies entity types and what they do; entity selection for a
specific situation routes to qualified counsel and tax advisors.

- **Loan-out entity** — an entity that "loans out" the artist's services;
  contracts run through the entity. Common structuring choice; tax and liability
  implications route to qualified counsel/tax.
- **Band / partnership agreements** — govern ownership of name, masters,
  compositions, voting, leaver provisions, and dissolution among members. Absence
  is a major dispute risk.
- **Co-publishing / administration entities** — hold and administer compositions.
- **Liability shielding** — the general purpose of entity structuring is to
  separate personal and business liability; specifics route to counsel.

## Commercial, Employment & Confidentiality Agreements (Adjacent)

The agent also recognizes general commercial agreements that surround a music
career:

- **NDA / confidentiality** — mutual vs. one-way; definition-of-confidential-
  information scope; standard carve-outs (public domain, independently developed,
  required by law); residuals clauses; term and return/destroy obligations.
- **Services / MSA + SOW** — master terms vs. statement of work; SLA enforcement;
  limitation of liability; indemnification tiers.
- **Independent-contractor vs. employee** — classification tests (in the US,
  ABC/IRS/economic-realities tests) determine whether a collaborator is a
  contractor or an employee; misclassification creates wage-and-hour and
  IP-ownership risk. WFH/IP assignment language interacts with classification.
- **Restrictive covenants** — non-compete and non-solicit enforceability varies
  by jurisdiction; flag for territorial qualification.

For all of these, the agent identifies the clause type and what it does, marks
gaps, and routes legal opinion and negotiation to qualified counsel and the
management function.
