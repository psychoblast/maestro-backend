## Music Copyright — The Two Copyrights

Every recorded song carries TWO distinct copyrights:
- **Composition** (musical work) — melody, harmony, and lyrics. Typically owned
  by songwriters/publishers.
- **Sound recording** (master) — the specific captured performance. Typically
  owned by the recording artist/label.

A single use (e.g., a sync placement or a sample) usually requires clearing
BOTH. Identify which copyright a question concerns before analyzing rights.

## Copyright Basics by Major Territory

State territorial applicability explicitly. US doctrine does not govern other
territories.

- **USA** — Copyright Act of 1976; copyright is automatic on fixation.
  Registration (copyright.gov) is a prerequisite to suing for US works and
  unlocks statutory damages and attorneys' fees. Term: life + 70 years for
  post-1978 works. DMCA § 512 governs platform takedown/safe harbor. No general
  music moral right (VARA covers visual art only). Currency-verify rate schedules
  and case law.
- **UK** — Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988; automatic, no registration.
  Term life + 70 years. Stronger moral rights than the US. Independent of EU
  post-Brexit.
- **EU** — Copyright in the Digital Single Market Directive (2019); Article 17
  shifts platform liability for user uploads. Term life + 70 years. Droit moral
  is strong in France/Germany.
- **Canada** — Copyright Act; automatic; optional registration (CIPO). Term life
  + 70 years (post-2022). CanCon rules affect broadcast.
- **Other markets** (Australia, Nigeria, Brazil, South Korea, South Africa,
  Jamaica) — terms and registration regimes vary; route specifics to qualified
  local counsel and verify currency.

The Berne Convention provides automatic protection across member states without
formality, but national law governs term, remedies, and registration effects.

## Preliminary Infringement Analysis (Framework — Not an Opinion)

Apply the two-part US test (access + substantial similarity; extrinsic +
intrinsic) from the legal-doctrine file. Key disciplines:

- Copyright protects original expression, NOT ideas, common chords, standard
  progressions, scales, generic structures, short phrases, or genre-common
  rhythmic patterns (Skidmore v. Led Zeppelin, 9th Cir. 2020 en banc).
- "Striking similarity" can substitute for proven access (Arnstein v. Porter).
- Current doctrine leans toward requiring similarity in specific protectable
  expression, not general "feel" — but this is currency-sensitive; verify before
  output.
- Output label: PRELIMINARY INFRINGEMENT ANALYSIS — NOT A LEGAL OPINION. The
  agent identifies factors a court would weigh; it never concludes infringement
  occurred or that a claim would succeed. Routes to copyright litigation counsel.

## IP Chain of Title

Chain of title is the documented sequence of ownership and transfers establishing
who controls a copyright today. Defects are classified MINOR / BLOCKING /
LITIGATION RISK per the legal-doctrine file.

**Work-for-hire determination (US):** a work is "made for hire" only if (a) it is
created by an employee within the scope of employment, OR (b) it is specially
ordered/commissioned, falls within one of the statutorily enumerated categories,
AND the parties signed a written WFH agreement. Sound recordings are not cleanly
enumerated; WFH applied to a composition or an independent contractor's
recording without the qualifying basis is frequently ineffective and creates a
blocking defect or litigation risk.

**Assignment vs. license:** an assignment transfers ownership; a license grants
permitted use while ownership stays with the grantor. The right to terminate a
service agreement does not automatically reclaim assigned copyright — reversion
is a separate provision governed by its own terms.

**Due-diligence checklist (identify presence, do not opine on sufficiency):**
executed assignments for each claimed right · split sheets for co-written works ·
registrations consistent with the claimed chain · no competing/undisclosed claims
· no active dispute notices · WFH agreements where WFH is asserted.

## Trademark — Name & Logo Protection

Trademark protects brand identifiers (artist name, logo) in connection with
goods/services — distinct from copyright. The agent identifies the mechanism; it
does not opine on registrability or likelihood of confusion (those route to
qualified trademark counsel).

- **Home registration** — USPTO (US), UKIPO (UK), CIPO (Canada), IP Australia,
  EUIPO (covers the EU member states) and others. Registration is per class of
  goods/services.
- **International** — the WIPO Madrid Protocol allows a single application to
  extend protection to many member countries.
- **Likelihood-of-confusion** analysis (the core registrability/infringement
  question) weighs mark similarity, goods/services relatedness, and channels —
  this is a legal-opinion determination that routes to counsel.

## International Deal Mechanics

- **Territorial qualification is mandatory.** Label US analysis "In the US…" and
  route the governing jurisdiction's analysis to qualified local counsel.
- **Withholding tax** — cross-border royalty and performance income may be subject
  to withholding; treaty rates vary. Identify the issue; route rate specifics and
  optimization to qualified counsel/tax.
- **Moral rights** — strong in France/Germany (droit moral), narrow in the US.
- **Neighboring rights** — meaningful income in the EU/UK; limited in the US
  (digital performance only via SoundExchange; no US terrestrial performer
  neighboring right).
- **Fair use vs. fair dealing** — the US four-factor fair use is flexible; UK/EU
  fair dealing is a narrow enumerated set. Do not transplant US fair-use
  reasoning into a UK/EU question.

## Dispute Resolution Mechanics

- **Escalation ladder:** demand/negotiation → mediation (non-binding, fastest) →
  arbitration (binding, private, often contractually mandated) → litigation (last
  resort, public, slow, expensive).
- **Governing-law and forum clauses** determine which jurisdiction's law applies
  and where disputes are heard — identify whether the agreement defines both.
- **Statute of limitations** varies by claim and jurisdiction and is
  currency-sensitive — flag, do not state a specific period as settled without a
  Tier A source.
- **Common music disputes:** royalty underpayment (audit → demand →
  arbitration/litigation), manager-commission disputes, co-writer credit/ownership,
  venue non-payment, and copyright infringement.

The agent maps the available mechanisms and what each clause does; it does not
recommend which to pursue or predict outcomes. That is legal strategy and routes
to qualified counsel.
