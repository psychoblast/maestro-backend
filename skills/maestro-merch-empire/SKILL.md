## The Merch Opportunity

1,000 streams = approximately $4. Same fan buying a hoodie = $60-80. Merchandise is the highest-margin, most direct revenue stream available to independent artists. One superfan who buys merch is worth 15,000 streams.

## Product Selection by Career Stage

GOLD — Start simple:
- T-shirt (1-2 designs maximum)
- Sticker pack
- Digital download bundle
- Total SKUs: 3-5 maximum

PLATINUM — Build the line:
- T-shirts and hoodies (core items)
- Hat or cap
- Tote bag
- Phone case
- Limited edition items tied to releases
- Total SKUs: 8-15

DIAMOND — Full merchandise operation:
- Complete apparel line (t-shirts, hoodies, joggers, shorts)
- Accessories (hats, bags, jewelry)
- Lifestyle products (mugs, posters, phone cases)
- Collectibles and limited editions
- Vinyl and physical media
- Luxury/premium items for superfans
- Total SKUs: 20+

## Production Options

PRINT ON DEMAND (best for Gold tier):
- Printful: integrates with Shopify and Etsy, ships globally
- Printify: lower cost, wide product range
- Teespring/Spring: built-in marketplace, zero upfront cost
- Pros: no inventory risk, no upfront cost, automatic fulfillment
- Cons: lower margin (30-40% vs 60-70% bulk), less quality control

BULK MANUFACTURING (best for Platinum and Diamond):
- Minimum order quantities: 50-100 units per design
- Cost per unit drops dramatically at volume — hoodie $15 bulk vs $30 POD
- Requires inventory management and fulfillment solution
- Canadian suppliers: Allmade, Stanley/Stella (sustainable options)
- US suppliers: Gildan, Bella+Canvas, Next Level Apparel
- International: local suppliers for touring markets reduce shipping costs

## Pricing Strategy

Rule: Sell at minimum 3-4x production cost for POD, 5-7x for bulk.

T-shirt (bulk cost $8-12): Retail $35-45
Hoodie (bulk cost $20-30): Retail $65-85
Hat (bulk cost $8-15): Retail $35-45
Tote bag (bulk cost $5-8): Retail $25-35
Sticker pack (bulk cost $1-2): Retail $8-12

## Online Store Platforms

SHOPIFY: Best overall — full control, integrates everything, professional
- Cost: $29-79/month
- Best for: Platinum and Diamond artists

BANDCAMP: Best for independent music artists specifically
- Takes 15% (drops to 10% after $5,000 in sales)
- Built-in music + merch platform
- Strong indie music audience
- Best for: All tiers, especially Gold

ETSY: Good for handmade and limited edition items
- Discovery platform — fans find artists here
- Best for: Gold and Platinum

DIRECT LINK (via distributor store): DistroKid, TuneCore some offer merch
- Simple but limited
- Best for: Gold getting started

## Tour Merch Strategy

VENUE HALL FEE: Venues typically take 20-30% of merch revenue
- Always negotiate — push to reduce or buy out the hall fee
- Buy-out: pay flat fee upfront instead of percentage
- At smaller venues: sometimes waivable entirely

TOUR MERCH RULES:
- Bring more than you think you need — running out is lost revenue
- Have card payment ready — most fans do not carry cash
- Square, Stripe, or SumUp for mobile payments
- Staff the merch table during and after the show
- Tour-exclusive items drive urgency — only available at shows
- Track inventory nightly: units in, sold, remaining, revenue

## Limited Drop Strategy

Scarcity drives demand:
- Announce drop date in advance — build anticipation
- Limited quantity — when it is gone it is gone
- Never restock the same item — creates urgency for next drop
- Bundle with exclusive content — download, signed photo, video message
- Drop around releases, milestones, and tour dates

## Global Fulfillment

For international fans:
- Printful and Printify ship globally from multiple warehouses
- For tour markets: source locally to avoid customs and shipping costs
- EU customers: VAT implications for UK and European sales post-Brexit
- Australian customers: GST on imported goods over AUD $1,000
- Always display shipping costs clearly — cart abandonment spikes with surprise shipping fees
