## Beat Licensing Explained

NON-EXCLUSIVE LEASE ($20-100):
- You can use the beat but producer can sell it to unlimited other artists
- Limited distribution (usually 50,000-500,000 streams)
- Standard for emerging artists on non-priority releases
- Cannot be used for sync licensing
- Cannot be used on major label releases

EXCLUSIVE LEASE ($200-2,000+):
- Only you can use this beat going forward
- Higher stream limits or unlimited
- Better for priority releases
- Still owned by producer — you license the master only

EXCLUSIVE BUYOUT ($500-10,000+):
- Full ownership transfer of the master recording (beat)
- You own the track outright
- Best for artists who want full catalog control
- Required for most sync licensing and label deals

CUSTOM PRODUCTION (negotiated):
- Producer creates a beat specifically for you
- Cost varies dramatically by producer profile
- Emerging: $200-500, Developing: $500-2,500, Established: $2,500-25,000+
- Always negotiate ownership upfront — who owns the master

## Producer Agreement Essentials

Every production relationship needs a written agreement covering:
- Ownership of the master recording
- Publishing split of the composition (producer often wants 50% of publishing)
- Royalty split if commercial release
- Credit: "Produced by [Name]" — confirm billing
- Exclusivity terms
- Sync licensing rights
- Sample clearance responsibility (who is liable if undisclosed samples are found)
- Stem delivery requirements

## Studio Booking Strategy

HOME STUDIO vs PROFESSIONAL STUDIO:
- Home studio: lower cost, unlimited time, comfort — best for writing and demos
- Professional studio: quality gear, engineer expertise, focus — best for final recording

WHEN TO BOOK PROFESSIONAL STUDIO:
- Vocals are primary instrument and quality matters most
- Need live instruments recorded
- Mixing in a treated room
- Video content of recording session planned

STUDIO COSTS BY MARKET:
- Toronto: $75-300/hour, $500-1,500/day
- New York: $150-500/hour, $1,000-3,000/day
- Los Angeles: $150-500/hour, $1,000-3,000/day
- London: £100-400/hour, £800-2,500/day
- Lagos: NGN 50,000-200,000/day (approximately $50-200 USD)
- Accra: GHS 500-2,000/day (approximately $40-160 USD)

MAXIMIZING STUDIO TIME:
- Arrive fully prepared — lyrics written, references ready
- Warm up vocals before arriving
- Book engineer you have worked with before when possible
- Record more than you need — unused takes have value

## Sample Clearance

If a beat contains samples:
- Producer is responsible for disclosing all samples
- Uncleared samples = cannot release commercially without clearance
- Sample clearance cost: $500-10,000+ depending on original recording profile
- Mandatory: get written confirmation from producer that all samples are cleared
- Best practice: get stems and confirm no uncleared samples before paying full production fee
