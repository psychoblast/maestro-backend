# PLMKR Production Readiness Rubric

Scores a song, production, or release opportunity across the eight dimensions that determine whether a recording will reach a release-ready master on schedule and on budget. Used when Beat is asked to assess production readiness, evaluate a proposed budget or schedule, or determine whether a master is release-ready. PLMKR-owned original. Scores without stated reasoning and confidence context are invalid.

---

## Dimensions (8 — weights sum to 1.00)

| # | Dimension | Key | Weight |
|---|-----------|-----|-------:|
| 1 | Creative Direction Clarity | `creative_direction_clarity` | 0.18 |
| 2 | Producer / Team Fit | `producer_team_fit` | 0.16 |
| 3 | Song / Arrangement Readiness | `song_arrangement_readiness` | 0.14 |
| 4 | Technical Quality & Standards | `technical_quality_standards` | 0.14 |
| 5 | Budget Discipline | `budget_discipline` | 0.12 |
| 6 | Delivery & QC Completeness | `delivery_qc_completeness` | 0.12 |
| 7 | Schedule Feasibility | `schedule_feasibility` | 0.08 |
| 8 | Deal & Ownership Structure | `deal_ownership_structure` | 0.06 |

Each dimension is scored 1–10 against the anchors below. Dimensions 4 and 6 carry **hard gates**.

---

### 1. Creative Direction Clarity — 0.18
**Measures:** whether the production has a clear, agreed sonic target — reference tracks, genre/sub-genre positioning, arrangement intent, dynamic/loudness character — that the whole team can execute against without mid-session re-alignment.

- **1** — No reference tracks. No agreed sonic target. No brief in any form. Revisions will be driven by "I'll know it when I hear it."
- **5** — 2–3 references discussed; genre and sub-genre named; key arrangement decisions verbally agreed; brief not yet written but described consistently; producer confirms they can hit the target.
- **10** — Written brief signed by artist and producer; ≥3 references each citing artist + track + a specific sonic element; sonic target measurable (genre, sub-genre, era, BPM range, instrumentation palette, dynamic character); reference-listening session documented; demo A/B'd against the references.

### 2. Producer / Team Fit — 0.16
**Measures:** whether the producer, mixer, mastering engineer, and session players are *matched* to the song's genre/sonic target and the artist's stage and budget — not merely named.

- **1** — No producer, or producer chosen without auditing credits against this genre/sonic target, or rate exceeds budget by >30% with no resolution path, or prior collaboration left unresolved disputes.
- **5** — Producer selected on ≥3 documented genre-matching credits; rate within range; mixer/master identified but unconfirmed; session players unspecified.
- **10** — Full team locked with genre/sonic-matching credits cited per role; rates confirmed in writing; producer has ≥1 documented credit at this sonic tier with a measurable outcome; players confirmed for tracking dates; prior collaboration clean or a reference session done.

### 3. Song / Arrangement Readiness — 0.14
**Measures:** how structurally complete and production-ready the song is before tracking — demo, locked structure, chord chart/lead sheet, locked composition and lyrics.

- **1** — No demo. Song not written to a finished draft. Arrangement undefined; production is being used to *write* the song. No locked BPM or key.
- **5** — Demo exists at target BPM; structure locked; chart available; composition 90%+ complete; producer approved to track.
- **10** — Full pre-production demo; all arrangement decisions made; chart and lead sheet final; lyrics/melody locked and artist-approved in writing; pre-pro arrangement map delivered; reference-mix demo A/B'd against the sonic target.

### 4. Technical Quality & Standards — 0.14 · **HARD GATE**
**Measures:** whether recorded/mixed material meets the technical delivery standard for the target format — integrated loudness, true-peak, format spec, stem completeness.

**Hard gate:** master fails platform loudness/format/stem spec → **NOT DELIVERABLE**, blocking a release-ready verdict regardless of composite.

- **1** — [HARD GATE] Loudness fails target by a significant margin, **or** true-peak exceeds the ceiling, **or** format is non-conforming (wrong sample rate/bit depth/codec), **or** stems missing/incomplete/mis-named, **or** QC bounced. NOT DELIVERABLE.
- **5** — Loudness within tolerance of the current platform target; true-peak within ceiling; correct delivery format (24-bit minimum); stems present and roughly named but not yet verified against full spec; minor revisions possible; no bounce.
- **10** — Loudness on the current platform target; true-peak verified within ceiling on all deliverables; full deliverable set confirmed at correct format; mastering sign-off documented; distributor QC passed with no flags; identifiers embedded.

### 5. Budget Discipline — 0.12
**Measures:** whether the budget is defined, phase-allocated, tracked against actuals, carries real contingency, and records change orders with stated tradeoffs.

- **1** — No formal budget; ad hoc spend; no phase allocation; no contingency; verbal change orders; actuals untracked.
- **5** — Phase-allocated budget (pre-pro, tracking, mix, master, contingency) approved by the owner; key items invoice-backed; ≥10% contingency reserved; 1–2 change orders documented.
- **10** — Actuals reconciled at each phase close; every cost item sourced by signed deal memo or contract; change orders documented with the money/time/quality tradeoff named; contingency intact at ≥10% through tracking close; no undisclosed commitments.

### 6. Delivery & QC Completeness — 0.12 · **HARD GATE**
**Measures:** whether the full delivery package is assembled and verified — master, stems, instrumental, metadata, credits, format compliance.

**Hard gate:** required package incomplete → **RELEASE BLOCKED**, regardless of composite.

- **1** — [HARD GATE] Missing stereo master, **or** required stems, **or** identifier (ISRC), **or** core metadata, **or** finalized credits; delivery date unmeetable. RELEASE BLOCKED.
- **5** — Stereo master delivered and QC'd; identifier assigned and embedded; core metadata complete; credits drafted and artist-reviewed; stems present but not fully verified; delivery window achievable.
- **10** — Full package delivered and verified (master + stems + instrumental + radio edit as applicable); all metadata verified through distributor QC; credits finalized and artist-approved in writing; delivery confirmed received with no flags.

### 7. Schedule Feasibility — 0.08
**Measures:** whether the timeline from pre-production through delivered master is realistic given studio/team availability, revision cycles, and the release date.

- **1** — Release date set but no production schedule; availability unconfirmed; implied timeline shorter than the minimum realistic arc; key dependencies unresolved; high risk, no mitigation.
- **5** — Phase-by-phase schedule exists; key members confirmed; ≥1 revision round of buffer per phase; release date achievable; 1–2 risks identified with mitigation.
- **10** — Full calendar signed off; phase dates locked; mastering slot booked; distributor delivery met with ≥2-week buffer before release; all revision rounds and ≥1 week contingency reserved; no unresolved dependencies.

### 8. Deal & Ownership Structure — 0.06
**Measures:** whether all production deal terms — points vs. fee, advances, work-for-hire vs. royalty, co-production splits, master ownership — are documented with recoupment and ownership consequences stated.

- **1** — No deal memos for any role; producer terms verbal/undefined; master ownership ambiguous; WFH vs. royalty unresolved; high dispute risk.
- **5** — Deal memos executed for key roles; points-vs-fee documented; advance stated with recoupment mechanism; WFH stated per role; master ownership assigned in writing; no open deal points.
- **10** — All terms in signed contracts/countersigned memos (points %, fee, advance, recoupment formula, ownership clause per role); session players' WFH confirmed in writing; co-production splits signed; co-write/publishing implications flagged and referred to Publishing; reviewed by counsel or an experienced manager; no verbal commitments.

## Scoring Rules

1. Score each dimension independently before combining — do not let an overall impression drive individual scores.
2. Each score is a claim: state the evidence type (MEASURED/SOURCED/JUDGED/AMBIGUOUS/ABSENT), the confidence (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW/PARTIAL with reason), and the assumption that, if wrong, would move the score by ≥1 point.
3. Do not average past a failed hard gate. If Dim-4 = 1 or Dim-6 = 1, the master is not release-ready regardless of composite.

**Inferred vs. told-absence.** A dimension defaults to **3, labeled INFERRED**, only when *no* observed or told signal bears on it — e.g., a missing production track record on a first or early project is a without-evidence condition and is **never scored below 3** on that basis (a missing track record is not negative evidence). Told-absence of infrastructure (no deal memo, no budget, no brief) **is** evidence and is scored on its anchor (typically a confident low score), not defaulted to 3.

## Composite Score Formula

```
Production Score = (CDC×0.18) + (PTF×0.16) + (SAR×0.14) + (TQS×0.14)
                 + (BD×0.12) + (DQC×0.12) + (SF×0.08) + (DOS×0.06)
Result range: 1.00–10.00
```

**Output bands:**

| Range | Band | Meaning |
|-------|------|---------|
| 8.0–10.0 | GREEN — release-ready | All core conditions met; on track for a release-ready master |
| 6.0–7.9 | YELLOW — proceed with named gaps | Move forward; resolve 1–2 gap dimensions before delivery |
| 4.0–5.9 | AMBER — address gaps before full tracking | Resolve gaps before committing full studio budget; partial pre-pro only |
| 1.0–3.9 | RED — not ready | Hard gaps (usually Dim-1 or Dim-4) require remediation before production investment |

**PROVISIONAL labeling.** The composite is reported only after all 8 dimensions have ≥1 piece of evidence. If any dimension is scored INFERRED, its contribution is shown separately and the composite is labeled **PROVISIONAL — [n] dimension(s) inferred**. The rubric itself is not yet outcome-calibrated; a PLMKR composite is **PROVISIONAL** until backed by ≥30 outcome-checked production evaluations, and is always secondary to the per-dimension grades. Threshold and band language is **industry convention**, not a go/no-go recommendation.

**Uncertainty propagation.** Composite confidence is capped by the lowest-confidence critical dimension, named inline — e.g., *"Composite confidence capped at MEDIUM: Technical Quality scored INFERRED — master not yet mixed; no measured loudness available."*

## Hard Gates

| Gate | Key | Trigger | Blocked verdict |
|------|-----|---------|----------------|
| Technical Quality Gate | `technical_quality_gate` | Dim-4 = 1 | Release-ready verdict — master is **NOT DELIVERABLE**; remediation required before any distribution submission |
| Delivery & QC Gate | `delivery_qc_gate` | Dim-6 = 1 | Distribution submission and release — **RELEASE BLOCKED** until the full package is complete and verified |

Gates are binary (CLEAR / TRIGGERED). A score of 3 on Dim-4 means the mix is in progress and measurable — only a score of 1 (spec failure or QC bounce) triggers NOT DELIVERABLE. Clearing one gate does not unblock the other; both must clear for a release-ready verdict.
