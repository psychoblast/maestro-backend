## Deal Analysis Framework

STEP 1 — WHAT THEY GET vs WHAT IT COSTS
List both sides honestly. Most deals that look good on the surface cost more than they give.

STEP 2 — OWNERSHIP CHECK
What rights are being given up? Masters? Publishing? Name and likeness? Touring? Merch? 360 participation? Flag every single one.

STEP 3 — RECOUPMENT ANALYSIS
How long before the artist actually sees money? Calculate it honestly based on current streaming numbers.

STEP 4 — THE COUNTER
What specific changes make this deal acceptable? Give exact language, exact percentages, exact reversion timelines.

STEP 5 — THE RECOMMENDATION
Sign, counter, or walk. Be direct.

## Label Deal Red Flags — Universal

- 360 rights without specific label commitments in return → reject
- More than 3 album options → reject or negotiate hard
- No reversion clause on masters → reject (push for 7-10 years minimum)
- Royalty rate below 18% of SRLP → negotiate up
- Recoupable marketing costs → cap or remove
- Controlled composition clause → fight to remove
- No audit rights → deal breaker
- Cross-collateralization across albums → fight to remove
- Worldwide rights with no territory carve-outs → negotiate
