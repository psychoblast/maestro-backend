# Royalty Recovery Readiness Rubric

The scoring instrument for a Royalty Recovery Audit. It measures one thing: how
completely is this catalog collecting the money it has already earned, and how
ready is it to recover what it is not? It scores the **recovery state** — not the
catalog's value, and not the merits of any deal.

All thresholds and percentages below are stated as **industry convention** —
working reference points, not owner-confirmed mandates and not guarantees.

---

## Preamble: Provisional Composite Rule

The composite output is **PROVISIONAL** until an outcome corpus of ≥30
outcome-checked recovery audits exists. Until that threshold is met:

1. Score each dimension A+–F with per-dimension justification citing evaluable
   sub-signals.
2. Compute the provisional composite (formula below).
3. Report it labeled **PROVISIONAL COMPOSITE** with the unlock condition:
   *"≥30 outcome-checked recovery audits."*
4. The composite is secondary to the per-dimension grades.
5. Do not present the provisional composite as calibrated across catalogs.

---

## Hard Gates (evaluate before computing the composite)

Hard gates are pass/fail conditions that change the audit posture before any
dimension is scored.

**HG-1 (No Statement or Registration Data — NOT EVALUABLE):**
If the minimum data set for the requested recovery analysis is absent (no statement
data for anomaly detection; no works list or registration export for a registration-
gap audit; a recoverable-amount request with no statement history and no dated
registration-gap range), no recovery figure is produced. State NOT EVALUABLE, name
the minimum data required, and stop. HG-1 supersedes all dimension scoring. There is
no confidence-capped or "illustrative" variant of HG-1.

**HG-2 (Fabricated Recovery Figure — INVALID):**
If the analysis states a recoverable dollar amount, royalty rate, mechanical rate,
or society-retention figure with no documented (Tier A/B) source and not provided by
the user as their own data, the figure is fabricated and the output is INVALID.
Replace with qualitative recovery context + the data that would enable a valid
estimate.

**HG-3 (Lag Misdiagnosed as Underpayment — INVALID):**
If the analysis concludes income is "missing" or "underpaid" without first mapping
the expected collection pipeline and ruling out normal lag for that stream, the
conclusion is INVALID. Income within the normal collection window is in transit.
Re-run the Pipeline-First Diagnosis before asserting a loss.

**HG-4 (Expired Audit Window — TIME-CRITICAL FLAG):**
If the contractual audit window for the period in question has expired, recovery via
formal audit for that period is foreclosed and must be flagged as TIME-CRITICAL /
FORECLOSED. This does not stop the audit; it changes which recovery paths remain
open and routes any salvage strategy to counsel.

## Seven-Dimension Royalty Recovery Readiness Model

| # | Dimension | Weight | What It Measures |
|---|-----------|--------|------------------|
| 1 | **Registration Integrity** | **0.20** | Is every work registered everywhere it earns (PRO, mechanical, neighboring rights, identifiers)? |
| 2 | **Statement Verification** | **0.18** | Are statements independently verified against activity data; is anomaly detection practiced? |
| 3 | **Black-Box Recovery Readiness** | **0.16** | Are unmatched-pool and historical-claim recovery mechanisms worked? |
| 4 | **Pipeline Coverage** | **0.14** | Are all applicable income streams identified and actually flowing? |
| 5 | **Audit Readiness** | **0.12** | Is the audit window tracked and is the recovery evidence base retained? |
| 6 | **Collection-Timing Discipline** | **0.10** | Is lag correctly distinguished from loss across streams? |
| 7 | **Recovery Documentation** | **0.10** | Is the evidentiary chain (ownership, splits, identifiers) sufficient to file claims? |
| | **Total** | **1.00** | |

---

### D1: Registration Integrity — Weight 0.20

**Definition:** Is every work registered with every collector that owes it money?
A registration gap is the single most common reason earned money never arrives —
it cannot be matched to its owner.

| Sub-signal | Classification | Anchor |
|-----------|----------------|--------|
| PRO registration complete across the home territory | SOURCED | All works registered: full signal; partial: caps the grade |
| Mechanical registration complete (works + correct publisher) | SOURCED | Complete: full; gaps on the tail: partial |
| Neighboring-rights registration where applicable | SOURCED | Registered: full; not registered with applicable collectors: gap |
| Identifier coverage (ISWC / ISRC / IPI) on revenue-earning works | MEASURED/SOURCED | Active-revenue works without identifiers: HG-1 candidate |

**Anchors:** A+/A — registration closed across PRO, mechanical, neighboring rights,
and identifiers on all earning works. B — minor tail gaps with a remediation path.
C — one collector class unregistered. D — multiple classes unregistered on active
revenue. F — active revenue with no identifiers (HG-1 should have triggered).

### D2: Statement Verification — Weight 0.18

**Definition:** Is each statement treated as a claim to verify, with anomaly
detection run against independent activity data, before it is accepted as income?

| Sub-signal | Classification | Anchor |
|-----------|----------------|--------|
| Statements verified against DSP/society/independent data | JUDGED | Routine cross-reference: full; accept-as-sent: weak |
| Anomaly checklist applied before acceptance | JUDGED | Twelve-category review practiced: full |
| Reserve and deduction math reviewed | JUDGED | Reserve/deduction lines reconciled: full |
| Anomalies distinguished from normal lag | JUDGED | Lag ruled out before flagging loss: required for credit |

**Anchors:** A+/A — every statement verified against independent data with the
anomaly checklist and reserve/deduction review; lag ruled out before any loss
claim. B — verification practiced but not systematic. C — spot checks only. D —
statements accepted with little review. F — no verification; statements banked as
received.

### D3: Black-Box Recovery Readiness — Weight 0.16

**Definition:** Is the catalog actively recovering unmatched-pool and historical
money — or is it leaving it to age out and be redistributed?

| Sub-signal | Classification | Anchor |
|-----------|----------------|--------|
| Unmatched-pool / mechanical-backlog claims filed | SOURCED | Filed and tracked: full; unworked: gap |
| Active black-box claim program with holding collectors | SOURCED | In place: full; none: gap |
| Historical / retroactive recovery windows tracked | JUDGED | Tracked against deadlines: full |
| Proof-of-ownership package ready for claims | JUDGED | Ready: full; missing: caps recoverability |

**Anchors:** A+/A — unmatched-pool claims filed, a black-box program is running, and
retroactive windows are tracked with an ownership package ready. C — mechanisms
identified but unworked (quantifiable value currently uncaptured). F — no recovery
mechanism and recoverable money aging toward redistribution.

### D4: Pipeline Coverage — Weight 0.14

**Definition:** Are all applicable income streams identified and actually flowing?
A single play generates master, performance, and mechanical income — plus
neighboring rights and sync backend where applicable. Coverage that checks one
stream understates leakage.

| Sub-signal | Classification | Anchor |
|-----------|----------------|--------|
| All applicable streams identified | JUDGED | Master, performance, mechanical, neighboring, sync backend each in-scope or excluded with reason |
| In-scope streams confirmed flowing | SOURCED | Each modeled stream has a confirmed income path |
| Out-of-scope streams excluded with a stated reason | JUDGED | Clear, reasoned exclusion (e.g., no masters → no neighboring rights) |

**Anchors:** A+/A — all applicable streams identified, in-scope confirmed flowing,
out-of-scope excluded with reason. C — primary streams flow; secondary streams
unaddressed. F — a material stream absent without acknowledgement.

### D5: Audit Readiness — Weight 0.12

**Definition:** Is the contractual audit window tracked, and is the evidence base
retained so a recovery case can actually be built within the window?

| Sub-signal | Classification | Anchor |
|-----------|----------------|--------|
| Audit window status known (within / approaching / expired) | SOURCED | Tracked: full; unknown: weak; expired: HG-4 flag |
| Statement history retained for the recoverable period | JUDGED | Full history retained: full |
| Soft-audit / written-inquiry practice in place | JUDGED | Documented inquiries used before formal audit: full |

**Anchors:** A+/A — window tracked and not expired, full statement history retained,
soft-audit practice in place. D — window unknown and history incomplete. F — window
expired on the period in question (HG-4) with no salvage path identified.

### D6: Collection-Timing Discipline — Weight 0.10

**Definition:** Across every stream, is income in normal transit correctly
distinguished from income that is genuinely stuck or underpaid? Misdiagnosis here
produces false recovery claims (HG-3) or missed real losses.

| Sub-signal | Classification | Anchor |
|-----------|----------------|--------|
| Expected lag mapped per stream | JUDGED | Stream-specific lag applied: full |
| Usage date vs. receipt date compared | JUDGED | Compared before any loss claim: required |
| In-transit vs. stuck vs. underpaid classification applied | JUDGED | Each questioned stream classified: full |

**Anchors:** A+/A — stream-specific lag mapped and every questioned stream
classified before any loss claim. D — lag mentioned but not applied; income treated
as missing on the earning date. F — lag ignored; underpayment asserted on in-transit
income (HG-3).

### D7: Recovery Documentation — Weight 0.10

**Definition:** Is the evidentiary chain sufficient to actually file a claim or hand
a recovery case to counsel? A claim with no documentation is a complaint.

| Sub-signal | Classification | Anchor |
|-----------|----------------|--------|
| Proof of ownership / chain of title available | SOURCED | Documented: full; missing: caps every claim |
| Split sheets executed for co-written works | SOURCED | Executed: full; absent: ownership ambiguity |
| Identifiers and registration records assembled | SOURCED | Assembled: full |
| Line-item evidence for contested statement items | JUDGED | Specific items cited with basis: full |

**Anchors:** A+/A — ownership documented, splits executed, records assembled, and
contested items cited line-by-line. C — partial documentation; some claims
unsupportable. F — no evidentiary chain; nothing filable.

## Composite Score Calculation

```
Composite = (D1×0.20)+(D2×0.18)+(D3×0.16)+(D4×0.14)+(D5×0.12)+(D6×0.10)+(D7×0.10)

Grade-to-numeric:
A+ = 10.0 | A = 9.0 | A- = 8.5 | B+ = 8.0 | B = 7.0 | B- = 6.5
C+ = 6.0  | C = 5.0 | C- = 4.5 | D+ = 4.0 | D = 3.0 | D- = 2.5 | F = 1.0
```

**Composite interpretation (industry-convention bands — descriptive, not a
recommendation):**

| Range | Interpretation |
|-------|----------------|
| 8.5–10.0 | Recovery state is strong — collecting comprehensively; refinements only |
| 7.0–8.4 | Adequate — collecting most income; named gaps with clear remediation |
| 5.0–6.9 | Leaking — material uncollected income; recovery actions warranted |
| <5.0 | Severely leaking — fundamental registration/verification gaps; prioritize the recovery plan |

Output every composite labeled **PROVISIONAL COMPOSITE** until the unlock condition
is met.

## Recovery Posture Classification (descriptive severity)

A label for how much the catalog is leaking — **descriptive severity, never a
recommendation to sign, sell, or litigate:**

`FULLY_COLLECTING` · `MINOR_LEAKAGE` · `NOTABLE_LEAKAGE` · `SIGNIFICANT_LEAKAGE` ·
`SEVERE_LEAKAGE`

---

## Recovery Plan Tiers (the Action Profile)

Every audit produces a four-tier plan. Recoverable amounts are labeled ESTIMATE
(with basis) only where data supports them, else NOT ESTIMABLE:

- **IMMEDIATE** — time-critical actions (an approaching audit-window expiry; an
  unmatched-pool claim aging toward redistribution).
- **PRIORITY** — the highest-value recovery actions (file the unmatched claims;
  close the registration gaps that unlock retroactive money).
- **OPTIMIZE** — improvements that reduce future leakage (systematize statement
  verification; add territory/neighboring-rights coverage).
- **MAINTAIN** — dimensions already strong; keep them closed.

---

## Anti-Fake-Precision Mechanics

- No recoverable dollar amount without statement history + a dated gap/anomaly range
  → otherwise NOT ESTIMABLE.
- No rate, retention, or multiple stated as "market standard" without a Tier A/B
  source.
- Forward-looking recovery assumptions are at least Tier C, even when anchored on
  measured history.
- The composite is PROVISIONAL and not comparable across catalogs.

## Required Output Elements

A complete Royalty Recovery Audit states: the seven dimension grades with
classifications and confidence; all four hard gates (CLEAR/TRIGGERED with reason);
the PROVISIONAL composite with formula and unlock condition; the Recovery Posture
classification; the Leak Map (where money is most likely going missing, with
evidence); the four-tier Recovery Plan; NOT EVALUABLE items with the data required;
and the advisory footer routing execution out.
