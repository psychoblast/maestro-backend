## The Sync Opportunity

A single sync placement can generate more revenue than years of streaming:
- National TV commercial: $25,000-500,000+
- Major film (end title): $50,000-250,000+
- TV show (scene use): $2,000-25,000 per episode
- Indie film: $500-5,000
- YouTube/web content: $100-2,000
- Video game: $5,000-100,000+
- Trailer: $10,000-150,000+

Every sync requires TWO licenses:
1. SYNC LICENSE: Permission to use the composition (from publisher/songwriter)
2. MASTER LICENSE: Permission to use the specific recording (from master owner)

Independent artists who own both masters and publishing can negotiate both sides — major advantage over signed artists.

## Sync-Ready Music Checklist

For a song to be sync-ready:
- Clean version available (no explicit content for most TV/advertising)
- Stems available (separated vocal, instrumental, drums, bass)
- Metadata complete: ISRC, ISWC, songwriter splits, publisher info
- Both master and sync rights cleared (no uncleared samples)
- High quality WAV file (24-bit, 48kHz minimum)
- Both artist registered with PRO and distributor

## Music Supervisor Pitching

Music supervisors are the people who choose music for film, TV, and advertising. They are the gatekeepers.

HOW TO PITCH:
- Research: know their recent projects, know their taste
- Personalize: reference a specific project they worked on
- Be brief: supervisor gets hundreds of pitches, respect their time
- Provide context: where does this song fit in a scene
- Include: streaming link (never attachment), stems available note, both rights cleared confirmation
- Follow up: once, after 2 weeks, then move on

WHERE TO FIND SUPERVISORS:
- IMDb Pro: credits on films and TV shows
- Guild of Music Supervisors: guildofmusicsupervisors.com
- LinkedIn: search "music supervisor"
- Guild events and conferences: SXSW sync sessions, Guild events
