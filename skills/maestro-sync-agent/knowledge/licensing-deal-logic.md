# PLMKR Sync Agent — Licensing Deal Logic

---

## LAYER 1 — GENERAL FRAMEWORKS (domain-neutral licensing logic)

### Deal anatomy: the six dials
Every license is six dials, and value moves only when a dial moves:
1. **Scope** — which rights, for what use
2. **Term** — how long
3. **Territory** — where
4. **Media** — on what channels/formats
5. **Exclusivity** — who else can license the same asset
6. **Price** — what it costs

Rule: never negotiate price before the other five are fixed. A "high" or "low" number is meaningless until the dials it buys are defined; most bad deals are price agreements over undefined scope.

### Negotiation structures
- **Anchoring with stated logic.** First credible number frames the deal — but an anchor without stated comparables invites a counter-anchor war. Anchor + reasoning is harder to dismiss than anchor alone.
- **Options.** Lower upfront + pre-negotiated option fees (renewal, expanded media, expanded territory) trade present certainty for future upside; right when the buyer's success is uncertain and the seller wants the relationship.
- **MFN (most-favored-nations).** Price-parity clauses propagate: one underpriced party reprices everyone tied to it. Granting MFN cheaply is a hidden discount on every future negotiation in the chain.
- **Package vs. itemized.** Bundling hides per-unit price (useful when one item is weak); itemizing exposes it (useful when every item is strong).

### Pricing logic
- Price to the **buyer's use-value and budget tier**, not the seller's effort or attachment.
- Walk-away = value of the alternative pipeline + value of the relationship — never zero, never infinite. A deal below walk-away is a subsidy; name what it buys (relationship, credit, data) or refuse.
- **Quote posture (doctrine):** open at the top of the comparable-supported band — roughly 10–20% (ESTIMATE) above expected close — and never above what a stated comparable justifies; the real cost of over-anchoring in sync is silent disqualification by a time-poor buyer, not a lower counter.
  - Switch to quote-to-close when: (1) the buyer disclosed a budget — quote inside it; treating a disclosed budget as an anchor war insults the buyer; (2) repeat buyers with known budget patterns; (3) speed-is-the-product deals (trailers, rush ads) where a clean instant number closes the slot.
  - Opaque competitive bids (trailer houses comparing many tracks): quote the number you'd be happy with — there is no negotiation round.
  - MFN-bound deals demand precision, not anchoring — the number propagates.

### Leverage sources (audit both sides before any negotiation)
Uniqueness of the asset · deadline pressure asymmetry · simplicity of saying yes (one-stop) · each side's live alternatives · who needs the credit/precedent more.

## LAYER 2 — MUSIC MODULES (sync-specific)

### Fee structure basics
- Sync fees quote **per side**: master use license (recording) and synchronization license (composition). Industry default is MFN between the two sides — a $10k master quote implies a $10k publishing expectation unless explicitly decoupled.
- A party controlling both sides (one-stop) can price the package and close in one signature — that convenience itself carries a premium.

### Fee tiering
> **ALL FIGURES ESTIMATE — NOT QUOTABLE.** Indicative US-market bands from general industry knowledge. No number in this table is ever quoted to a buyer: quotes cite *real* comparables from the deal log or are explicitly framed as exploratory. Real closed-deal comparables replace these bands at the first quarterly pass.

| Use category | Indicative range (per side) | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Indie film, festival-only | $0–2.5k | Often gratis-with-step-up if distributed |
| Indie film, distributed | $2.5k–15k | Step deals common |
| Studio film, background | $15k–35k | |
| Studio film, featured/end-title | $35k–75k+ | |
| TV, background (cable/streamer) | $1.5k–7.5k | Streamer buyouts trend higher |
| TV, featured / main title | $10k–50k+ | Main-title themes negotiate as their own class |
| Trailer | $25k–150k+ | Premium class; short term, huge reach |
| National ad campaign | $50k–300k+ | Term/media/territory dials dominate |
| Regional/online-only ad | $5k–50k | |
| Video games | $2.5k–25k | Usually buyout, in-perpetuity |

**Within-band movers** (what moves a deal inside its band):
- **Up:** prominence of use (featured/lyric-forward vs. background) · broad media bundle (all-media, in-perpetuity) · exclusivity of any kind (category, term, territory) · artist profile/chart heat · MFN propagation from a higher-priced side
- **Down:** background/incidental use · limited term/media/territory · festival-only or non-commercial scope · package/bulk licensing · named relationship strategic value

**Calibration path:** every real quote and closed fee is logged from day one; bands are re-stated from actual comparables at the first quarterly pass.

### Sync deal terms that matter
- **Term/media standards:** "all media now known or hereafter devised, worldwide, in perpetuity" (buyout) vs. limited term/media with options. Buyout demands a multiple of the limited-term fee.
- **MFN across co-publishers:** any split composition will see each publisher demand MFN with the others — one holdout's number becomes everyone's number.
- **Options to pre-price the future:** renewal options, media-expansion options (festival → all media), sequel/season options. Pre-priced options trade upside for certainty — price them like the buyer will exercise only when it favors them, because they will.
- **Gratis and festival uses (doctrine):** free is strategic **only when it creates a documented future obligation or relationship** — a pre-papered step-up on distribution, a first-look on the buyer's next project, or entry to a target supervisor/music-house relationship. Free that produces only a credit is value-eroding: the credit doesn't negotiate and the $0 does, becoming the comparable for the next deal. Every gratis license is logged with its intended payoff and a check-by date — a gratis deal is a logged, falsifiable prediction, and the policy tightens or loosens on its observed conversion rate.
