# PLMKR Tour & Live — Tour Campaign Quality Rubric

The scoring backbone for any structured assessment of a tour campaign. Weights,
hard-gate thresholds, and grade anchors below are **PLMKR defaults** —
industry-convention values held until an outcome corpus calibrates them.

---

## The Provisional Composite Rule (Do Not Skip)

The composite score is **PROVISIONAL** until an outcome corpus of ≥30
outcome-checked tour-campaign evaluations exists. Until that threshold is met:

1. Score each dimension A+–F with per-dimension justification citing evaluable
   sub-signals.
2. Calculate the provisional composite (formula below).
3. Report it labeled **PROVISIONAL COMPOSITE**.
4. State the unlock condition in every output: *"This composite is PROVISIONAL —
   unlock condition: ≥30 outcome-checked tour-campaign evaluations."*
5. The provisional composite is secondary to per-dimension grades and is locked
   from client-facing use until the unlock condition is met.
6. Do not present it as calibrated or comparable across campaigns.

When unlocked: remove the PROVISIONAL label; recalibrate anchors and weights
against outcome data.

## Eight-Dimension Tour Campaign Quality Model

| # | Dimension | Weight | Campaign Quality Connection |
|---|-----------|--------|------------------------------|
| 1 | **Routing Logic** | **0.20** | Foundation: strategic market sequence and dead-leg efficiency |
| 2 | **Financial Model Integrity** | **0.20** | A sound break-even P&L exists before any routing commitment |
| 3 | **Offer Evaluation Quality** | **0.15** | Offers evaluated against the framework before acceptance |
| 4 | **Production Readiness** | **0.15** | Production scoped, contracted, and advanced on schedule |
| 5 | **Ticketing Strategy** | **0.12** | Price points set; scaling determined; monitoring in place |
| 6 | **International Readiness** | **0.08** | Appropriate to artist stage; permits confirmed if applicable |
| 7 | **Merch Planning** | **0.06** | Merch planned, produced, and shipped in time for tour start |
| 8 | **Settlement Process** | **0.04** | Settlements tracked, verified, and anomalies flagged |
| | **Total** | **1.00** | |

---

## Hard Gates

Binary pass/fail checks that a strong composite cannot override. Any FAIL = flag
regardless of dimensional grades.

**HG-1: Break-Even Model Before Commitment**
- PASS: a documented break-even model (revenue vs. expenses with sensitivity at
  70%, 85%, 100% capacity) existed before any routing was confirmed.
- FAIL: any date was booked or routing announced before a break-even model was built.
- Consequence: campaign flagged HIGH FINANCIAL RISK regardless of other grades.

**HG-2: Anchor Dates Confirmed Before Routing Fill**
- PASS: the top 3–5 anchor markets are confirmed offers before secondary dates were added.
- FAIL: routing built geography-outward, or secondary dates announced before anchors confirmed.
- Consequence: Dimension 1 (Routing Logic) capped at C+.

**HG-3: Work-Permit Pathway Confirmed for International Leg**
- PASS: for any international leg, the applicable work-permit/visa category is
  confirmed with qualified immigration counsel before dates are announced.
- FAIL: international dates announced without a confirmed permit pathway.
- Consequence: Dimension 6 (International Readiness) = F; campaign flagged HIGH RISK.
- NOT APPLICABLE: if the campaign has no international dates.

**HG-4: Production Advance Completed Before Show Day**
- PASS: production advance (rider delivery + technical advance confirmation)
  completed ≥2 weeks before every show.
- FAIL: any show entered production advance less than 1 week out, or had no advance.
- Consequence: Dimension 4 (Production Readiness) capped at D.

## Dimension Grade Anchors

### Dimension 1 — Routing Logic (0.20)
Evaluates strategic market sequence, anchor-first protocol, dead-leg efficiency,
routing efficiency ratio, and alignment with the release-campaign geography.

| Grade | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| A+ | Anchor-first confirmed; efficiency ratio ≥0.70; dead legs eliminated or justified; sequence aligned with streaming/release-priority markets |
| A | Anchor-first; ratio ≥0.65; minor justified dead legs |
| B | Mostly anchor-first; some geography-driven fill; ratio 0.55–0.65; dead legs present but not excessive |
| C | Mixed anchor/geography; significant dead legs; ratio 0.45–0.55; sequence not clearly strategic |
| D | Geography-first; no anchor protocol; dead legs unanalyzed |
| F | No routing logic documented; dates booked without analysis |

NOT EVALUABLE: fewer than 3 confirmed dates; no market-draw data to assess sequence.

### Dimension 2 — Financial Model Integrity (0.20)
Evaluates whether a break-even model exists before commitment, whether the P&L
carries all revenue/expense lines, and whether sensitivity scenarios are modeled.

| Grade | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| A+ | Complete P&L (all lines); sensitivity at 70/85/100%; break-even calculated; modeled before any routing lock |
| A | Complete P&L; break-even calculated; modeled before routing; one sensitivity scenario incomplete |
| B | P&L exists; break-even calculated; modeled before commitment; some expenses estimated without basis |
| C | P&L modeled after initial commitments, or break-even not calculated, or major expense lines missing |
| D | Informal P&L only; no documented break-even |
| F | No P&L before routing commitment |

### Dimension 3 — Offer Evaluation Quality (0.15)
Evaluates whether offers were tested against the framework before acceptance:
split-point reachability, marketing commitments, red-flag clauses.

| Grade | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| A+ | All offers evaluated against the full checklist; split-points verified; marketing commitments documented; red-flags resolved |
| A | All offers evaluated; minor checklist gaps; no red-flag accepted undocumented |
| B | Most offers evaluated; some below-threshold accepted without rationale; partial split-point analysis |
| C | Informal evaluation; no consistent checklist; some red-flags accepted unflagged |
| D | Evaluation absent; decisions on guarantee amount alone |
| F | No evaluation; below-break-even offers accepted without rationale |

### Dimension 4 — Production Readiness (0.15)
Evaluates rider completeness and deal-level fit, advance completion, current
stage plot, and per-venue provision tiers.

| Grade | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| A+ | Complete rider fit to deal level; advance ≥4 weeks before every show; stage plot current; all discrepancies resolved before show week |
| A | Rider complete; advance ≥2 weeks; minor discrepancies resolved before show day |
| B | Rider complete; some show-day discrepancies; 1–2 shows without full advance |
| C | Rider sent late (<2 weeks for some); advance calls not completed |
| D | No advance for most shows; discrepancies on show day; load-in delays |
| F | No advance process; no complete rider |

### Dimension 5 — Ticketing Strategy (0.12)
Evaluates market-based price points, venue scaling, tier/VIP structure, and
secondary-market monitoring.

| Grade | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| A+ | Market-by-market price analysis; GA+mid+VIP tiers; scaling documented; secondary signals monitored and used |
| A | Market analysis done; tier structure in place; minor monitoring gaps |
| B | Uniform pricing without market-specific analysis; partial tier structure |
| C | Prices set by intuition without data; no VIP tier; scaling undocumented |
| D | Price set by venue/promoter without artist input; no tiers; no monitoring |
| F | No ticketing strategy; pricing delegated entirely without review |

### Dimension 6 — International Readiness (0.08)
Evaluated only when international dates are in scope: the four trigger
prerequisites, confirmed work permits, withholding modeled in the P&L.

| Grade | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| A+ | All four prerequisites passed; permits applied ≥8 weeks out; withholding modeled |
| A | All prerequisites passed; permits confirmed; one minor planning gap |
| B | Most prerequisites met; withholding not fully modeled or one prerequisite borderline |
| C | Booked on incomplete prerequisite check; permit applied late (<6 weeks); withholding unmodeled |
| D | Permits unconfirmed before announcement; P&L excludes withholding; demand signal absent |
| F | Booked without any prerequisite check; permits not applied for |
| NOT APPLICABLE | No international dates in scope |

### Dimension 7 — Merch Planning (0.06)
Evaluates whether merch is planned, produced, and shipped in time; the operating
structure fit; and whether hall fees are negotiated or at least known.

| Grade | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| A+ | Merch produced and at first venue before tour start; per-market inventory planned; hall fees documented for all venues; online/tour balance set |
| A | Merch ready at tour start; hall fees documented; minor inventory gaps |
| B | Merch ready but cut close; hall fees not fully negotiated; no per-market plan |
| C | Merch late (missed first shows); hall fees unchecked before signing |
| D | Merch not planned until after tour start; no inventory management |
| F | No merch operation |

### Dimension 8 — Settlement Process (0.04)
Evaluates line-by-line settlement review, anomaly disputes, and tour-wide tracking.

| Grade | Anchor |
|-------|--------|
| A+ | Every settlement reviewed against the deal memo before signing; anomalies disputed in real time; tour-wide tracking maintained |
| A | Settlements reviewed; minor misses corrected on later review |
| B | Most shows reviewed; tracking maintained but some shows missing |
| C | Signed without full expense review on multiple shows; no tracking |
| D | Typically signed at end of night without review; no dispute process |
| F | No review process; promoter figures accepted unverified |

## Provisional Composite Formula

```
Composite = Σ (Dimension Grade Numeric × Dimension Weight)

Letter → Numeric:
A+ = 4.3  A = 4.0  A- = 3.7
B+ = 3.3  B = 3.0  B- = 2.7
C+ = 2.3  C = 2.0  C- = 1.7
D+ = 1.3  D = 1.0  D- = 0.7
F  = 0.0

Range: 0.0–4.3
Composite bands (default; descriptive only — never a go/no-go instruction):
≥3.7  Strong campaign — high likelihood of financial and strategic success
3.0–3.6  Adequate campaign — gaps that reduce efficiency or upside
2.0–2.9  Weak campaign — material risks; corrective action required
< 2.0  Critical risk — immediate intervention; hard gates likely failing
```

Report with the PROVISIONAL label and the unlock condition.

---

## Risk Classification (Descriptive Severity, Not a Recommendation)

Classify campaign severity into one of five descriptive tiers. This labels
severity — it is not a recommendation to proceed, hold, or cancel.

- **LOW_RISK** — composite ≥3.7, all hard gates clear.
- **NOTABLE_GAPS** — composite 3.0–3.6, all hard gates clear; addressable gaps.
- **SIGNIFICANT_GAPS** — composite 2.0–2.9, or one hard gate failing.
- **HIGH_FINANCIAL_RISK** — HG-1 failing (no break-even model before commitment),
  or composite < 2.0.
- **CRITICAL_RISK** — multiple hard gates failing; immediate intervention.

---

## Anti-Fake-Precision Mechanics

Each sub-signal is MEASURED, SOURCED, or JUDGED. Grade only where evidence
exists; otherwise mark NOT EVALUABLE and name it. Dimension confidence is capped
by the weakest critical sub-signal. Confidence is expressed in qualitative bands
(HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW with reasons); numeric probability percentages are not used
pre-unlock.

---

## Action Profile

Translate dimensional grades into operational priorities:

- **IMMEDIATE** (before any date is confirmed or announced): any hard-gate FAIL;
  Dimension 2 (Financial Model) < C; HG-1 or HG-2 fail conditions.
- **PRIORITY** (within 2 weeks): Dimension 1 (Routing) < B; Dimension 3 (Offer
  Evaluation) < B; Dimension 6 < B when international dates are in scope.
- **OPTIMIZE** (before the tour departs): Dimension 4 (Production) < A;
  Dimension 5 (Ticketing) < A; Dimension 7 (Merch) < B.
- **MAINTAIN** (ongoing during tour): Dimension 8 (Settlement) monitoring;
  secondary-market signal monitoring; production-advance completion.
