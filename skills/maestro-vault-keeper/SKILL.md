## Your Mission

## Global Business Structure Options

### CANADA
- Sole proprietor: simplest, personal liability, personal tax rates
- Corporation: ~15% federal corporate tax vs up to 53% personal — significant saving at $50K+
- Register: Ontario Business Registry, Corporations Canada
- HST/GST: register when income exceeds $30,000/year

### USA
- Sole proprietor: simplest, Schedule C filing
- LLC: liability protection, pass-through taxation, state-specific
- S-Corporation: tax advantages at higher income levels
- C-Corporation: for investment and complex structures
- Best state for music LLC: California (entertainment hub), Tennessee (Nashville), New York, Florida (no state income tax)

### UK
- Sole trader: simplest, self-assessment filing
- Limited company: lower corporate tax rate (19-25%), professional credibility
- LLP: good for partnerships
- Register: Companies House (companieshouse.gov.uk)

### AUSTRALIA
- Sole trader: simplest, ABN required
- Pty Ltd: limited liability, corporate tax rate 25% (base rate)
- Register: ASIC (asic.gov.au)

### NIGERIA
- Business name registration: simplest
- Limited liability company: CAC registration
- Register: Corporate Affairs Commission (cac.gov.ng)

### SOUTH AFRICA
- Sole proprietor
- Private company (Pty Ltd): CIPC registration
- Register: CIPC (cipc.co.za)

### JAMAICA
- Sole trader
- Limited liability company: Companies Office of Jamaica
- Register: ORC (orcjamaica.com)

### BRAZIL
- MEI (Microempreendedor Individual): simplest, lowest tax
- ME (Microempresa): next level
- LTDA: limited liability company
- Register: Receita Federal

### SOUTH KOREA
- Sole proprietor
- Corporation (Jusik Hoesa)
- Entertainment company structure common for K-Pop

## When To Incorporate — Universal Signals

Regardless of country, consider incorporation when:
- Earning $40,000-50,000+ annually from music
- Significant equipment purchases planned
- International touring beginning
- Signing major deals
- Want liability protection from contracts
- Hiring team members

## Income Stream Tracking — Universal

Every artist worldwide must track:
INCOME: Streaming royalties (master), performance royalties (PRO), mechanical royalties, neighbouring rights, live performance fees, sync fees, merchandise, brand deals, grants, teaching, YouTube/social monetization

EXPENSES: Recording, equipment, software, distribution, marketing, travel, professional fees, merchandise production, insurance, education, instrument maintenance, PRO memberships

## Royalty Statement Review

Universal red flags in any royalty statement:
- Unexplained deductions
- Territories missing from report
- Royalty rate lower than contract specifies
- Recoupment calculation errors
- Cross-collateralization applied without contractual basis
- Missing platform reporting

## Tour Budget Framework — Universal

INCOME: Guarantees + merchandise + grant support + sponsorship
EXPENSES: Transportation + accommodation + per diem + crew + equipment + marketing + insurance + contingency (10%)
RULE: Income must exceed expenses by minimum 20% or tour needs restructuring

## The Passman Principle

"Audit rights exist in contracts because labels frequently underpay. Audits consistently find underpayments. Never sign a deal without audit rights."
