## Your Mission

Director X (Drake, Rihanna, Kardinal Offishall): "Visual identity is the artist's second language. If the video doesn't feel like the music, the audience feels the disconnect."

A great music video extends the life of a song, creates new discovery opportunities, and deepens the artist's visual identity. A bad music video can undermine a great song.

## Music Video Types by Budget

LYRIC VIDEO ($0-500):
- Can be created with AI tools (Runway ML) and basic editing
- Animated text over generated or licensed visuals
- Still drives YouTube streams and engagement
- Best for: Gold tier, non-priority releases, quick content

PERFORMANCE VIDEO ($500-2,000):
- Artist performing in one or two locations
- Simple but authentic
- Can look excellent with good cinematography and lighting
- Best for: Gold and Platinum tier, streaming-focused releases

CONCEPT VIDEO ($2,000-10,000):
- Narrative or artistic concept
- Multiple locations or sets
- Wardrobe and styling
- Professional crew
- Best for: Platinum tier, priority singles

MINI FILM ($10,000-50,000):
- Full narrative story
- Professional cast and crew
- Multiple days of shooting
- Full post-production
- Best for: Diamond tier, major releases

MAJOR PRODUCTION ($50,000+):
- Hollywood-level production
- Major director
- Multiple locations including international
- Full post-production suite
- Best for: established artists, major label campaigns

## Budget Breakdown

For a $5,000 concept video:
- Director fee: $1,000-1,500
- Director of Photography (DP): $500-800/day
- Camera equipment rental: $300-500/day
- Lighting equipment: $200-400/day
- Location fees: $0-500
- Wardrobe and styling: $200-500
- Hair and makeup: $200-400
- Production assistant: $150-250/day
- Editing: $500-1,000
- Color grading: $300-600
- Contingency: 10%

## Finding the Right Director

BY CAREER STAGE:
- GOLD: Film school students, emerging directors building portfolio
- PLATINUM: Directors with 2-5 music video credits in the genre
- DIAMOND: Established directors with major credits

WHERE TO FIND:
- Vimeo: search music videos in your genre
- Instagram: search [genre] + music video director
- YouTube: find videos you love, find the director in credits
- Director's Guild databases
- Referrals from other artists in your network

HOW TO EVALUATE:
- Review their full portfolio — not just their best work
- Does their aesthetic match your vision
- Can they work within your budget
- Do other artists speak well of them
- Do they understand your genre culturally

## The Treatment

Before any video goes into production, get a written treatment from the director:
- Concept overview: what is the video about
- Visual references: images that inspire the look
- Location ideas
- Wardrobe direction
- Shot list overview
- Post-production approach

Do not approve budget until you have approved the treatment.

## FACTOR Music Video Grant

Canadian artists can apply to FACTOR for music video funding:
- Up to $20,000 for eligible artists
- Must apply before production begins
- Requires SOCAN membership and Canadian distributor
- Strong application needs: treatment, budget, director CV, artist career evidence

## Video Distribution Strategy

YOUTUBE: Primary platform — upload highest quality file, optimize title, description, tags for SEO
VEVO: Apply for Vevo channel — adds credibility, separate analytics
APPLE MUSIC: Submit video through distributor for Apple Music video content
TIDAL: Submit for Tidal video content
SOCIAL MEDIA: Cut 15, 30, and 60 second clips for Instagram, TikTok, Twitter

PREMIERE STRATEGY:
- Music blog premiere: pitch video to a relevant outlet for exclusive premiere
- YouTube premiere: use YouTube's premiere feature to create watch event
- Instagram and TikTok snippet 48 hours before YouTube release

## Behind the Scenes Content

Every video shoot should generate:
- BTS footage for social media (30-60 second clips)
- Production photos
- Time-lapse of setup
- Candid moments with crew and cast
- Director interview about the concept

BTS content often outperforms the actual video on social media.
